## Detailed Checks

### 1. Doctrinal Soundness
All content is grounded in historic evangelical Christianity. The centrality of the cross and resurrection is maintained. No compromise on the exclusivity of Christ. God's attributes are presented consistently — His love does not override His justice. No moralism; transformation is attributed to God's work through His Word and Spirit.

### 2. Cross & Resurrection Non-Negotiable
The cross and resurrection are referenced in Core Teaching of Lesson 5, Lesson 7, and in flashcard Q10. They are presented as the foundation of salvation.

### 3. No Named Teachers
All 3 output files are free of any teacher, preacher, or author names. Source material theology was synthesized without attribution.

### 4. Hook Integrity
All 7 lessons begin with a real-world scenario or question. No Hook starts with a Bible verse.

### 5. Core Teaching Prose-Only
All 7 Core Teaching sections are written in continuous prose. No bullet points, numbered lists, or subheadings appear in any Core Teaching section.

### 6. Practical Applications Specific & Measurable
- Lesson 1: Spend 10 minutes listening to someone's background without preaching.
- Lesson 2: Ask 3 people about the afterlife and categorize them.
- Lesson 3: Read Matthew 7:15–23 and write 3 specific fruits in your own life.
- Lesson 4: Memorize 3 diagnostic questions and practice with one person.
- Lesson 5: Identify one proud or broken person and choose the right approach.
- Lesson 6: Spend 20 minutes listening without preaching.
- Lesson 7: Identify a real-time signal and adjust your approach.

All applications are concrete, measurable, and doable by a new believer.

### 7. Universal Applicability
All illustrations use universal human experiences: conversation, listening, relationships, trust, sickness, joy, grief. No culture-specific references.

### 8. No Moralism
All content points to the work of God through Christ, the Spirit, and Scripture as the source of change. No "try harder" messaging.

### 9. Module Boundaries Respected
- COVERS: Listening skills ✓, discernment ✓, understanding background/beliefs ✓, asking diagnostic questions ✓, tailoring approach ✓, building rapport ✓, true vs false conversion ✓, reading your audience ✓, different types of people ✓
- DOES NOT COVER: Gospel content (Module 01) — not taught ✓; Starting the conversation (Module 04) — not taught ✓
