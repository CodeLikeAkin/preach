## 1. Washer_KLG_Part1.txt — Knowing the Living God (ESV Edition 1A)

### Chapter 3: God Is One and Three

**God Is One (Monotheism)**
- Deuteronomy 6:4 is one of the most important declarations in Scripture — the LORD our God is one LORD
- The Hebrew word for "one" is *echad*, which often refers to a unity of more than one person (e.g., Genesis 2:24 — "they shall become one flesh")
- Monotheism is the foundation of Old and New Testament faith
- Believing in one God must be firmly rooted in heart and mind — it is the foundation stone of faith
- Isaiah 43:10 — "You are My witnesses... that you may know and believe Me and understand that I am He"
- Three key words: Know (personal relationship), Believe (trust/faith), Understand (implications for how we live)
- Exodus 20:2-6 — no other gods before Me; the jealousy of God is not like human jealousy but His rightful claim on His creation
- Mark 12:28-30 — love the Lord your God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength

**God Is a Trinity**
- The word "trinity" comes from Latin *trinitas* meaning "threefold" or "three in one"
- First employed by Tertullian to describe what the Bible teaches about God
- The one true God exists as a Trinity: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit
- Three distinct Persons who share the same divine nature or essence
- They relate to one another in unbroken fellowship
- Matthew 28:19 — baptize in the **name** (singular) of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit
- II Corinthians 13:14 — Trinitarian blessing showing absolute equality
- Mark 1:10-11 — at Jesus' baptism, the Son is baptized, the Spirit descends, the Father speaks — all three distinct at the same moment
- John 14:16-17 — the Son prays to the Father, the Father sends the Spirit, the Spirit dwells in believers
- Not three independent Beings (tritheism), not one Person wearing masks (modalism)

**Summary Truths (7 points):**
1. God is One — not three different Gods (tritheism is heresy)
2. God is Three — one God exists as three Persons
3. Both truths must be held equally — heresy occurs when we affirm one and deny the other
4. The three Persons are real and distinct — not modalism
5. The three Persons are perfectly equal — Son not less than Father, Spirit not less than Son
6. They manifest differently and carry out different functions — Father unseen, Son incarnate, Spirit indwelling
7. Mystery is not grounds for denial — if we only believed what we could understand, there would be little left
8. Most illustrations (water/ice/steam, etc.) are inadequate and can be misleading or heretical

**Implications of the Trinity (5):**
1. God is relational — the Persons have existed in perfect love eternally; believers are invited into this fellowship
2. God has no need — He did not create out of loneliness but out of superabundance and for His glory
3. God is love — love is eternal within the Godhead, not something that began with creation
4. Salvation is the work of God — Father designs, Son accomplishes, Spirit indwells and seals
5. Model for human relationships — equality with different roles; submission is not inferiority

### Chapter 4: The Deity of the Son and the Spirit

- "Deity" from Latin *deitas* — divinity or the state of being God
- A person cannot be a Christian without recognizing the Son and Spirit as God
- The Father's deity is rarely disputed; the deity of the Son and Spirit have been under constant attack

**The Son Is God:**
- John 1:1 — the Word (Logos) was with God and was God
  - In Greek philosophy: an impersonal force
  - In Old Testament: God's communication/self-expression
  - John 1:14 — the Word became flesh
- Colossians 1:16-17 — all things created through the Son
- Key texts: John 1:18, John 20:28 (Thomas: "My Lord and my God"), Philippians 2:6 (form of God, equality with God), Titus 2:13, Romans 9:5, Colossians 2:9 (fullness of Deity dwells in bodily form)

## 7. Spurgeon_8QuotesTrinity.txt

Eight quotes from Spurgeon on the Trinity:

1. "We are baptized into the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost. Not into three names, but one name."
2. "The doctrine of the Trinity is a mystery; but it is a revealed mystery, and therefore we believe it."
3. "I cannot understand the Trinity; but I can believe it, and I can worship the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost, the one God."
4. "The Father is all God, the Son is all God, the Holy Ghost is all God. There is none greater, none less. They are co-equal, co-eternal, and co-existent."
5. "As there is but one God, so there is but one Mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus; and yet this Mediator is himself God over all, blessed forever."
6. "The Holy Spirit is a person, not a mere influence. He can be grieved, he can be vexed, he can be blasphemed against."
7. "The Father sent the Son, the Son came, the Spirit applies the work. All three Persons unite in the salvation of a sinner."
8. "We believe in the Father, we believe in the Son, we believe in the Holy Ghost. This is the faith of the church universal."

## 8. Spurgeon_PersonalityOfHolyGhost.txt

**Full Sermon on John 14:16-17 (January 21, 1855)**

Key arguments for the Holy Spirit as a Person, not a mere influence:

**Personal Designations:**
- Called by personal pronouns — "He" shall teach you
- Term "Comforter" signifies a person
- Spirit is represented as coming, acting, abiding as a person

**Personal Attributes:**
- Has a mind (Romans 8:27)
- Has a will (I Corinthians 12:11)
- Has understanding (I Corinthians 2:10)
- Can be grieved (Ephesians 4:30)
- Can be vexed (Isaiah 63:10)
- Can be blasphemed against (Matthew 12:31)
- Cannot slander an attribute, vex an influence, or grieve an emanation

**Personal Offices:**
- Teacher (John 14:26), Guide (John 16:13), Commander (Acts 13:2), Director (Acts 16:6)
- Creator (Job 26:13), Quickener (John 6:63), Comforter (Acts 9:31)
- Sanctifier (Romans 15:16), Witness (Romans 8:16), Helper in infirmities (Romans 8:26)

**Personal Relations:**
- Relation to Father and Son: Spirit of God, Spirit of Christ, proceeds from Father, sent by Son
- Relation to world: strives with men (Genesis 6:3), convinces of sin (John 16:8-11)
- Relation to church: dwells in it (I Corinthians 3:16), gives gifts (I Corinthians 12:4), ordains ministers (Acts 20:28)
- Relation to believers: dwells in them (I Corinthians 6:19), leads them (Romans 8:14), witnesses (Romans 8:16), seals (Ephesians 1:13)

**Practical Duties:**
- Reverence, love, seek fellowship, obey, do not grieve, honor in baptism, invoke aid in prayer, expect blessing

## 9. Spurgeon_Sermons_Vol7_Trinity.txt

**Sermon on John 17:3 — "The Great Supreme"**

Four points:
1. The knowledge of God is eternal life — cause, sustenance, and perfection of life
2. This knowledge is of the only true God — Father as fountain, Son as revealer, Spirit as applier
3. The blessedness of this knowledge — peace, joy, hope, holiness, immortality
4. This knowledge is obtained only through Jesus Christ — He reveals the Father, sends the Spirit, gives eternal life

"The Father is the fountain of all good. The Son is the channel through which it flows. The Holy Spirit is the power by which it is applied to our souls. And these three are one."

## 10. Spurgeon_ThreefoldSanctification.txt

**Sermon on Jude 1 — "Threefold Sanctification"**

- The source of sanctification is the Father — He chose us unto holiness
- The streams of sanctification come through Jesus Christ — He preserves us
- The application of sanctification is by the Holy Spirit — He calls
- All three Persons of the Trinity cooperate in sanctification
- "The Father purposed it, the Son secured it, and the Spirit applies it"
- Practical inferences: dignity of sanctification, certainty of it, importance of holiness, Trinity of the Godhead
