# Flashcards: Creation
## 10 Q&A Pairs + Memory Verse Drill

---

### Q1 — What is the most fundamental distinction in the Bible?

**A:** The Creator-creature distinction. God alone is uncreated, self-existent, and sovereign. Everything else is created, contingent, and dependent on Him.

**Passages:** Genesis 1:1; Psalm 90:2; Romans 1:25

---

### Q2 — What does it mean that God created ex nihilo?

**A:** It means God created out of nothing. He did not use pre-existing materials. He spoke, and what He commanded came into being. This demonstrates His absolute sovereignty and power.

**Passages:** Psalm 33:6-9; Hebrews 11:3

---

### Q3 — Why is humanity unique in creation?

**A:** Because God made man in His own image (imago Dei). This gives humans rational minds, moral awareness, creativity, relational capacity, and the responsibility of dominion over creation.

**Passages:** Genesis 1:26-27; Psalm 8:3-6; Genesis 9:6

---

### Q4 — What is general revelation?

**A:** What God reveals about Himself through creation and conscience. The heavens, the earth, and the order of the cosmos all bear witness to the Creator's eternal power and divine nature.

**Passages:** Psalm 19:1-4; Romans 1:18-20; Acts 14:15-17

---

### Q5 — What is the purpose and limitation of general revelation?

**A:** It is sufficient to condemn — it leaves every person without excuse before God — but it is not sufficient to save. It reveals God's power and divine nature but not His plan of redemption through Christ.

**Passages:** Romans 1:20; Romans 10:14-17

---

### Q6 — Why did God declare creation "very good"?

**A:** Because the original creation was exactly as God intended — without sin, suffering, death, or decay. Everything was working according to its design, marked by order, beauty, abundance, and harmony.

**Passages:** Genesis 1:31; 1 Timothy 4:4

---

### Q7 — How did the fall affect creation?

**A:** Creation was subjected to futility and now groans under the weight of sin. The ground was cursed, death entered the world, and all of creation eagerly awaits its redemption.

**Passages:** Genesis 3:17-19; Romans 8:19-22

---

### Q8 — What is the protoevangelium?

**A:** The first gospel promise, given in Genesis 3:15, that the seed of the woman will crush the serpent's head. It is the first announcement of redemption and the promise of Christ's victory over Satan.

**Passages:** Genesis 3:15

---

### Q9 — How is the Holy Spirit involved in creation?

**A:** The Spirit hovered over the chaotic waters (Genesis 1:2), bringing order, beauty, and life. The Spirit fills, beautifies, and empowers creation. This same Spirit brings about new creation in believers.

**Passages:** Genesis 1:2; Psalm 104:30; Job 33:4; 2 Corinthians 5:17

---

### Q10 — What is the relationship between creation and new creation?

**A:** The same God who created all things will make all things new. Redemption is not the abandonment of creation but its restoration. Christ, the agent of creation, is also the agent of new creation through His death and resurrection.

**Passages:** Colossians 1:16; 2 Corinthians 5:17; Revelation 21:1-5; 2 Peter 3:13

---

## Memory Verse Drill

**Prompt:** "In the beginning —"
**Response:** " — God created the heavens and the earth." (Genesis 1:1)

**Prompt:** "By the word of the Lord —"
**Response:** " — the heavens were made, and by the breath of His mouth all their host." (Psalm 33:6)

**Prompt:** "The heavens —"
**Response:** " — declare the glory of God, and the sky above proclaims His handiwork." (Psalm 19:1)

**Prompt:** "For since the creation of the world —"
**Response:** " — His invisible attributes, His eternal power and divine nature, have been clearly seen, being understood through what has been made, so that they are without excuse." (Romans 1:20)

**Prompt:** "Therefore if anyone is in Christ —"
**Response:** " — he is a new creature; the old things passed away; behold, new things have come." (2 Corinthians 5:17)

**Prompt:** "All things were created —"
**Response:** " — through Him and for Him." (Colossians 1:16)

**Prompt:** "Behold —"
**Response:** " — I am making all things new." (Revelation 21:5)
