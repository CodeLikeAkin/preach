# Study Guide: Creation

## Module Overview
Module 03 — Creation explores the biblical doctrine of creation: God as the sovereign Creator who made all things from nothing by His word, creation's revelation of God, the goodness of the original creation, mankind as image-bearers, the fall's effect on creation, and the hope of new creation in Christ.

---

## 1. The God Who Creates

### Core Truth
The Bible's opening declaration is a profound statement of who God is and what He has done. "In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth" (Genesis 1:1). This is not a proof of God but an assumption of His existence as the starting point for all reality.

### Creation Ex Nihilo
God did not merely shape pre-existing material. He created out of nothing. "By the word of the Lord the heavens were made, and by the breath of His mouth all their host" (Psalm 33:6). The universe had a beginning, and God alone is eternal.

The triune God was active in creation. The Father purposed, the Son carried out, and the Spirit empowered. "Let Us make man in Our image" (Genesis 1:26) hints at the plurality within the Godhead.

### Key Distinction
- **Creator**: God alone is uncreated, self-existent, sovereign over all.
- **Creation**: Everything else is dependent, contingent, subject to the Creator.

This distinction is foundational. Worship belongs to the Creator alone, not to the creature (Romans 1:25).

---

## 2. Creation Reveals God

### General Revelation
God has not left Himself without witness. "The heavens declare the glory of God, and the sky above proclaims His handiwork" (Psalm 19:1). Creation is a universal revelation available to every human being.

What creation reveals:
- **God's eternal power** — the magnitude, order, and complexity of the universe
- **God's divine nature** — beauty, goodness, wisdom, majesty
- **God's care** — provision for all creatures, regularity of seasons

### Man Without Excuse
Because creation clearly reveals God, all people are accountable. "For since the creation of the world His invisible attributes, His eternal power and divine nature, have been clearly seen, being understood through what has been made, so that they are without excuse" (Romans 1:20).

General revelation is sufficient to condemn but not to save. It leaves man responsible but provides no remedy for sin. Special revelation — Scripture and Christ — is necessary for salvation.

---

## 3. The Goodness of Creation

### God Declares It Good
After each day of creation, God saw that it was good. After the sixth day, "God saw all that He had made, and behold, it was very good" (Genesis 1:31). The original creation was without flaw, sin, or corruption.

Creation was good because:
- It came from a good God
- It reflected His wisdom, order, and purpose
- It was exactly what He intended it to be

### Order and Design
The creation account reveals structure and purpose:
- **Day 1-3**: Forming the realms (light, sky, land/vegetation)
- **Day 4-6**: Filling the realms (lights, birds/fish, animals/man)
- **Day 7**: Rest — completion and delight

This order shows a God of purpose, not randomness. Creation is not chaos but cosmos.

---

## 4. The Image of God in Man

### Uniquely Made
Humanity is the pinnacle of creation — not because of inherent worth separate from God, but because God chose to make man in His own image. "So God created man in His own image, in the image of God He created him; male and female He created them" (Genesis 1:27).

### What the Image Means
- **Relational capacity** — man is made for relationship with God and others
- **Moral responsibility** — man is accountable to God
- **Rationality and creativity** — man reflects God's intelligence and creativity
- **Dominion** — man is given stewardship over creation as God's vice-regent
- **Male and female** — both equally bear the image, distinct yet complementary

### The Fall and the Image
The image was not lost at the fall but was marred and distorted. After the flood, God still affirms that man is made in His image (Genesis 9:6), establishing the basis for human dignity and the prohibition of murder.

---

## 5. The Fall and Its Effects

### Sin Enters
Adam's rebellion against the Creator brought sin into the world. This was not a minor mistake but high treason against the sovereign King. The consequences were catastrophic.

### Effects on Creation
Creation itself was subjected to futility. "For the creation was subjected to futility, not willingly, but because of Him who subjected it, in hope that the creation itself also will be set free from its slavery to corruption into the freedom of the glory of the children of God" (Romans 8:20-21).

The ground is cursed. Thorns and thistles appear. Childbirth is painful. Work becomes toil. Death enters the world. All of creation now groans under the weight of sin.

### God's Purposes Continue
Yet God did not abandon His creation. The protoevangelium — the first gospel — is given in Genesis 3:15: the seed of the woman will crush the serpent's head. Redemption is promised from the very beginning.

---

## 6. The Hope of New Creation

### Christ and Creation
"All things were created through Him and for Him" (Colossians 1:16). Christ is not only the agent of creation but its goal. All things were made for His glory.

Because Christ is the Creator, He is also the Redeemer. The same power that spoke the universe into existence is at work to bring about new creation. "Therefore if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creature; the old things passed away; behold, new things have come" (2 Corinthians 5:17).

### The Spirit's Role
The Holy Spirit was active in creation, "hovering over the face of the waters" (Genesis 1:2). The Spirit who brought order out of chaos, who beautified and filled creation, is the same Spirit who regenerates dead souls and produces fruit in believers.

### The Future Hope
Creation itself will be redeemed. We await "new heavens and a new earth, in which righteousness dwells" (2 Peter 3:13). The groaning of creation will give way to glory. The story that begins in a garden ends in a city — the New Jerusalem, where God dwells with His people forever.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Creation Ex Nihilo | Creation out of nothing — God did not use pre-existing materials |

| Dominion | Man's God-given stewardship over creation |
| Protoevangelium | The first gospel promise (Genesis 3:15) |
| New Creation | The future redeemed state of all things in Christ |
