# Lessons: Creation
## 7 Lessons for Discipleship

---

## Lesson 1: In the Beginning, God

**Hook:**
"Before the mountains were born, before the earth and the world were brought forth, God simply was."

**Key Scripture:** Genesis 1:1 — "In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
The Bible does not argue for God's existence — it assumes it. The first four words of Scripture establish the foundational truth of the Christian faith: God is eternal, self-existent, and the sovereign Creator of all things. He did not create out of need, but out of His own good pleasure and for His own glory.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Genesis 1:1 — God is before all things
- Psalm 90:2 — "Before the mountains were born... from everlasting to everlasting, You are God"
- Isaiah 45:18 — "Thus says the Lord, who created the heavens, He is the God who formed the earth and made it"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The Creator-creature distinction is the most fundamental distinction in the Bible. God is not a part of His creation — He is separate from it, sovereign over it, and independent of it. All other beings (angels, humans, animals) are created, contingent, and dependent. God alone is uncreated. This truth shapes everything: worship belongs to the Creator, not to the creature. When we confuse Creator and creature, we commit idolatry.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Because God is the Creator, He has rightful authority over your life. You do not belong to yourself. Your body, your time, your resources, your future — all belong to Him. The question is not whether God has a right to command you, but whether you will submit to His rightful authority.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
"All things were created through Him and for Him" (Colossians 1:16). Christ is not only the agent of creation but its goal. The whole universe was made for His glory. To know the Creator is to know Christ, for He is the one through whom all things exist.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
The God who created all things is the same God who redeems all things. The Creator has not abandoned His creation. He is working to make all things new.

## Lesson 2: Let Us Make Man

**Hook:**
"God spoke galaxies into being with a word, but when He made man, He stooped down and formed him from the dust."

**Key Scripture:** Genesis 1:26-27 — "Then God said, 'Let Us make man in Our image, according to Our likeness.'"

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Humanity is the pinnacle of God's creative work. Not because of anything inherently worthy in man, but because God chose to set man apart — making him in His own image. This is the source of all human dignity, value, and purpose.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Genesis 1:26-27 — Made in God's image, male and female
- Genesis 2:7 — "The Lord God formed man of dust from the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life"
- Psalm 8:3-6 — "You have made him a little lower than God, and crown him with glory and majesty"
- Genesis 9:6 — "For in the image of God He made man"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The image of God (imago Dei) means that humans reflect God in ways that the rest of creation does not. We have rational minds, moral awareness, creativity, the capacity for relationship, and the responsibility of dominion — stewardship over creation as God's representatives. Both male and female equally bear God's image, reflecting the relational nature of the triune God. The image also means we are accountable. Being made in God's image is not merely a gift — it is a commission. We are called to represent God on earth.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Every person you meet bears the image of God. This changes how you speak to them, think about them, and treat them. The image is marred by sin but not erased. Your identity is not found in what you do, what you have, or what others think of you — it is found in whose image you bear. You belong to God.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is "the exact representation of God's nature" (Hebrews 1:3). He is the perfect image of God. Through faith in Christ, believers are being "conformed to the image of His Son" (Romans 8:29) — restored to what God intended humanity to be.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Though the image is marred, God is restoring it. One day we will be fully like Christ, bearing His image perfectly in the new creation.

## Lesson 3: The God Who Speaks

**Hook:**
"He spoke, and atoms obeyed. He commanded, and light exploded into being."

**Key Scripture:** Psalm 33:6 — "By the word of the Lord the heavens were made, and by the breath of His mouth all their host."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
God created not by effort or struggle, but by His word. He spoke, and it was done. This demonstrates God's absolute sovereignty and power. Creation is not the result of a cosmic battle, but the free act of a sovereign God who spoke the universe into existence.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Psalm 33:6-9 — "He spoke, and it was done; He commanded, and it stood fast"
- Genesis 1:3 — "Then God said, 'Let there be light'; and there was light"
- Hebrews 11:3 — "By faith we understand that the worlds were prepared by the word of God, so that what is seen was not made out of things which are visible"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Creation ex nihilo — out of nothing — means that God did not use pre-existing materials. He did not shape eternal matter or battle chaotic forces. He simply spoke, and what He commanded came into being. This is utterly unique. No human can create in this way; we can only shape what already exists. God's word carries creative power. The same God who spoke creation into existence speaks through His Word, the Scriptures, to bring about new creation in the hearts of His people.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
If God's word has such power, how should you treat His Word? The same voice that called light out of darkness speaks to you through Scripture. Do you believe that His Word has the power to change you? To create faith where there is unbelief? To bring life where there is death? Trust the power of God's Word in your life.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
"In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God... All things came into being through Him" (John 1:1-3). Christ is the eternal Word of God, the one through whom the Father created all things. The same Word who spoke creation into existence became flesh and dwelt among us.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
The God who speaks is still speaking through His Word. His voice has not lost its power. What He promises, He performs. What He commands, He creates.

## Lesson 4: It Is Very Good

**Hook:**
"Before sin, before sorrow, before death — there was a moment when God looked at everything and said, 'It is very good.'"

**Key Scripture:** Genesis 1:31 — "God saw all that He had made, and behold, it was very good."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
The original creation was perfect. There was no sin, no suffering, no death, no disease, no decay. Everything was exactly as God intended it to be. The goodness of creation is a declaration of God's character — a good God makes a good world.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Genesis 1:4, 10, 12, 18, 21, 25 — Repeated declaration: "it was good"
- Genesis 1:31 — "It was very good"
- Genesis 2:8-9 — The Garden of Eden as a place of beauty and provision
- 1 Timothy 4:4 — "Everything created by God is good"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The goodness of creation is not about moral perfection in the abstract — it is about purpose and function. Everything was working as designed. The light separated from darkness, the waters gathered, the plants produced seed, the creatures multiplied — all according to God's design. Creation was marked by order, beauty, abundance, and harmony. There was no conflict between man and God, man and woman, man and creation, or creature and creature. This original goodness gives us the standard by which we understand the tragedy of the fall and the hope of redemption.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Though creation is now marred by sin, it is not evil. Physicality, materiality, and the human body are not inherently sinful. God created a physical world and called it good. You can enjoy creation — food, beauty, relationships, work — with gratitude to the Creator, without falling into either asceticism (denying creation as evil) or idolatry (worshipping creation instead of the Creator).

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Through Christ, God is making all things new. The goodness of the original creation points forward to the glory of the new creation. What was lost in Adam will be restored in Christ. The creation that was subjected to futility will be set free.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
The story does not end in a fallen world. The God who declared the original creation "very good" is bringing about a new heavens and a new earth, where everything will again be exactly as He intends.

## Lesson 5: What May Be Known

**Hook:**
"The skeptic says there is no evidence for God. The Bible says there is so much evidence that no one has an excuse."

**Key Scripture:** Romans 1:20 — "For since the creation of the world His invisible attributes, His eternal power and divine nature, have been clearly seen, being understood through what has been made, so that they are without excuse."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
God has revealed Himself through creation. This is called general revelation. Every human being has access to it. The heavens, the earth, the complexity of life, the order of the cosmos — all bear witness to the Creator. This revelation is clear, universal, and sufficient to hold every person accountable before God.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Romans 1:18-23 — God's wrath revealed against those who suppress the truth
- Psalm 19:1-4 — "The heavens declare the glory of God"
- Acts 14:15-17 — God "did not leave Himself without witness"
- Acts 17:24-28 — Paul's address at the Areopagus

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
General revelation is clear but rejected. The problem is not a lack of evidence but a suppression of truth. People "suppress the truth in unrighteousness" (Romans 1:18). They know God exists but do not honor Him as God or give thanks. Instead, they exchange the glory of God for idols. General revelation is sufficient to condemn — it leaves humanity without excuse — but it is not sufficient to save. It reveals God's power and divine nature, but not His plan of redemption through Christ. For that, special revelation — Scripture and the gospel — is necessary.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Do you see God in creation? When you look at the stars, the ocean, a newborn child — do you recognize the hand of the Creator? Give thanks to God for the beauty and order of His creation. And remember: the people around you who do not acknowledge God are not lacking evidence. They are suppressing the truth. Pray for their hearts to be opened.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is "the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of His nature" (Hebrews 1:3). General revelation shows that God exists; Christ shows us who God is. The Word who was in the beginning with God — through whom all things were made — has now been revealed in the flesh, the most complete revelation of God to man.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
God has not hidden Himself. From the heavens above to the conscience within, He has made Himself known. And He has made Himself fully known in Jesus Christ. No one who seeks Him will fail to find Him.

## Lesson 6: The Spirit of Creation

**Hook:**
"Before the first word was spoken, the Spirit was there — hovering, waiting, ready to bring order out of chaos."

**Key Scripture:** Genesis 1:2 — "The earth was formless and void, and darkness was over the surface of the deep, and the Spirit of God was hovering over the surface of the waters."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
The Holy Spirit was actively involved in creation. The Spirit did not merely observe — He participated. He hovered over the chaotic waters, bringing order, beauty, and life. The Spirit is the one who fills, beautifies, and empowers creation. His work in the original creation parallels His work in the new creation.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Genesis 1:2 — The Spirit hovering
- Psalm 104:30 — "You send forth Your Spirit, they are created; and You renew the face of the ground"
- Job 33:4 — "The Spirit of God has made me, and the breath of the Almighty gives me life"
- 2 Corinthians 5:17 — "If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creature"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The Spirit's work in creation reveals His personhood and deity. The Spirit is not an impersonal force but a divine Person who acts with purpose and power. In creation, the Spirit brought order out of chaos. In redemption, the Spirit does the same — bringing spiritual life out of spiritual death, order out of sinful chaos. The Spirit who filled the creation with life is the same Spirit who fills believers with new life in Christ. Every good thing in creation — every work of beauty, every expression of life, every act of renewal — is ultimately the work of the Spirit.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Do you have a relationship with the Holy Spirit, or do you ignore Him? The same Spirit who was active in creation is active in your life if you are in Christ. He is the one who convicts you of sin, illuminates Scripture, produces fruit in your character, and empowers you to serve. Do not grieve the Spirit. Do not quench the Spirit. Walk by the Spirit.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
The Spirit who hovered over creation is the same Spirit who descended on Jesus at His baptism and who raised Jesus from the dead. The Spirit takes what is Christ's and makes it known to us. The new creation in Christ is the work of the Spirit, applying the finished work of the Son.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
The Spirit is still at work. Where the Spirit is, there is life, freedom, beauty, and order. The Spirit who began a good work in you will carry it to completion.

## Lesson 7: From Creation to New Creation

**Hook:**
"The Bible begins in a garden and ends in a city. Between them is the story of a God who refuses to give up on His creation."

**Key Scripture:** 2 Corinthians 5:17 — "Therefore if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creature; the old things passed away; behold, new things have come."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Creation is not just the beginning of the Bible's story — it is the foundation for the entire biblical narrative. God created a good world. Sin marred it. But God is redeeming it. The end of the story is not destruction but renewal — a new heavens and a new earth where righteousness dwells. Redemption is not the abandonment of creation but its restoration.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Isaiah 65:17 — "For behold, I create new heavens and a new earth"
- 2 Peter 3:13 — "We look for new heavens and a new earth, in which righteousness dwells"
- Revelation 21:1-5 — "Behold, I am making all things new"
- Romans 8:19-23 — Creation eagerly awaits its redemption

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The Bible's story is not a circle (returning to the Garden as it was) but a line moving toward something even greater. The first creation was good; the new creation will be glorious. The first creation was marred by sin; the new creation will be imperishable. The first creation was the stage for human history; the new creation will be the eternal home of the redeemed. Already, through Christ, the new creation has begun. Every believer is a new creation. The resurrection of Christ is the firstfruits of the new creation — a guarantee that what God began in Genesis, He will complete in Revelation.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
You are part of this story. If you are in Christ, you are already a new creation. The old has passed away. But you are also awaiting the final consummation. Live with hope. Your labor in the Lord is not in vain. Every act of obedience, every step of faithfulness, every moment of service is part of God's new creation work. Do not cling to this passing world. Set your mind on things above, where Christ is.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is the Alpha and the Omega, the Beginning and the End. He created all things, and He will make all things new. Through His death and resurrection, He has secured the redemption of all creation. The One who hung on a cross is the One who holds the keys of death and Hades. He is the author of creation and the finisher of new creation.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
The God who began His work in creation will complete His work in new creation. He who promised is faithful. The groaning of this present age will give way to the glory of the age to come. He is making all things new — and He will not fail.
