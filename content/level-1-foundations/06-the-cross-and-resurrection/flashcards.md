# Flashcards: The Cross & Resurrection
## 10 Q&A Pairs + Memory Verse Drill

---

### Q1 — What is the divine dilemma?

**A:** The Bible says God forgives all types of sin, yet also says He will punish every sin. The dilemma is: how can God be just and still justify the wicked? The answer is the cross — God punished sin in Christ so He could forgive sinners justly.

**Passages:** Exodus 34:6-7; Proverbs 17:15; Romans 3:23-26

---

### Q2 — What was in the cup Jesus asked to be removed in Gethsemane?

**A:** The cup was the wrath of Almighty God. Jesus was not afraid of physical suffering (martyrs went to crosses singing). He was facing the full, undiluted wrath of God against the sins of His people.

**Passages:** Matthew 26:39; Jeremiah 25:15-16; Psalm 75:8

---

### Q3 — What does it mean that Christ was made sin for us?

**A:** It means our sin was imputed (legally credited) to Christ on the cross. He was treated as guilty and bore the punishment we deserved, even though He was personally sinless. His righteousness is then credited to us.

**Passages:** 2 Corinthians 5:21; Isaiah 53:4-6; Galatians 3:13

---

### Q4 — What is propitiation?

**A:** Propitiation is a sacrifice that satisfies the justice of God and appeases His wrath. Christ displayed publicly as a propitiation in His blood — demonstrating that God is both just and the justifier of those who believe.

**Passages:** Romans 3:25-26; 1 John 2:2; Hebrews 2:17

---

### Q5 — What is justification?

**A:** Justification is God's legal declaration that a believer is righteous through faith in Christ. It is not God making us righteous internally — it is God declaring us righteous forensically, as a judge pronounces a verdict.

**Passages:** Romans 3:24; Romans 4:5; Romans 5:1; Romans 8:1

---

### Q6 — Why is the resurrection essential to Christianity?

**A:** If Christ has not been raised, then preaching is useless, faith is futile, the apostles are liars, believers are still in their sins, the dead have perished, and Christians are most pitied. The resurrection is the capstone — remove it and Christianity collapses.

**Passages:** 1 Corinthians 15:1-8; 1 Corinthians 15:12-19

---

### Q7 — What evidence does Paul give for the resurrection?

**A:** Eyewitness testimony: Christ appeared to Peter, the Twelve, more than 500 brothers at once (most still alive when Paul wrote), James, all the apostles, and Paul himself. This is the most well-attested fact in ancient history.

**Passages:** 1 Corinthians 15:5-8; Acts 2:24

---

### Q8 — What kind of body will believers receive at the resurrection?

**A:** An imperishable, glorified body — sown perishable, raised imperishable; sown in dishonor, raised in glory; sown in weakness, raised in power; sown a natural body, raised a spiritual body. We will bear the image of the man of heaven.

**Passages:** 1 Corinthians 15:35-49; Philippians 3:20-21

---

### Q9 — How does Christ's resurrection guarantee our resurrection?

**A:** Through union with Christ and federal headship. As in Adam all die, so in Christ all are made alive. Christ is the firstborn from the dead — the prototype. Our union with Him means His resurrection secures ours.

**Passages:** 1 Corinthians 15:21-22; Romans 5:12-19; Colossians 1:18; Ephesians 2:5-6

---

### Q10 — What does it mean that death is swallowed up in victory?

**A:** The sting of death is sin, and the power of sin is the law. Christ satisfied the law's demands and removed sin's penalty. Death no longer has power to condemn believers. It is now a doorway into the presence of Christ.

**Passages:** 1 Corinthians 15:54-58; Romans 6:4-9; Romans 8:37-39

---

## Memory Verse Drill

**Prompt:** "The LORD, the LORD God, compassionate and gracious —"
**Response:** " — slow to anger, and abounding in lovingkindness and truth." (Exodus 34:6)

**Prompt:** "All of us like sheep have gone astray —"
**Response:** " — each of us has turned to his own way; but the LORD has caused the iniquity of us all to fall on Him." (Isaiah 53:6)

**Prompt:** "For all have sinned —"
**Response:** " — and fall short of the glory of God, being justified as a gift by His grace through the redemption which is in Christ Jesus." (Romans 3:23-24)

**Prompt:** "He made Him who knew no sin —"
**Response:** " — to be sin on our behalf, so that we might become the righteousness of God in Him." (2 Corinthians 5:21)

**Prompt:** "Christ died for our sins —"
**Response:** " — according to the Scriptures, and that He was buried, and that He was raised on the third day according to the Scriptures." (1 Corinthians 15:3-4)

**Prompt:** "It is sown a perishable body —"
**Response:** " — it is raised an imperishable body; it is sown in dishonor, it is raised in glory." (1 Corinthians 15:42-43)

**Prompt:** "Death is swallowed up in victory —"
**Response:** " — O death, where is your victory? O death, where is your sting?" (1 Corinthians 15:54-55)

**Prompt:** "Thanks be to God, who gives us the victory —"
**Response:** " — through our Lord Jesus Christ. Therefore, my beloved brethren, be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord." (1 Corinthians 15:57-58)
