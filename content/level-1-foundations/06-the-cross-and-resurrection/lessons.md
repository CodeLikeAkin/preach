# Lessons: The Cross & Resurrection
## 7 Lessons for Discipleship

---

## Lesson 1: The Divine Dilemma

**Hook:**
"If God is just, He cannot simply forgive you. A judge who pardons a murderer is not merciful — he is corrupt."

**Key Scripture:** Exodus 34:6-7 — "The LORD, the LORD God, compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, and abounding in lovingkindness and truth; who keeps lovingkindness for thousands, who forgives iniquity, transgression and sin; yet He will by no means leave the guilty unpunished."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
The Bible presents a seemingly impossible problem. On one hand, God declares Himself compassionate and forgiving — He forgives iniquity, transgression, and sin. On the other hand, He declares He will by no means leave the guilty unpunished. How can both be true? How can a holy God forgive sinners without compromising His justice? This is the central question the entire Bible exists to answer.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Exodus 34:6-7 — God forgives and punishes
- Proverbs 17:15 — "He who justifies the wicked is an abomination to the Lord"
- Romans 3:23-26 — "All have sinned... being justified as a gift by His grace"
- Romans 3:26 — "So that He would be just and the justifier of the one who has faith in Jesus"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The divine dilemma is this: God cannot simply ignore sin. If He did, He would be an unjust judge — worse than the criminals He pardons. The Old Testament sacrifices could not ultimately solve this problem — they only pointed forward to the one who could. The answer is the cross. On Calvary, God poured out every ounce of wrath that His people deserved upon His own Son. Every sin that would be forgiven was first punished — punished in Christ. This means that when God forgives you, He is not being lenient. He is not sweeping your sin under a rug. He already punished every one of your sins in the body of His Son. Justice has been served.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Do not think that God forgives because He is too soft to punish. The cross shows you that God hates sin more than you can imagine. He crushed His own Son rather than leave sin unpunished. When you struggle to believe you are forgiven, remember: every sin you have ever committed or ever will commit was already punished at Calvary. There is no outstanding debt.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is the answer to the dilemma that no philosopher could solve and no religion could resolve. In Him, God's justice and mercy meet. He is the propitiation — the sacrifice that satisfies God's wrath and demonstrates His righteousness.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Because of the cross, you do not serve a God who compromises His justice to forgive. You serve a God who satisfied His justice through His own Son. You can be fully forgiven without God being unjust in the slightest. The debt is paid.

## Lesson 2: The Cup of Wrath

**Hook:**
"Jesus was not afraid of the cross — He was facing something infinitely worse: the full, undiluted wrath of Almighty God."

**Key Scripture:** Matthew 26:39 — "And He went a little beyond them, and fell on His face and prayed, saying, 'My Father, if it is possible, let this cup pass from Me; yet not as I will, but as You will.'"

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
In Gethsemane, Jesus was not recoiling from physical suffering. Thousands of Christian martyrs went to their crosses singing hymns and rejoicing. What terrified the sinless Son of God was the cup of God's wrath — the holy hatred of God against sin that He was about to drink to its dregs.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Matthew 26:36-44 — Jesus prays three times about the cup
- Jeremiah 25:15-16 — "Take this cup of the wine of wrath from My hand"
- Psalm 22:1-6 — "My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?"
- Psalm 75:8 — "For a cup is in the hand of the LORD... all the wicked of the earth must drink"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The cup is a consistent biblical symbol for the wrath of God. Jeremiah was told to make the nations drink the cup of God's wrath. In Gethsemane, Christ looked into that cup and saw every sin of every person who would ever believe — every lie, every lust, every act of rebellion, every secret thought. He saw the full fury of divine justice that those sins deserved. And He drank it all. On the cross, from the sixth to the ninth hour, darkness covered the land — a darkness like the plague of Egypt. In that darkness, Christ was shut up alone with the wrath of God. Wave after wave of divine judgment crashed over Him until every demand of justice was satisfied. Then He cried out, "It is finished!" The cup was empty.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
When you face suffering, remember: the worst suffering you will ever face is nothing compared to what Christ endured for you. He drank the cup of wrath so that you will never have to taste it. Every trial in this life is temporary and filtered through the hands of a loving Father. There is no wrath left for you.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ did not merely sympathize with your suffering — He endured what you will never have to endure. He became the curse for you. He was forsaken so that you would never be forsaken. The darkness He entered guarantees that you will always walk in the light of His presence.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Because Christ drank the cup of wrath to its last drop, there is no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus. The cup is empty. Justice is satisfied. You will never face the wrath of God.

## Lesson 3: Substitutionary Atonement

**Hook:**
"Christ was not punished for His own sins — He had none. He was punished for yours. He stood in your place and received what you deserved."

**Key Scripture:** 2 Corinthians 5:21 — "He made Him who knew no sin to be sin on our behalf, so that we might become the righteousness of God in Him."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Every religion in the world teaches self-salvation: do enough good works, follow enough rules, and you will be saved. Christianity is radically different. Christianity teaches substitution — someone else did for you what you could not do for yourself. Christ took your place. He bore your sin. He died your death.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- 2 Corinthians 5:21 — He who knew no sin became sin for us
- Isaiah 53:4-6 — "The LORD has caused the iniquity of us all to fall upon Him"
- Isaiah 53:10 — "The LORD was pleased to crush Him"
- Galatians 3:13 — "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the Law, having become a curse for us"
- 1 Peter 2:24 — "He Himself bore our sins in His body on the cross"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Substitutionary atonement means that Christ died in your place. This is not an abstract theory — it is a legal transaction called imputation. Your sin was legally credited to Christ, and His righteousness was legally credited to you. He was treated as guilty so you could be treated as righteous. He bore the specific curses of the law — cursed as an idolator, cursed as an adulterer, cursed as a murderer — because He was bearing the specific sins of His people. The infinite worth of Christ means His sacrifice has infinite value. One man suffering a few hours on a cross could save an infinite number of people because that one man is God in the flesh. His worth outweighs all of creation combined.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Stop trying to pay for your own sins. Every time you wallow in guilt as though Christ's death was insufficient, you insult the sacrifice of the Son of God. Your sin was punished fully at Calvary. There is nothing left for you to pay. You are not saved by your goodness — you are saved because Christ's goodness was credited to you.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is the last Adam. The first Adam brought condemnation and death to all who were in him. The last Adam brings justification and life to all who are in Him. Where Adam failed, Christ succeeded. Where Adam's disobedience brought the curse, Christ's obedience brings the blessing.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Your sins have been transferred to Christ and will never be held against you. His righteousness has been transferred to you and will never be taken away. The exchange is complete and permanent.

## Lesson 4: Justification

**Hook:**
"The moment you believe in Jesus, God does not make you righteous — He declares you righteous. It is a verdict from the throne of heaven."

**Key Scripture:** Romans 3:24 — "Being justified as a gift by His grace through the redemption which is in Christ Jesus."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Justification is a legal declaration. It is not God changing your nature — it is God changing your legal standing. The moment you believe in Jesus Christ, God declares you righteous before His law. You stand before Him not as forgiven sinner trying to do better, but as perfectly righteous in Christ.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Romans 3:23-26 — Justified as a gift by His grace
- Romans 4:5 — "To the one who does not work, but believes in Him who justifies the ungodly, his faith is credited as righteousness"
- Romans 5:1 — "Therefore, having been justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ"
- Romans 8:1 — "There is therefore now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
There are only two religions in the world: the religion of works and the religion of grace. The Muslim says, "I am righteous by my deeds." The Jew says, "I obey the law." But the Christian says, "I have no righteousness of my own. My only hope is the righteousness of another — Jesus Christ." Justification means God looks at the believing sinner and sees Christ's perfect obedience instead of the sinner's rebellion. Christ's active obedience (His perfect life of keeping the law) is credited to you. Christ's passive obedience (His suffering and death) paid for your sins. Together, they give you a perfect standing before God.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
You do not need to earn God's favor. You already have it in Christ. Stop trying to be good enough for God. You never will be. Instead, rest in the finished work of Christ. He is your righteousness. When God looks at you, He sees His Son. Live from that reality, not toward it.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is our righteousness. He is the end of the law for righteousness to everyone who believes. In Him, we are not only forgiven — we are declared righteous. We are not neutral — we are positively right before God.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Justification is permanent. It cannot be lost, undone, or diminished. If you are in Christ, your standing before God is as secure as Christ's own standing. You are accepted in the Beloved. There is no condemnation.

## Lesson 5: The Resurrection — Historical Fact

**Hook:**
"Christianity rises or falls with one event: the resurrection of Jesus Christ. If He did not rise, we are liars and fools. If He did rise, everything changes."

**Key Scripture:** 1 Corinthians 15:3-4 — "For I delivered to you as of first importance what I also received, that Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, and that He was buried, and that He was raised on the third day according to the Scriptures."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
The resurrection is not an optional belief within Christianity — it is the capstone in the arch. Remove it and the entire structure collapses. Paul treats the resurrection as a matter of first importance, and he provides three lines of evidence for it: authority, eyewitness evidence, and logical necessity.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- 1 Corinthians 15:1-8 — The gospel Paul received and passed on
- 1 Corinthians 15:12-19 — Seven consequences if no resurrection
- 1 Corinthians 15:20-28 — Christ the first fruits of the resurrection
- Acts 2:24 — "God raised Him up, putting an end to the agony of death"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Paul argues from authority: the apostolic gospel is the standard, and those who deny the resurrection are not offering a valid alternative interpretation — they are denying Christianity. Jesus Himself said to Peter, "Get behind Me, Satan" when Peter offered a different version of the Messiah's mission. Paul then presents eyewitness evidence: Peter, the Twelve, more than 500 brothers at one time (most still alive when Paul wrote), James, all the apostles, and Paul himself. This is the most well-attested fact in ancient history. The hallucination theory fails because 500 people do not share the same hallucination, and if they did, the body would still be in the tomb. Finally, Paul argues from logic: if there is no resurrection, then preaching is useless, faith is futile, the apostles are liars, believers are still in their sins, the dead have perished, and Christians are the most pitied people on earth. But Christ has been raised — every one of those conclusions is reversed.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Your faith is not based on wishful thinking. It is based on a historical event that happened in space and time, witnessed by hundreds of people, recorded in documents we can still read today. When doubt attacks, remember the evidence. The resurrection is the anchor of your hope.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
The same Christ who died for your sins is the Christ who rose from the dead. He is not a dead martyr — He is a living Lord. He holds the keys of death and Hades. He is alive forevermore.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Because Christ is risen, your salvation is secure. His resurrection is God's receipt — the payment for sin was accepted. If Christ had not satisfied the Father, He would not have been raised. His resurrection proves that your debt is paid in full.

## Lesson 6: The Resurrection Body

**Hook:**
"Your body is not a prison to be escaped — it is a seed to be transformed. The best is yet to come."

**Key Scripture:** 1 Corinthians 15:42-44 — "So also is the resurrection of the dead. It is sown a perishable body, it is raised an imperishable body; it is sown in dishonor, it is raised in glory; it is sown in weakness, it is raised in power; it is sown a natural body, it is raised a spiritual body."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
The resurrection is not the escape from the body into a purely spiritual existence. It is the transformation of the body into something glorious. Christians do not hope to become disembodied spirits floating on clouds — they hope to receive resurrection bodies like Christ's.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- 1 Corinthians 15:35-49 — The resurrection body explained
- 1 Corinthians 15:51-53 — "We will all be changed, in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye"
- Romans 8:23 — "We ourselves groan within ourselves, waiting eagerly for our adoption as sons, the redemption of our body"
- Philippians 3:20-21 — Christ "will transform the body of our humble state into conformity with the body of His glory"
- Colossians 1:18 — "He is the beginning, the firstborn from the dead"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Paul uses the analogy of a seed. What you sow is a bare kernel — but God gives it a body as He chooses. Your current body is the seed; your resurrection body is the harvest. The first Adam was from the dust; the last Adam (Christ) is from heaven. As we have borne the image of the dust, we shall bear the image of heaven. Christ is the firstborn from the dead — the prototype of the new creation. The resurrected Christ is a species that never existed before: a man who can never die again. And those who are in Christ will share that same resurrection life. Our union with Christ guarantees our resurrection. Because He lives, we will live also.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
This truth changes how you view your body. Your body is not irrelevant — it will be redeemed. The suffering, aging, and decay you experience now are temporary. You are not waiting to escape your body — you are waiting for your body to be redeemed. Treat your body with honor. It belongs to the Lord and will be raised like His.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is not only the guarantee of the resurrection — He is the pattern. His resurrection body is the template for yours. He ate food, could be touched, and bore the marks of His wounds — yet He was no longer subject to death. That is what awaits you.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
One day, the perishable will put on the imperishable. The mortal will put on immortality. Death will be swallowed up in victory. You will not be less than you are now — you will be infinitely more.

## Lesson 7: Victory Over Death

**Hook:**
"Death is not the end. The last enemy has already been defeated. The victory is won."

**Key Scripture:** 1 Corinthians 15:54-57 — "But when this perishable will have put on the imperishable, and this mortal will have put on immortality, then will come about the saying that is written, 'Death is swallowed up in victory. O death, where is your victory? O death, where is your sting?' The sting of death is sin, and the power of sin is the law; but thanks be to God, who gives us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Death is not natural — it is the enemy. God did not create humans to die. Death entered the world through sin, and it has reigned over humanity ever since. But Christ has conquered death. Through His own death and resurrection, He has broken death's power forever.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- 1 Corinthians 15:54-58 — Death swallowed up in victory
- Romans 6:4-5 — We walk in newness of life; united with Him in resurrection
- Romans 6:9 — "Christ, having been raised from the dead, is never to die again; death no longer is master over Him"
- Romans 8:37-39 — Nothing can separate us from the love of God
- Ephesians 2:5-6 — Made us alive together with Christ, raised us up, seated us with Him

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The sting of death is sin — it is sin that gives death its power to terrify and condemn. The power of sin is the law — the law reveals our sin and pronounces judgment. Christ has dealt with both. Through His perfect obedience, He satisfied the law's demands. Through His substitutionary death, He removed sin's penalty. The sting has been extracted. Death is no longer a punishment — it is a doorway. But the victory is not just future — it is present. We have been raised with Christ. We are to walk in newness of life right now. The same power that raised Christ from the dead is at work in us, giving us victory over sin, empowering us for holiness, and enabling us to live for God. This resurrection life has absolute mastery over sin, death, and Satan.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Stop living as though death has the last word. You are a resurrection person. You have been raised with Christ, and your life is hidden with Christ in God. You do not have to fear death. You do not have to fear the devil. You do not have to be enslaved to sin. The power that raised Jesus is in you. Live like it.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is the victor over death. He holds the keys of death and Hades. He is the resurrection and the life. To be absent from the body is to be present with the Lord. Because He conquered death, death is now the servant of the saints — it ushers us into His presence.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Because Christ has conquered death, your labor in the Lord is not in vain. Nothing you do for Him is wasted. The resurrection guarantees that every prayer, every act of obedience, every gospel conversation has eternal significance. Be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord.
