# Study Guide: Who Is Man?

## Module Overview
Module 04 — Who Is Man explores the biblical doctrine of humanity: our creation in God's image, our constitution as body/soul/spirit, the fall and its effects, total depravity, and the hope of restoration in Christ.

---

## 1. Made in God's Image

### Core Truth
Humanity is the pinnacle of God's creative work. "Then God said, 'Let Us make man in Our image, according to Our likeness'" (Genesis 1:26). This is not a statement about human worth in isolation but about God's sovereign choice to set man apart. The image of God (imago Dei) is what distinguishes humans from all other creation.

### The Triune God and the Image
The triune God — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — made man in His image. The plural "Let Us" hints at the plurality within the Godhead. Man was created for relationship, reflecting the relational nature of the triune God. The woman proceeded from the man's side, and children proceed from their union — a created reflection of the eternal relationship within the Trinity.

### What the Image Includes
- **Mentally**: Humans are rational, volitional agents who can reason and choose, reflecting God's intellect and freedom
- **Morally**: Humanity was created in righteousness and perfect innocence, reflecting God's holiness
- **Socially**: Humans were created for fellowship, reflecting God's triune nature and love

### Dominion and Purpose
As God's vice-regents, humans were given dominion over creation — not to abuse it but to work it and keep it. They were to be fruitful and multiply, filling the earth with image-bearers who would reflect God's glory.

## 2. The Constituent Parts of Man

### Body, Soul, and Spirit
Scripture presents man as a unified being composed of material and immaterial parts. "The Lord God formed man of dust from the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living being" (Genesis 2:7).

The body is the physical, material aspect of man. The soul (nephesh/psuche) refers to the seat of life, emotion, and will. The spirit (ruach/pneuma) is the aspect that relates to God and has eternal consciousness.

### Distinction Between Soul and Spirit
While closely related, soul and spirit are not identical. Hebrews 4:12 distinguishes them: "The word of God is living and active and sharper than any two-edged sword, and piercing as far as the division of soul and spirit." The soul is the seat of personality (mind, emotion, will); the spirit is the faculty by which man communes with God.

### The Whole Person
Man does not have a soul — he is a soul. He does not have a spirit — he is a spirit who possesses a soul and lives in a body. The whole person is accountable to God. At death, the body returns to dust but the spirit returns to God who gave it (Ecclesiastes 12:7).

## 3. The Fall of Man

### The Covenant of Works
God entered into a covenant with Adam at creation. Adam was constituted as the federal head (representative) of all mankind. By his obedience, he could have secured everlasting holiness for his descendants. The Tree of Life and the Sabbath served as sacraments of this covenant.

### The Temptation
The serpent questioned God's Word ("Did God actually say?"), denied God's Word ("You will not surely die"), and impugned God's motives ("God knows that when you eat of it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God"). Eve was deceived, but Adam sinned with full awareness.

### The Results of the Fall
- **Spiritual death**: Immediate separation from God; humanity is now "dead in trespasses and sins" (Ephesians 2:1)
- **Physical death**: The body begins to die; "the wages of sin is death" (Romans 6:23)
- **Eternal death**: Separation from God forever for those outside of Christ
- **The image marred**: The image of God was not erased but severely damaged
- **Creation cursed**: The ground was cursed; thorns and thistles appear; work becomes toil

### Federal Headship
Adam acted as the representative of all humanity. "Therefore, just as sin entered the world through one man, and death through sin, and in this way death came to all people, because all sinned" (Romans 5:12). We did not sin in Adam against our will — Adam was our perfect representative and we would have done no better.

## 4. Total Depravity

### What It Means
Total depravity does not mean that every person is as evil as they could possibly be. It means that sin has affected every part of our being — mind, will, emotions, and body. There is no part of man untouched by sin.

### What Scripture Says
- "The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately sick; who can understand it?" (Jeremiah 17:9)
- "None is righteous, no, not one; no one understands; no one seeks for God" (Romans 3:10-11)
- "The mind that is set on the flesh is hostile to God, for it does not submit to God's law; indeed, it cannot" (Romans 8:7)

### The Bondage of the Will
Man's will is not free in spiritual matters. Apart from grace, man is unable to choose God, love God, or please God. "No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws him" (John 6:44). Dead men do not choose life — they must be made alive.

### Implications
- Man is not sick but dead in sin
- Man does not seek God; God seeks man
- Salvation cannot be initiated by man's decision
- The sinner needs nothing less than regeneration — being born again

## 5. The Sin Nature

### Origin and Transmission
The sin nature is inherited from Adam. "I was brought forth in iniquity, and in sin did my mother conceive me" (Psalm 51:5). This is not a statement about David's parents but about the universal condition of mankind. Every person is born with a nature inclined toward sin.

### Effects of the Sin Nature
- **Natural inclination to sin**: No one has to teach a child to lie or be selfish
- **Hostility toward God**: The natural mind is at enmity with God
- **Inability to please God**: "Those who are in the flesh cannot please God" (Romans 8:8)
- **Spiritual blindness**: "The natural person does not accept the things of the Spirit of God, for they are folly to him, and he is not able to understand them" (1 Corinthians 2:14)

### The Universal Reality
Every human being — apart from Christ — has a sin nature. The only exception is Jesus Christ, who was conceived by the Holy Spirit and born without sin. His virgin birth allowed Him to bypass the curse passed down from Adam.

---

## 6. Human Dignity and Depravity

### The Paradox
Man is both glorious and wretched. Made in the image of God, yet fallen in sin. Able to reason and create, yet hostile to his Creator. Capable of great love and great evil. This paradox runs throughout Scripture.

### The Image Remains
Though marred, the image of God is not erased. Even after the fall, God declares: "Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed, for God made man in his own image" (Genesis 9:6). James 3:9 warns that we bless God and curse men "who are made in the likeness of God." Human dignity is rooted in creation, not in human achievement.

### The Cross Reveals Both
The cross reveals both the extent of human depravity and the value of human souls. As R.C. Sproul said: "By taking sin seriously, we take man seriously. Evil may mar the divine image and cloud its brilliance, but it cannot destroy it. The image can be defaced, but it can never be erased. The most obscene symbol in human history is the Cross; yet in its ugliness it remains the most eloquent testimony to human dignity."

## 7. The Hope of New Creation

### Regeneration
Regeneration is the work of the Holy Spirit by which a person is made spiritually alive. It is not the result of human decision but the sovereign work of God. "He saved us, not because of works done by us in righteousness, but according to his own mercy, by the washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit" (Titus 3:5).

### A New Nature
Those who are in Christ are new creations. "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; the old has passed away; behold, the new has come" (2 Corinthians 5:17). The believer receives a new nature — not sinless perfection, but a fundamental reorientation toward God.

### The Ongoing Struggle
The sin nature remains in the believer, resulting in a lifelong struggle. Paul describes this in Romans 7:15-25: "I do not understand my own actions. For I do not do what I want, but I do the very thing I hate." But the Spirit provides the power to overcome.

### Ultimate Restoration
God is restoring His image in believers through sanctification. We are being "conformed to the image of his Son" (Romans 8:29). The work that God began will be completed. One day, we will bear Christ's image perfectly in the new creation.
