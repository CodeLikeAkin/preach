# Lessons: Who Is Man?
## 7 Lessons for Discipleship

---

## Lesson 1: Let Us Make Man

**Hook:**
"God spoke galaxies into being, but when He made man, He stooped down and formed him from the dust and breathed into him His own breath."

**Key Scripture:** Genesis 1:26-27 — "Then God said, 'Let Us make man in Our image, according to Our likeness.'"

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Humanity is the pinnacle of God's creation — not because of any inherent worth in man, but because God chose to make us in His image. The imago Dei is what sets humans apart from animals and angels. It gives us dignity, purpose, and accountability.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Genesis 1:26-27 — Made in God's image, male and female
- Genesis 2:7 — God formed man from dust and breathed life into him
- Psalm 8:3-6 — "You have made him a little lower than God, and crown him with glory and majesty"
- Genesis 9:6 — The image remains after the fall

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The image of God means that humans reflect God in ways nothing else does. Mentally, we can reason and choose. Morally, we have conscience and accountability. Socially, we are made for relationship — with God and with each other. This image is the basis for all human rights and dignity. It is not earned but given. Every person — regardless of age, ability, race, or status — bears God's image. This is why murder is a capital crime (Genesis 9:6) and why cursing another person is inconsistent with blessing God (James 3:9-10).

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
How you treat others reveals what you believe about the image of God. Do you see God's image in the poor, the unborn, the elderly, the difficult person in your life? Your dignity is not in what you do but in whose image you bear. Rest in that. And extend that dignity to everyone you meet.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is "the exact representation of God's nature" (Hebrews 1:3). He is the perfect image of God. Through faith, believers are being conformed to His image. What was lost in Adam is being restored in Christ.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
The image is marred but not erased. God is restoring it. One day we will bear the image of Christ perfectly.

## Lesson 2: The Fall of Man

**Hook:**
"Adam stood as our representative. When he fell, we all fell with him."

**Key Scripture:** Romans 5:12 — "Therefore, just as sin entered the world through one man, and death through sin, and in this way death came to all people, because all sinned."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Adam was not just the first man — he was our federal head, our representative before God. When Adam sinned, he acted on behalf of all humanity. His sin is imputed to us, and his corrupt nature is inherited by us.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Genesis 3:1-7 — The account of the fall
- Romans 5:12-19 — Adam as type of Christ, federal headship
- 1 Corinthians 15:21-22 — "In Adam all die"
- Ephesians 2:1-3 — Dead in trespasses and sins

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The doctrine of federal headship explains why all humanity is condemned because of one man's sin. Adam was our covenant representative. If he had obeyed, we would have been confirmed in righteousness. When he disobeyed, we fell with him. This is not unjust — Adam was the perfect representative for humanity. None of us would have done better. The fall brought spiritual death (separation from God), physical death (the body decays), and eternal death (hell for those outside of Christ). Every part of creation was affected.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
You were born into a fallen world as a fallen person. This is not an excuse for sin — it is an explanation. Your problem is not just bad habits or a difficult environment. Your problem is that you are born alienated from God. You need more than moral improvement — you need to be born again.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
As the first Adam brought condemnation, the last Adam (Christ) brings justification. "For as in Adam all die, so also in Christ shall all be made alive" (1 Corinthians 15:22). Christ came to do what Adam failed to do and to undo what Adam did.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
The fall is not the end of the story. Even in the curse, God promised the seed of the woman who would crush the serpent's head. Redemption was planned before the foundation of the world.

## Lesson 3: Total Depravity

**Hook:**
"The heart is not sick — it is dead. Dead men do not choose life."

**Key Scripture:** Ephesians 2:1 — "And you were dead in the trespasses and sins."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Total depravity means that sin has affected every part of man — mind, will, emotions, and body. There is no part of human nature untouched by sin. Man is not merely weakened but spiritually dead, unable and unwilling to come to God apart from grace.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Romans 3:10-18 — "None is righteous, no not one"
- Ephesians 2:1-3 — "Dead in trespasses and sins"
- Jeremiah 17:9 — "The heart is deceitful above all things"
- Romans 8:7-8 — "The mind set on the flesh is hostile to God"
- John 6:44 — "No one can come to me unless the Father draws him"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Total depravity does not mean everyone is as evil as possible. It means sin is total in its extent — it affects every faculty. The mind is darkened, the will is in bondage, the emotions are disordered, and the body is subject to death. Man does not seek God; he suppresses the truth about God. Man does not love God; he is hostile to God. Man cannot please God; his best works are like filthy rags. This is not because man lacks knowledge — creation clearly reveals God — but because he loves darkness rather than light. The will is not free in spiritual matters. It is free to choose sin but not free to choose God apart from grace.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Do you still think you are basically good? Scripture says otherwise. Humility begins with an honest assessment of your condition. You are not capable of saving yourself or even of choosing God on your own. Your only hope is grace. This is both humbling and liberating — you do not have to manufacture faith or earn salvation. You must cry out to God to do what you cannot do.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ came to save the dead, not the self-sufficient. He did not come to help those who help themselves — He came to raise the dead. Salvation is not cooperation but resurrection.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
What is impossible with man is possible with God. The same God who said "Let there be light" can shine in your dark heart. The God who raised Christ from the dead can make you alive.

## Lesson 4: The Sin Nature

**Hook:**
"No one taught you to lie. It came naturally. That is the sin nature."

**Key Scripture:** Psalm 51:5 — "Behold, I was brought forth in iniquity, and in sin did my mother conceive me."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Every person is born with a sin nature — an inherent inclination toward rebellion against God. It is not learned but inherited from Adam. It is universal, pervasive, and can only be overcome by the power of God.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Psalm 51:5 — Born with sin
- Psalm 58:3 — "The wicked go astray from the womb"
- Romans 5:12 — Sin entered through one man
- Romans 7:14-25 — Paul's struggle with the sin nature
- 1 Corinthians 2:14 — The natural person cannot understand spiritual things

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The sin nature (sometimes called original sin) is the corruption of human nature inherited from Adam. It is not merely a tendency to sin but a condition of sin. It manifests itself in actual sins — thoughts, words, and deeds that violate God's law. The sin nature means that man is not a neutral observer weighing options between good and evil. He is by nature a child of wrath (Ephesians 2:3), inclined toward evil, averse to good, and hostile to God. This does not mean man is incapable of civic virtue or natural goodness — the lost can do good things for society. But even these good things are not motivated by love for God and therefore cannot please Him.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Your sin is not merely a collection of bad habits or mistakes. It flows from who you are by nature. You need more than behavior modification — you need a new nature. Do not underestimate the power of indwelling sin. Do not be surprised when you struggle. But do not make peace with sin either. The Christian life is a war against the flesh.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Jesus alone was born without a sin nature. His virgin birth allowed Him to bypass the curse of Adam. He lived a perfectly sinless life and died as the perfect substitute for sinners. Through Him, believers receive a new nature and the power to overcome the old.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
"If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation" (2 Corinthians 5:17). The old nature is not eradicated in this life, but its power is broken. The Spirit gives victory over the flesh.

## Lesson 5: The Bondage of the Will

**Hook:**
"Dead men do not have free wills. They need to be made alive before they can choose."

**Key Scripture:** John 6:44 — "No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws him."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Since the fall, man's will is in bondage to sin. He is free to choose according to his nature, but his nature is corrupted. He will always choose sin over God because that is what he wants. He cannot choose God unless God first changes his heart.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- John 6:44 — No one can come unless drawn
- Romans 3:11 — "No one seeks for God"
- Romans 8:7-8 — Cannot submit to God's law
- 1 Corinthians 2:14 — Cannot understand spiritual things
- Ephesians 2:8-9 — Faith is a gift of God
- Philippians 2:13 — God works in you to will and to do

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The will is not an independent faculty floating above human nature. The will is the instrument of the nature. A corrupt nature produces corrupt choices. A tree is known by its fruit — a bad tree cannot bear good fruit. Man chooses sin freely — he does what he wants. But what he wants is sin. His will is free in the sense that he is not coerced, but it is bound in the sense that he cannot choose otherwise given his nature. Fallen man has a bias toward evil that makes it impossible for him to choose God. This is why salvation must be monergistic — a work of God alone, not a cooperation between God and man.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Does this mean you should do nothing? No. The Bible commands repentance and faith. But it teaches that even your desire to repent and believe is a gift from God. So pray: "Lord, give me a new heart. Take my heart of stone and give me a heart of flesh. Draw me to yourself, for I cannot come on my own."

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Jesus is the Good Shepherd who calls His sheep by name. The sheep hear His voice because He has chosen them, not because they were looking for Him. He lays down His life for the sheep, and no one can snatch them from His hand.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
"If the Son sets you free, you will be free indeed" (John 8:36). The bondage of the will is broken in Christ. In Him, you are free to obey, free to love, free to choose God.

## Lesson 6: Body, Soul, and Spirit

**Hook:**
"You are not a body with a soul. You are a spirit who possesses a soul and lives in a body."

**Key Scripture:** 1 Thessalonians 5:23 — "May your whole spirit and soul and body be kept blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Man is a unified being with distinct aspects: body (physical), soul (mind, emotion, will), and spirit (the faculty that communes with God). These are not separate compartments but integrated dimensions of the whole person.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Genesis 2:7 — God formed man from dust and breathed life into him
- Hebrews 4:12 — Divides soul and spirit
- Ecclesiastes 12:7 — The spirit returns to God
- 2 Corinthians 4:16 — "Though our outer man is wasting away, our inner man is being renewed"
- Luke 23:46 — "Into your hands I commit my spirit"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Scripture presents man as a duality (body and soul/spirit) or trichotomy (body, soul, and spirit). The body is the physical, material aspect — made from the dust, subject to decay and death. The soul is the seat of personality — mind, emotion, will, the "self." The spirit is the deepest part of man — that which knows God and is capable of eternal consciousness. The distinction between soul and spirit is subtle but real. Animals have souls (they have mind, emotion, will at a basic level), but only man is an eternal spirit made for communion with God. The fall affected all three aspects. The body became subject to death. The soul became corrupted in its thinking and desires. The spirit became dead toward God, unable to commune with Him until regenerated.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
You are responsible for your whole person — body, soul, and spirit. Do not neglect any aspect. The body is not evil — God created it and will raise it. Treat it with honor. The soul needs renewal through the Word of God. The spirit needs to be fed through prayer, worship, and the Word. Your spirit is the part of you that will live forever — invest in it.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Through Christ, the whole person is redeemed. The body will be raised glorified. The soul is being renewed. The spirit is made alive to God. Christ saves not just your spirit but your whole being.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Your body will be redeemed. The resurrection is not just spiritual survival — it is the redemption of your body. "He who raised Christ Jesus from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies" (Romans 8:11).

## Lesson 7: From Fallen to New Creation

**Hook:**
"You don't need a remodel — you need a resurrection."

**Key Scripture:** 2 Corinthians 5:17 — "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; the old has passed away; behold, the new has come."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
The gospel does not merely inform us about salvation — it creates it. Through regeneration, the Holy Spirit makes us new creatures. We are no longer defined by our fallen nature but by our union with Christ.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- 2 Corinthians 5:17 — New creation
- Ezekiel 36:26-27 — A new heart and a new spirit
- John 3:3-7 — You must be born again
- Romans 6:4 — Walk in newness of life
- Colossians 3:9-10 — Put off the old self, put on the new
- 1 John 3:9 — No one born of God makes a practice of sinning

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Regeneration is not self-improvement or behavior modification. It is a supernatural work of the Holy Spirit that makes a spiritually dead person alive. It produces repentance and faith — these are not the causes of regeneration but its first fruits. The new nature is not sinless perfection but a fundamental reorientation of the heart. The believer now loves what he once hated and hates what he once loved. The old nature remains, producing a lifelong struggle (Romans 7). But the new nature is the dominant reality that defines the believer. Sanctification is the process by which the new nature increasingly governs the believer's life.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
If you are in Christ, you are not the same person you were. Your identity has changed. You are no longer a sinner who occasionally does good — you are a saint who occasionally sins. The old labels no longer apply. You are a child of God, a new creation. Live like it. Put off the old self. Put on the new. The power for this life comes from the Spirit, not from your own effort.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Union with Christ is the ground of the new creation. You are in Christ. His death is your death to sin. His resurrection is your new life. Everything you need for life and godliness is found in Him.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
"He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion" (Philippians 1:6). The new creation is not yet fully revealed, but it is already real. One day, you will be like Christ, for you will see Him as He is.
