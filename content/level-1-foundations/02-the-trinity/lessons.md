## Lesson 1 — One God, Three Persons

### HOOK
Imagine loving someone deeply but having no way to understand what love really is. Now imagine being told that Love itself has a name and a face, and that Love has existed from all eternity not as a lonely force but as a perfect relationship between three Persons who have always known and delighted in one another. That is the God the Bible reveals.

### CORE TEACHING
The most basic truth about God is this: He is one. Deuteronomy 6:4 declares, "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one." This is the foundation on which everything else stands. But the Bible reveals something astonishing about this oneness. The word for "one" here is the Hebrew word *echad*, the same word used in Genesis 2:24 when it says a man and woman "shall become one flesh." It is a unity made of more than one. And that is exactly what we see throughout Scripture. At the baptism of Jesus, all three persons of the Godhead appear at the same moment — the Son is in the water, the Spirit descends like a dove, and the Father speaks from heaven. Jesus commands His disciples to baptize "in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit" — one name, three persons. The apostle Paul blesses the church with "the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the communion of the Holy Spirit." This is not three gods. The Bible is fiercely monotheistic. But neither is God one person wearing three different masks. The Father is not the Son, the Son is not the Spirit, and the Spirit is not the Father. They are distinct persons who share one divine nature. They have always existed in perfect love and harmony. Before creation existed, before angels sang, before time began, the Father loved the Son, the Son loved the Spirit, and the Spirit glorified the Father. This is the God we worship — not a lonely deity who needed a world to feel complete, but a Triune God who is love by His very nature.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Deuteronomy 6:4 — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one!" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Matthew 28:19 — "Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Mark 1:10-11 — "And immediately, coming up from the water, He saw the heavens parting and the Spirit descending upon Him like a dove. Then a voice came from heaven, 'You are My beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- II Corinthians 13:14 — "The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the communion of the Holy Spirit be with you all. Amen." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, read Mark 1:9-11 slowly three times. Each time, focus on a different person — first the Son, then the Spirit, then the Father. Write down one sentence about what each person does in this passage.

### SUMMARY
- God is one in essence but three in person — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
- The three persons are distinct, not three masks of the same person, and not three separate gods.
- The Trinity has existed eternally in love, so God did not create because He was lonely but from fullness.

## Lesson 2 — Who Is God the Father?

### HOOK
When you hear the word "Father," what comes to mind? For some it is warmth and safety. For others it is pain or absence. But when the Bible calls God "Father," it is not borrowing a human idea to describe God. It is revealing something eternally true about who He is.

### CORE TEACHING
God the Father is the first person of the Trinity. But the word "first" does not mean better, older, or more divine. The Father, Son, and Spirit are fully equal in every way. When we say the Father is "first," we mean something else. The Father is the fountain of deity, the one from whom the Son is eternally begotten and from whom the Spirit eternally proceeds. This is not about sequence in time — there was never a time when the Son did not exist or the Spirit was not. It is about eternal relationship. The Father loves the Son with a perfect, eternal love (John 3:35, 17:24). The Son glorifies the Father (John 17:4). The Spirit proceeds from the Father and the Son (John 15:26). When Jesus taught His disciples to pray, He said, "Our Father in heaven." He was not giving them a formula. He was inviting them into the relationship He Himself has with the Father. Through the Son, believers are adopted into this family and cry out "Abba, Father" (Romans 8:15). The Father is the one who designed salvation before the foundation of the world (Ephesians 1:4-5). He chose us, predestined us, and draws us to the Son (John 6:44). He does not sit passively while the Son does all the work. He is actively, personally involved in every detail of redemption. When you pray, you are not speaking into the dark. You are speaking to a Father who sees, hears, and cares because He has made you His child through the Son.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- John 17:24 — "Father, I desire that they also whom You gave Me may be with Me where I am, that they may behold My glory which You have given Me; for You loved Me before the foundation of the world." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 8:15 — "For you did not receive the spirit of bondage again to fear, but you received the Spirit of adoption by whom we cry out, 'Abba, Father.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- John 6:44 — "No one can come to Me unless the Father who sent Me draws him; and I will raise him up at the last day." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, each morning before you eat breakfast, pray the Lord's Prayer (Matthew 6:9-13) slowly, pausing after each phrase. Do not rush through it. Let the words "Our Father in heaven" sit in your heart before you continue.

### SUMMARY
- God the Father is the fountain of deity — the Son is eternally begotten of Him, the Spirit eternally proceeds from Him.
- Through Christ, believers are adopted and can call the Father "Abba."
- The Father is not distant — He designed salvation, draws people to Christ, and personally cares for His children.

## Lesson 3 — Who Is Jesus Christ? The Deity of the Son

### HOOK
Some people say Jesus was a great teacher but not God. Others say He became God at some point. These are not minor disagreements. If Jesus is not God, then Christianity is a lie. But if He is, then everything hinges on who He is.

### CORE TEACHING
The Bible does not leave room for a halfway Jesus. It presents Him as fully God and fully man. John 1:1 says, "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." The same Word — the Son — "became flesh and dwelt among us" in verse 14. This is the doctrine of the incarnation: God became man without ceasing to be God. Thomas, after the resurrection, fell at Jesus' feet and said, "My Lord and my God" (John 20:28). Jesus did not correct him. Paul writes that Christ "being in the form of God, did not consider it robbery to be equal with God" (Philippians 2:6). In Colossians 2:9, he says that "in Him dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily." That means Jesus did not have a piece of divinity or a measure of God's power — all of God dwells in Him. The writer of Hebrews says to the Son, "Your throne, O God, is forever and ever" (Hebrews 1:8). Why does this matter? Because only God could save us. A mere man, no matter how good, could not bear the weight of God's wrath against sin. But the Son, who is God, could. And only God could reveal the Father. Jesus said, "He who has seen Me has seen the Father" (John 14:9). We will go deeper on the work of the cross in Module 06. But here is the foundation: the One who died for you is not a prophet or a teacher. He is God the Son, the second person of the Trinity, who took on human flesh to do what only God could do.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- John 1:1,14 — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God... And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we beheld His glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father, full of grace and truth." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Philippians 2:6 — "Who, being in the form of God, did not consider it robbery to be equal with God." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- John 20:28 — "And Thomas answered and said to Him, 'My Lord and my God!'" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, open your Bible to John chapter 1. Read verses 1-18 slowly. Circle every title or description of Jesus you find. At the end of the week, count them and write them down.

### SUMMARY
- Jesus is fully God — John 1:1, Colossians 2:9, and Hebrews 1:8 all affirm His absolute deity.
- Jesus is also fully man — He took on human flesh in the incarnation without ceasing to be God.
- The deity of Christ matters because only God could reveal the Father and only God could save us.

## Lesson 4 — Who Is the Holy Spirit? The Deity and Personhood of the Spirit

### HOOK
Many believers treat the Holy Spirit like a force — something you use, like electricity. But the Bible introduces us not to a force but to a Person who can be grieved, obeyed, loved, and even lied to. This changes how we relate to Him.

### CORE TEACHING
The Holy Spirit is God. This is not a secondary doctrine. In Acts 5, Ananias lies to the Holy Spirit, and Peter says, "You have not lied to men but to God." Paul calls believers "the temple of God" in one breath and "the temple of the Holy Spirit" in the next (I Corinthians 3:16, 6:19) — the two phrases are interchangeable because the Spirit is God. But the Holy Spirit is also a person, not an impersonal influence. He has a mind and searches the deep things of God (I Corinthians 2:10). He has a will and distributes gifts "as He wills" (I Corinthians 12:11). He can be grieved (Ephesians 4:30), which only a person can experience. He speaks, commands, and directs (Acts 13:2, 16:6). Jesus called Him "another Comforter" — the Greek word *parakletos* means one called alongside to help. The word "another" means one of the same kind — as much a person as Jesus Himself. The Holy Spirit is not shy or tentative. He is God living inside every believer. He is the One who convicts the world of sin (John 16:8), who teaches and reminds us of Christ's words (John 14:26), who bears witness that we are children of God (Romans 8:16), and who seals us for the day of redemption (Ephesians 4:30). He does not draw attention to Himself — His work is always to glorify the Son and point us to Christ (John 16:14). But He is not a lesser person in the Godhead. He is co-equal, co-eternal, and co-existent with the Father and the Son. When you received Christ, the Holy Spirit took up residence in you. You are not waiting for Him to show up. He is already there.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Acts 5:3-4 — "But Peter said, 'Ananias, why has Satan filled your heart to lie to the Holy Spirit... You have not lied to men but to God.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- I Corinthians 6:19 — "Or do you not know that your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit who is in you, whom you have from God, and you are not your own?" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- John 16:13-14 — "However, when He, the Spirit of truth, has come, He will guide you into all truth... He will glorify Me, for He will take of what is Mine and declare it to you." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, when you pray, include a sentence directly addressed to the Holy Spirit. Thank Him for living in you and ask Him to guide you. Notice whether this changes how aware you are of His presence during your day.

### SUMMARY
- The Holy Spirit is fully God — lying to Him is lying to God, and your body is His temple.
- The Holy Spirit is a Person with a mind, will, and emotions — not a force or influence.
- His work is to glorify Jesus, convict the world, teach believers, and seal us for eternity.

## Lesson 5 — The Trinity in Salvation

### HOOK
There may never have been a moment in your life when you stopped to realize this: your salvation was not a solo project. Every person of the Trinity was personally involved — the Father chose you, the Son died for you, and the Spirit lives in you. Each one did what only He could do.

### CORE TEACHING
Salvation is the united work of the Triune God. The Father designed it before the foundation of the world. Ephesians 1:4 tells us He "chose us in Him before the foundation of the world." He is the source and author of the entire plan. Not one detail was left to chance. Every step was purposed by the Father in love. The Son accomplished it. He did not come to earth to give suggestions or set a good example. He came to do what no one else could do. He lived the perfect life we could not live, died the death we deserved to die, and rose again to secure our redemption. All the fullness of the Godhead dwells in Him bodily (Colossians 2:9), and His work on the cross was sufficient because He is God. We will go deeper on the work of the cross in Module 06, but here we must see that the cross was a Trinitarian event. The Spirit applied it. After Jesus ascended, the Father and the Son sent the Spirit to dwell in every believer. The Spirit is the One who convicts you of sin and draws you to repentance. He is the One who regenerates your dead heart and makes you alive in Christ (Titus 3:5). He bears witness with your spirit that you are a child of God (Romans 8:16). He seals you for the day of redemption (Ephesians 4:30). The Spirit does not merely visit you — He takes up residence in you. The Father purposed your salvation from eternity past. The Son secured it in history. The Spirit applies it in your life today. Every person of the Trinity is committed to your salvation. You did not earn it and you cannot lose it, because it was never your project to begin with.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Ephesians 1:3-6 — "Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us with every spiritual blessing... just as He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Colossians 2:9 — "For in Him dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Titus 3:5 — "Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to His mercy He saved us, through the washing of regeneration and renewing of the Holy Spirit." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Ephesians 4:30 — "And do not grieve the Holy Spirit of God, by whom you were sealed for the day of redemption." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, take a piece of paper and draw three columns labeled "Father," "Son," and "Holy Spirit." Under each, write one thing that person specifically did in your salvation. Keep the paper where you can see it as a reminder that your salvation is held by the whole Godhead.

### SUMMARY
- The Father designed salvation — He chose you before the world began.
- The Son accomplished salvation — His life, death, and resurrection secured it fully.
- The Spirit applies salvation — He regenerates, indwells, witnesses, and seals every believer.

## Lesson 6 — The Trinity in Our Daily Lives

### HOOK
It is easy to think the Trinity is a doctrine for scholars, something you believe but never use. But the Trinity is the most practical truth you will ever learn. It shapes your prayer, your relationships, your worship, and even how you handle suffering.

### CORE TEACHING
The Trinity is not abstract theology. It is the pattern for how you relate to God and others every day. When you pray, you are living out the Trinity. You pray to the Father, through the Son, in the power of the Spirit. This is not a formula to memorize but a relationship to enter. The Spirit inside you helps you pray when you do not have words (Romans 8:26). The Son intercedes for you at the right hand of the Father (Romans 8:34). The Father hears you as His child. Every prayer is a Trinitarian event. When you worship, the Trinity is at work. The Spirit opens your eyes to see the beauty of Christ, and Christ leads you to the Father. True worship is not something you manufacture — it is the Spirit showing you the Son, and the Son glorifying the Father, and you being caught up in that eternal exchange. When you love others, the Trinity is the model. The Father, Son, and Spirit have loved each other perfectly for all eternity. They are equal in worth and dignity, yet they have different roles. This is the pattern for marriage, for friendship, for church life. Equality does not mean sameness. Submission does not mean inferiority. The Son is equal to the Father yet submitted to Him. When you suffer, the Trinity is your anchor. The Father is sovereign over your suffering. The Son sympathizes with your weakness because He was tempted in every way as a man (Hebrews 4:15). The Spirit strengthens you and prays for you. You are not alone in your pain — the whole Godhead is with you. The Trinity is not a puzzle to solve. It is a foundation to build your life on.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Romans 8:26-27 — "Likewise the Spirit also helps in our weaknesses. For we do not know what we should pray for as we ought, but the Spirit Himself makes intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 8:34 — "Who is he who condemns? It is Christ who died, and furthermore is also risen, who is even at the right hand of God, who also makes intercession for us." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Hebrews 4:15 — "For we do not have a High Priest who cannot sympathize with our weaknesses, but was in all points tempted as we are, yet without sin." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, before you pray each night, pause and say aloud: "Father, I come to You through Your Son Jesus, in the power of the Holy Spirit." Then pray. Do this every night for seven days and notice if your awareness of God changes.

### SUMMARY
- Every prayer is Trinitarian — you pray to the Father through the Son by the Spirit.
- The Trinity models relationships — equality with different roles, submission without inferiority.
- In suffering, the whole Godhead is at work — Father sovereign, Son sympathetic, Spirit strengthening.

## Lesson 7 — Living in the Light of the Triune God

### HOOK
If the God who created the universe exists in perfect love within Himself, then love is not something God does — it is who He is. And if you are made in His image, then your whole life was designed to be lived in love — toward God and toward others.

### CORE TEACHING
The Trinity is not the end of theology. It is the beginning of everything. If God is Triune, then relationship is at the center of reality. You were created for relationship because you were made in the image of a relational God. You were saved for relationship — the Father draws you, the Son buys you, the Spirit lives in you so that you can know God personally. And you will spend eternity in relationship. The Trinity tells you that God has no needs. He was not incomplete before He made you. He did not create you because He was lonely or lacking. He created you out of the overflow of His own fullness and love. This changes why you exist. You are not here to fill a gap in God. You are here to enjoy Him, glorify Him, and be satisfied in Him. The Trinity tells you that you are safe. Your salvation was not a backup plan or a risky experiment. It was designed by the Father, accomplished by the Son, and sealed by the Spirit. The same Spirit who raised Jesus from the dead lives in you (Romans 8:11). The same Son who died for you is praying for you. The same Father who chose you will never let you go. You are held by the Triune God. The Trinity tells you that love is the highest thing. Before the world existed, love was happening between the Father, Son, and Spirit. When you love God and love your neighbor, you are living the way the universe was designed. You are reflecting the image of the Triune God. Everything — worship, obedience, relationships, mission, suffering, hope — comes back to this one truth: God is love, not because love is something He gives, but because love is something He is. And you were made to know Him.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Romans 11:36 — "For of Him and through Him and to Him are all things, to whom be glory forever. Amen." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 8:11 — "But if the Spirit of Him who raised Jesus from the dead dwells in you, He who raised Christ from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies through His Spirit who dwells in you." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- I John 4:8 — "He who does not love does not know God, for God is love." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write a one-page letter to God thanking each person of the Trinity for what they have done for you. Address the Father first, then the Son, then the Holy Spirit. Keep the letter in your Bible and read it again in one month.

### SUMMARY
- If God is Triune, then relationship is at the center of all reality — you were made for relationship.
- Your salvation is secure because the whole Godhead — Father, Son, and Spirit — is committed to it.
- Love is not just a command — it is the eternal nature of God, and you were created to reflect it.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
