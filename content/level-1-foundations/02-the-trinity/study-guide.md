# Study Guide: The Trinity

**Level:** Level 1 — Foundations
**Module:** 02 — The Trinity

---

## 1. Core Definition

The Trinity is the biblical doctrine that the one true God exists eternally as three distinct persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — who share the same divine nature or essence. They are not three gods (tritheism), nor is God one person who appears in three different forms (modalism). They are co-equal, co-eternal, and co-existent, dwelling in perfect unity and unbroken love. The word "Trinity" comes from the Latin *trinitas* ("threefold"), first used by Tertullian to describe what Scripture consistently teaches.

---

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

### 2.1 God Is One
Deuteronomy 6:4 — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one" — is the foundation stone of biblical faith. The Hebrew word for "one" (*echad*) often signifies a compound unity (as in Genesis 2:24 — "they shall become one flesh"). Scripture repeatedly affirms that there is only one true God (Isaiah 43:10, 45:18; I Corinthians 8:6). [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.2 God Is Three Persons
At Jesus' baptism (Mark 1:10-11), all three persons appear simultaneously and distinctly: the Son is baptized, the Spirit descends like a dove, and the Father speaks from heaven. In John 14:16-17, Jesus prays to the Father, who gives the Spirit. The Great Commission (Matthew 28:19) commands baptism "in the name (singular) of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.3 The Deity of the Son
John 1:1 declares that the Word (the Son) was with God and was God. John 1:14 confirms the Word became flesh. Thomas calls Jesus "my Lord and my God" (John 20:28). Paul writes that Christ existed in the form of God and was equal with God (Philippians 2:6), and that in Him dwells all the fullness of Deity bodily (Colossians 2:9). [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.4 The Deity of the Holy Spirit
Ananias lied to the Holy Spirit and was told he had lied to God (Acts 5:3-4). Believers are called the temple of God (I Corinthians 3:16) and the temple of the Holy Spirit (I Corinthians 6:19). The Holy Spirit has a mind (Romans 8:27), a will (I Corinthians 12:11), can be grieved (Ephesians 4:30), and blasphemed against (Matthew 12:31). [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.5 The Trinity in Salvation
The Father designed salvation and governs every detail. The Son accomplished it through His life, death, and resurrection. The Spirit applies it by dwelling in believers, sealing them for the day of redemption (Ephesians 4:30). All three Persons unite in the salvation of a sinner. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.6 Practical Implications
The Trinity reveals that God is relational by nature — love has existed eternally among the three Persons. God has no need; He did not create out of loneliness. The Trinity models equality with different roles, showing that submission is not inferiority.

---

## 3. Common Misconceptions

- **Tritheism** — believing the three Persons are three separate gods. The Bible clearly teaches one God.
- **Modalism** — believing God is one Person who wears three different masks or appears in three modes at different times. Scripture shows all three Persons existing and acting simultaneously.
- **"Can't be explained"** — The Trinity is a mystery but it is revealed truth. The Bible does explain it; our inability to fully comprehend is not the same as it being unexplainable.
- **Inadequate illustrations** — Water (liquid/ice/steam) suggests modalism. Egg, apple, or three-leaf clover illustrations distort the doctrine. Better to affirm the truth without misleading illustration.

---

## 4. Practical Application Angles

- Worship all three Persons equally — never diminish one in honor of another
- Let the Trinity shape relationships — equality with different roles
- Rest in the security of Trinitarian salvation — the whole Godhead is committed to your redemption
- Pray to the Father through the Son in the power of the Spirit
- Honor the Holy Spirit as a Person — do not treat Him as a force or influence
- Let perfect Trinitarian love be the model for Christian fellowship

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Trinity | The one true God existing eternally as three distinct Persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit |
| Triune | Three-in-one; having the nature of a Trinity |
| Tritheism | The heresy of believing in three separate gods |
| Modalism | The heresy of believing God is one Person who appears in different modes |
| Hypostatic Union | The union of Christ's divine and human natures in one Person |
| Echad | Hebrew word for "one" that often denotes a compound unity |
| Deity | The state of being God; divinity |
| Logos | Greek term for "Word" — the Son as God's self-expression |
| Begotten | Term describing the Son's eternal relationship to the Father |
| Procession | Term describing the Spirit's eternal relationship to the Father and the Son |
