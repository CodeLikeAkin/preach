# Flashcards: The Authority of Scripture

## 10 Question & Answer Pairs

### 1
**Q: What does it mean that Scripture is "God-breathed"?**
A: It means Scripture originates from God — He is its ultimate source. The human authors wrote in their own styles and personalities, but the Holy Spirit guided them so that what they wrote is exactly what God intended (2 Timothy 3:16).

### 2
**Q: How many books, authors, languages, and continents make up the Bible?**
A: 66 books, over 40 authors, 3 languages (Hebrew, Greek, Aramaic), 3 continents (Asia, Africa, Europe), written over approximately 1,500 years.

### 3
**Q: Why is it significant that the Bible was written by eyewitnesses?**
A: Eyewitness testimony is the strongest form of evidence in any investigation. Peter, John, and the other apostles wrote about what they personally saw, heard, and touched — not about myths or secondhand stories.

### 4
**Q: What is falsifiability, and why does it matter for the Bible?**
A: A claim is falsifiable if it can be proven false. Paul made the resurrection falsifiable by naming over 500 eyewitnesses who were still alive — anyone could go and interview them. The claim was testable, and it passed the test.

### 5
**Q: Why is the "telephone game" objection to Bible translation incorrect?**
A: Translators of modern Bibles do not translate from earlier translations. They go back directly to the Hebrew, Greek, and Aramaic manuscripts. Every translation is a fresh translation from the source languages, not a chain of translations.

### 6
**Q: Why is the conspiracy theory about overzealous monks changing the Bible historically impossible?**
A: It would require monks to alter over 6,000 Greek manuscripts, all early translations (Syriac, Coptic, Latin), and all the writings of the early church fathers — in multiple languages — without anyone noticing. This is impossible.

### 7
**Q: How many New Testament manuscripts do we have, and how does that compare to other ancient documents?**
A: We have over 6,000 Greek New Testament manuscripts or portions, with the earliest within decades of the originals. By comparison, Caesar's Gallic Wars has about 10 manuscripts (earliest 900 years later), and Aristotle's Poetics has about 5 (earliest 1,400 years later).

### 8
**Q: Give one example of a specific Old Testament prophecy fulfilled in Jesus Christ.**
A: Psalm 22, written 1,000 years before crucifixion existed, describes a man whose hands and feet are pierced, whose garments are divided by lot, and who cries out "My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?" — all fulfilled at the cross.

### 9
**Q: How does the appropriate method for investigating the Bible differ from the scientific method?**
A: The scientific method requires observation, measurement, and repetition — which cannot be applied to past events. The Bible is investigated through the evidentiary method, like a courtroom: examining eyewitness testimony, corroboration, internal consistency, and external evidence.

### 10
**Q: What is the sufficiency of Scripture?**
A: The sufficiency of Scripture means the Bible contains everything necessary for salvation and godly living. It "thoroughly equips" the believer for every good work (2 Timothy 3:16-17). Nothing else is required.
