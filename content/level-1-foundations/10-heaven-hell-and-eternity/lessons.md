# Heaven, Hell & Eternity — 7 Lessons

---

## Lesson 1: What Happens After Death?

### Hook
Everyone standing at a graveside has asked the same question: Is this all there is? When the last breath leaves a body, does the person simply cease to exist, or is there something more? Every culture in every age has believed in life after death, and the Bible gives us a clear answer.

### Core Teaching
Death is not the end. The Bible teaches that every person who has ever lived will exist forever. The moment a believer dies, they are immediately in the presence of the Lord. Paul said that to be absent from the body is to be present with the Lord. This is the intermediate state — the period between physical death and the final resurrection. For the believer, it is a state of conscious fellowship with Christ, not sleep or unconsciousness. For the unbeliever, it is a state of conscious separation, awaiting final judgment. Jesus told the story of a rich man and a beggar named Lazarus to illustrate this reality. Both died. The rich man found himself in torment, Lazarus in comfort with Abraham. Between them was a great chasm that could not be crossed. The rich man wanted to warn his five brothers, but he was told they already had Moses and the Prophets. The point was clear: the time to respond to God is in this life, not after death. Think of death like moving from one country to another. When you board an international flight, you leave the familiar behind. Customs, language, currency, and climate all change. But you do not cease to exist. You simply begin living in a different place. Death is the flight every person takes. Where you arrive depends on whose passport you carry.

### Key Scriptures
- "For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, so that each one may be recompensed for his deeds in the body, according to what he has done, whether good or bad." — 2 Corinthians 5:10 [VERIFY]
- "We are of good courage, I say, and prefer rather to be absent from the body and to be at home with the Lord." — 2 Corinthians 5:8 [VERIFY]
- "And inasmuch as it is appointed for men to die once and after this comes judgment." — Hebrews 9:27 [VERIFY]
- "Now the poor man died and was carried away by the angels to Abraham's bosom; and the rich man also died and was buried. In Hades he lifted up his eyes, being in torment." — Luke 16:22-23 [VERIFY]

### Practical Application
This week, write down three things you are anxious about. Next to each one, write the words: "This is temporary; eternity is real." Then ask yourself: Is my anxiety about this temporary thing robbing me of peace about the eternal reality? Let the certainty of what comes after death shrink your fears about what happens before it.

### Summary
- Death is a transition, not the end — every person will exist forever.
- Believers who die are immediately present with the Lord in conscious fellowship.
- The time to respond to God is in this life; after death comes judgment.

## Lesson 2: The Resurrection of the Dead

### Hook
Imagine a seed buried in the dark ground. To anyone watching, it looks like the end — dead, buried, gone. But then something impossible happens. A shoot breaks through the soil, then a stalk, then leaves, then fruit. The seed did not stay dead. It was transformed into something far more glorious than what was buried.

### Core Teaching
The resurrection of Jesus Christ is the cornerstone of the Christian hope. Because He rose from the dead, all who belong to Him will also rise. Paul wrote that if Christ has not been raised, our preaching is useless and our faith is useless. But Christ has been raised, and He is the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep. Firstfruits means the first sheaf of the harvest, guaranteeing that the full harvest is coming. Christ's resurrection guarantees our resurrection. There are two aspects to this resurrection. The first resurrection is spiritual — when you are born again, you are made alive in Christ. You were dead in your trespasses and sins, but God made you alive together with Christ and seated you with Him in heavenly places. You have already passed from death to life. The second resurrection is physical — at the end of the age, when Christ returns, the dead in Christ will rise. Their bodies will be raised imperishable, glorified, and immortal. This is not a vague spiritual survival of the soul. It is a bodily resurrection. The same power that raised Jesus from the grave will transform our lowly bodies to be like His glorious body. Paul describes it this way: what is sown perishable is raised imperishable. What is sown in dishonor is raised in glory. What is sown in weakness is raised in power. What is sown a natural body is raised a spiritual body. Think of a caterpillar that becomes a butterfly. The caterpillar does not simply cease to exist. It is transformed into something far more beautiful, far more capable, and far more free than it ever was before. That is what the resurrection will be for every believer. The body you have now is like the caterpillar. The body you will receive is like the butterfly.

### Key Scriptures
- "But now Christ has been raised from the dead, the first fruits of those who are asleep." — 1 Corinthians 15:20 [VERIFY]
- "For as in Adam all die, so also in Christ all will be made alive." — 1 Corinthians 15:22 [VERIFY]
- "Behold, I tell you a mystery; we will not all sleep, but we will all be changed, in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trumpet; for the trumpet will sound, and the dead will be raised imperishable, and we will be changed." — 1 Corinthians 15:51-52 [VERIFY]
- "For the Lord Himself will descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel and with the trumpet of God, and the dead in Christ will rise first." — 1 Thessalonians 4:16 [VERIFY]

### Practical Application
This week, if you know a believer who has died, take time to thank God for the resurrection. If no one comes to mind, visit a cemetery and read 1 Corinthians 15:50-58 aloud. Let the promise of the resurrection replace any fear of death you may carry.

### Summary
- Christ's resurrection guarantees the resurrection of all who belong to Him.
- The first resurrection is spiritual (new birth); the second is physical (glorified bodies).
- Believers will receive imperishable, glorified bodies like Christ's resurrection body.

## Lesson 3: The Reality of Hell

### Hook
A doctor who knows a patient has a fatal disease but refuses to tell them is not kind — he is cruel. The most loving thing he can do is tell the truth while there is still time to act. Jesus spoke more about hell than anyone else in Scripture, not because He enjoyed the subject, but because He wanted people to escape it.

### Core Teaching
Hell is a real place of eternal conscious punishment. Jesus described it as a place of unquenchable fire, outer darkness, and weeping and gnashing of teeth. He spoke of it as a place prepared for the devil and his angels, but one that anyone who rejects God will share. The existence of hell is not a contradiction of God's love. It is an expression of His justice. A judge who lets guilty criminals go free is not a loving judge — he is an unjust one. God is perfectly good, perfectly holy, and perfectly just. He cannot ignore sin or pretend it does not matter. Every wrong ever committed must be dealt with. For those who trust in Christ, their sin was dealt with on the cross. Jesus bore the full punishment in their place. For those who reject Christ, they must bear that punishment themselves — and that is what hell is. Hell is not a place God sends people against their will. It is the destination people choose when they reject the only way out. The rich man in Jesus' story did not end up in hell because God was cruel. He ended up there because he had ignored God's warnings and rejected God's provision during his life on earth. The Bible says that anyone whose name is not written in the Book of Life is thrown into the lake of fire. But the gospel is that God does not want anyone to perish. He wants everyone to come to repentance. He provided the way of escape through His Son. Hell is real, but it is avoidable. Picture a man drowning in the ocean. He can see the rescue boat approaching, but he refuses to grab the rope thrown to him. His drowning is not the rescue team's fault. The rope was there. The boat came. He chose to reject it.

### Key Scriptures
- "Then He will also say to those on His left, 'Depart from Me, accursed ones, into the eternal fire which has been prepared for the devil and his angels.'" — Matthew 25:41 [VERIFY]
- "And these will go away into eternal punishment, but the righteous into eternal life." — Matthew 25:46 [VERIFY]
- "If your hand causes you to stumble, cut it off; it is better for you to enter life crippled, than, having your two hands, to go into hell, into the unquenchable fire." — Mark 9:43 [VERIFY]
- "And if anyone's name was not found written in the book of life, he was thrown into the lake of fire." — Revelation 20:15 [VERIFY]

### Practical Application
This week, ask God to give you a holy burden for one person you know who does not know Christ. Write their name on a card and keep it where you will see it every day. Pray for them by name each day, asking God to open their eyes before it is too late.

### Summary
- Hell is a real place of eternal conscious punishment, and Jesus spoke about it more than anyone else.
- Hell is the just consequence of rejecting God's offer of salvation through Christ.
- Hell is avoidable — God has provided the way of escape through His Son.

### Next Lesson Preview

## Lesson 4: The Reality of Heaven

### Hook
Think of the best day of your life — the moment you felt most alive, most joyful, most at peace. Multiply that joy by a thousand, a million, an infinity. Remove every trace of pain, disappointment, and fear. That is a faint shadow of what heaven will be like.

### Core Teaching
Heaven is a real place. Jesus told His disciples, "In My Father's house are many dwelling places. I go to prepare a place for you." He was not speaking metaphorically. Heaven is the dwelling place of God, and those who are redeemed will live there with Him forever. What will heaven be like? The Bible describes it as a city of unimaginable beauty — streets of gold, gates of pearl, foundations of precious stones. But these physical descriptions point to something far greater. The primary glory of heaven is not the scenery; it is the presence of God. Revelation tells us that there will be no temple in the heavenly city because the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are its temple. There will be no need for sun or moon because the glory of God gives it light. Every tear will be wiped away. There will be no more death, mourning, crying, or pain. The first things will have passed away. But heaven is not a place of passive rest. Believers will reign with Christ. They will serve Him. They will worship Him face to face. Paul wrote that those who endure will also reign with Him. This is not sitting on clouds playing harps. It is active, glorious, meaningful existence in the presence of God. Think of a child who has been living in a dark, cramped room. One day, the door opens, and she steps out into a beautiful garden — sunlight, flowers, open space, and her father waiting with open arms. She does not miss the dark room. She does not want to go back. She is home. That is a glimpse of what it means to enter heaven.

### Key Scriptures
- "Do not let your heart be troubled; believe in God, believe also in Me. In My Father's house are many dwelling places; if it were not so, I would have told you; for I go to prepare a place for you." — John 14:1-2 [VERIFY]
- "And He will wipe away every tear from their eyes; and there will no longer be any death; there will no longer be any mourning, or crying, or pain; the first things have passed away." — Revelation 21:4 [VERIFY]
- "The city has no need of the sun or of the moon to shine on it, for the glory of God has illuminated it, and its lamp is the Lamb." — Revelation 21:23 [VERIFY]
- "Blessed and holy is the one who has a part in the first resurrection; over these the second death has no power, but they will be priests of God and of Christ and will reign with Him for a thousand years." — Revelation 20:6 [VERIFY]

### Practical Application
This week, take five minutes each morning to close your eyes and picture one aspect of heaven from Revelation 21 — the absence of tears, the presence of God, the joy of seeing Christ face to face. End each time by saying: "This is my future because of Jesus." Let this hope set the tone for your day.

### Summary
- Heaven is a real place where God dwells, described in Scripture as a city of unimaginable beauty.
- The primary glory of heaven is the direct presence of God, with no more death, pain, or sorrow.
- Believers will not be passive — they will reign with Christ, serve Him, and worship Him forever.

## Lesson 5: The Final Judgment

### Hook
Every person has had moments they wish they could undo — words spoken in anger, decisions made in selfishness, secrets they hope never come to light. The Bible tells us that one day, every thought, word, and deed will be brought into the light. But for those in Christ, that day holds no terror.

### Core Teaching
The Bible teaches that there are two judgments. The first is the Judgment Seat of Christ, also called the Bema, where believers give an account of their lives. This judgment is not about whether they are saved — that was settled at the cross. It is about reward. Paul wrote that we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ so that each one may be recompensed for his deeds in the body, according to what he has done, whether good or bad. The works of the believer will be tested by fire. Some works will survive and bring reward. Other works will be burned up, and though the believer himself will be saved, it will be as one escaping through the flames. The second is the Great White Throne Judgment, where unbelievers are judged according to their deeds. Revelation describes this scene with breathtaking gravity. The dead, great and small, stand before the throne. Books are opened. Another book is opened — the Book of Life. The dead are judged according to what is written in the books, by what they have done. Anyone whose name is not found written in the Book of Life is thrown into the lake of fire. This is the second death. The standard of judgment is perfect righteousness. No one can meet that standard on their own. The purpose of this judgment is not to see if people are good enough — no one is. The purpose is to demonstrate that every person who stands there has rejected the only righteous One who could save them. For believers, the judgment is different. Because Christ has already borne their punishment, they will not face condemnation. Their names are written in the Book of Life. They appear before Christ not to determine their destiny but to receive what they have built for His kingdom. Imagine two students taking the same exam. One has already been accepted into the university — the exam is about placement and honors, not admission. The other has not been accepted yet — the exam determines everything. That is the difference between the two judgments.

### Key Scriptures
- "For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, so that each one may be recompensed for his deeds in the body, according to what he has done, whether good or bad." — 2 Corinthians 5:10 [VERIFY]
- "But when you give a reception, invite the poor, the crippled, the lame, the blind, and you will be blessed, since they do not have the means to repay you; for you will be repaid at the resurrection of the righteous." — Luke 14:13-14 [VERIFY]
- "Then I saw a great white throne and Him who sat upon it, from whose presence earth and heaven fled away, and no place was found for them." — Revelation 20:11 [VERIFY]
- "And if anyone's name was not found written in the book of life, he was thrown into the lake of fire." — Revelation 20:15 [VERIFY]

### Practical Application
This week, do one thing that will count at the judgment seat of Christ. Serve someone who cannot repay you. Give quietly to a person in need. Share the gospel with one person. Do it knowing that Christ sees, and that He will reward what is done for His glory.

### Summary
- Believers face the Judgment Seat of Christ for reward, not condemnation — salvation is already secure.
- Unbelievers face the Great White Throne Judgment, where their works demonstrate their need for a Savior they rejected.
- The only name that secures acquittal at the final judgment is the name written in the Book of Life.

## Lesson 6: Eternal Life

### Hook
If someone offered you a pill that would let you live forever on earth with the same body, the same struggles, and the same disappointments, would you take it? Living forever in a broken world is not good news. But eternal life is not just endless existence. It is something radically different and infinitely better.

### Core Teaching
Eternal life is more than living forever. The Bible defines it this way: "This is eternal life, that they may know You, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom You have sent." Eternal life is knowing God. It is not primarily about duration; it is about relationship. A person could exist forever without experiencing eternal life. Eternal life is the very life of God living in and through the believer. First John says, "God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. He who has the Son has the life; he who does not have the Son does not have the life." Eternal life is not something you get when you die. It is something you receive the moment you believe in Jesus. Jesus said, "Truly, truly, I say to you, he who hears My word and believes Him who sent Me has eternal life, and does not come into judgment, but has passed from death to life." Notice the verb tense: has. Present tense. Right now. Eternal life begins the moment you are born again. It is not interrupted by physical death. It simply continues in a fuller, more glorious form in heaven. This life is a gift. It is not earned by good behavior or religious effort. It is received through faith in Christ. And because it is God's own life, it cannot be lost or taken away. It is eternal. It endures forever because its source is the eternal God Himself. Think of a branch connected to a vine. The branch does not produce its own life. The life flows from the vine into the branch, and the branch bears fruit because of that connection. Cut the branch off, and it withers. Stay connected, and the life never stops flowing. Eternal life is being connected to Jesus, the true vine, and receiving His life into yours.

### Key Scriptures
- "This is eternal life, that they may know You, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom You have sent." — John 17:3 [VERIFY]
- "Truly, truly, I say to you, he who hears My word and believes Him who sent Me, has eternal life, and does not come into judgment, but has passed from death to life." — John 5:24 [VERIFY]
- "And the testimony is this, that God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. He who has the Son has the life; he who does not have the Son does not have the life." — 1 John 5:11-12 [VERIFY]
- "For the wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." — Romans 6:23 [VERIFY]

### Practical Application
This week, set aside ten minutes to sit quietly and pray using John 17:3 as your guide. Say to God: "Father, I want to know You. I want to know Jesus Christ whom You sent." Then be still and let the reality of knowing God settle in your heart. Eternal life is not a distant promise — it is a present relationship to enjoy today.

### Summary
- Eternal life is knowing God and Jesus Christ — a relationship, not just endless existence.
- Eternal life is a present possession for every believer, beginning at the moment of faith.
- It is a free gift received through Christ, not earned by good works.

## Lesson 7: Living in Light of Eternity

### Hook
Jonathan Edwards prayed, "God, stamp eternity on my eyeballs." He wanted to see everything through the lens of forever. If you knew you had only one week left to live, how would you spend it? The fact is, none of us knows how much time we have. But we can choose to live every day as if eternity matters — because it does.

### Core Teaching
The apostle Paul gives us two motivations for living in light of eternity, and they work together like the sun and the moon. The lesser light is the fear of judgment. Paul said that knowing the fear of the Lord, he persuaded men. The reality of standing before Christ and giving an account is a sobering motivation. It purifies how we live, what we pursue, and how we spend our time. But the greater light is the love of Christ. Paul wrote that the love of Christ controls us. Not fear of punishment, not desire for reward — but the overwhelming, bewildering reality that Christ loved us and gave Himself for us. This is the greater motivation because it is not about us. It is about Him. When you grasp that the Son of God loved you and died for you, living for yourself becomes unthinkable. The only rational response is to live for Him who died and rose again. Living in light of eternity means recognizing that this life is brief. James says it is a vapor that appears for a little while and then vanishes away. Every person who has ever lived has felt the shortness of life. The difference for the believer is that this brief life is preparation for an endless one. What you do in the next seventy years determines your experience of the next seventy billion. The Puritans used to say, "For every inward glance, give ten long gazes upon Christ." Do not be absorbed with examining your own spiritual performance. Look at Christ. Fix your eyes on Jesus, the author and perfecter of faith. When you see Him clearly, everything else comes into focus. Imagine a runner in a marathon. He does not look at his feet or worry about every step. He fixes his eyes on the finish line. The finish line pulls him forward. It gives him strength when his legs burn and his lungs ache. Christ is the finish line. Fix your eyes on Him, and run.

### Key Scriptures
- "For the love of Christ controls us, having concluded this, that one died for all, therefore all died; and He died for all, so that they who live might no longer live for themselves, but for Him who died and rose again on their behalf." — 2 Corinthians 5:14-15 [VERIFY]
- "Therefore, knowing the fear of the Lord, we persuade men." — 2 Corinthians 5:11 [VERIFY]
- "Yet you do not know what your life will be like tomorrow. You are just a vapor that appears for a little while and then vanishes away." — James 4:14 [VERIFY]
- "Fixing our eyes on Jesus, the author and perfecter of faith, who for the joy set before Him endured the cross, despising the shame, and has sat down at the right hand of the throne of God." — Hebrews 12:2 [VERIFY]

### Practical Application
Start a daily five-minute "eternity pause" this week. Set an alarm for the same time each day. When it goes off, stop whatever you are doing. Ask yourself: "If I died today, would I be ready? Is what I am doing right now building anything that will last forever?" Then thank Christ for securing your eternity and ask Him to help you live the next hour for His glory.

### Summary
- The fear of judgment and the love of Christ both motivate us to live for what matters — but the love of Christ is the greater motivation.
- This life is a vapor — brief and fleeting — and what we do in it determines our experience of eternity.
- Fix your eyes on Jesus, not on yourself, and let the reality of eternity shape every decision.
