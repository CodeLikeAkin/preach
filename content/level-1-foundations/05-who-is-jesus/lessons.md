# Lessons: Who Is Jesus?
## 7 Lessons for Discipleship

---

## Lesson 1: The Word Was God

**Hook:**
"A religious teacher who claims to be God is either a madman, a liar, or the Lord. There is no fourth option."

**Key Scripture:** John 1:1 — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Jesus Christ is fully God. This is not a later invention of the church but the clear teaching of Scripture from the beginning. John opens his Gospel with an unmistakable declaration: the Word (Jesus) is eternal, distinct from the Father, and fully divine. To deny Christ's deity is to deny Christianity itself.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- John 1:1-3 — The Word was God
- John 8:58 — "Before Abraham was, I AM"
- John 10:30 — "I and the Father are one"
- John 20:28 — Thomas calls Jesus "My Lord and my God"
- Romans 9:5 — "God over all, blessed forever"
- Colossians 2:9 — "All the fullness of the Deity dwells in bodily form"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Jesus claimed to be God, and those who heard Him understood exactly what He meant. When He said "Before Abraham was, I AM," the Jews picked up stones to kill Him for blasphemy. When He said "I and the Father are one," they tried to stone Him again. He accepted worship, forgave sins, and claimed authority over the Sabbath — all things that belong to God alone. C.S. Lewis framed the logical options: a man who claims to be God must be Lord, liar, or lunatic. His sinless life and resurrection prove He is Lord. The evidence for Christ's deity is overwhelming: fulfilled prophecy, divine titles, divine works (creation, sustaining, forgiving, judging), and the testimony of the apostles who were eyewitnesses of His glory.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
If Jesus is God, then He is worthy of your entire life. He is not a consultant to consult when you need advice — He is the Lord who deserves your absolute allegiance. Worship Him. Trust Him. Obey Him. Every other question in your life is secondary to the question of who Jesus is.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ did not become God — He has always been God. He is the eternal Word who was with God and was God from the beginning. He did not come to show us a way to God — He came as God Himself, dwelling among us.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Because Jesus is God, His promises are sure. His death has infinite value. His resurrection is definitive. His return is certain. The One who holds the universe together holds you in His hands.

## Lesson 2: The Word Became Flesh

**Hook:**
"The Creator of galaxies became a fetus. This is the scandal of the incarnation."

**Key Scripture:** John 1:14 — "And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we have seen His glory, glory as of the only Son from the Father, full of grace and truth."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Jesus Christ is fully man. The incarnation — God becoming man — is not a temporary appearance or a costume. The eternal Son of God took on a complete human nature: body, soul, and spirit. He became truly human while remaining truly God.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- John 1:14 — The Word became flesh
- Luke 2:52 — Jesus increased in wisdom and stature
- Hebrews 2:14-18 — He shared in flesh and blood; made like His brethren
- Hebrews 4:15 — Tempted in all things as we are, yet without sin
- 1 Timothy 2:5 — "One mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The humanity of Christ is not optional — it is essential to salvation. Jesus was born of a virgin, conceived by the Holy Spirit, so that He would be truly human yet without inherited sin. He experienced hunger, thirst, fatigue, sorrow, joy, and pain. He wept at Lazarus's tomb. He was angry at the moneychangers. He was troubled in Gethsemane. He suffered and died a real death. His human nature was real, not simulated. Yet He was without sin. He was tempted — genuinely tempted — but did not sin. This means He can sympathize with our weaknesses because He has experienced them.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Because Jesus became man, you can approach Him with confidence. He understands your struggles, your pain, your temptations. He is not a distant, unfeeling deity. He has been where you are. When you are weary, He knows. When you are tempted, He knows. When you suffer, He knows. Go to Him.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
The same Christ who spoke the universe into existence grew tired and sat by a well. The One who holds all things together felt the sting of rejection. The eternal Word became flesh not to observe our condition but to enter it and redeem it.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Christ's humanity is permanent. He is forever the God-man. In His glorified body, He intercedes for us at the right hand of the Father. Our mediator is one of us.

## Lesson 3: The Hypostatic Union

**Hook:**
"Jesus is not fifty percent God and fifty percent man. He is one hundred percent God and one hundred percent man — one person with two natures."

**Key Scripture:** Colossians 2:9 — "For in Him all the fullness of the Deity dwells in bodily form."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
The hypostatic union is the union of Christ's divine and human natures in one person. Jesus is not two persons (a divine person and a human person) but one person with two complete natures. These natures are united without confusion, change, division, or separation.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Colossians 2:9 — All the fullness of the Deity dwells in bodily form
- Philippians 2:5-8 — Christ existed in the form of God but emptied Himself to take the form of a servant
- Hebrews 1:3 — He is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of His nature
- 1 Timothy 3:16 — "God was manifest in the flesh"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
The hypostatic union is a mystery that transcends full human comprehension, but it is essential to understand. As God, Christ possesses all divine attributes: omniscience, omnipotence, omnipresence, eternality. As man, He experienced genuine human limitations. The two natures are not mixed (like coffee and cream) but are united in the one person of the Son. When Jesus was tired, His divine nature was not exhausted. When He said "I thirst," His divine nature did not experience dehydration. His human nature experienced these things while His divine nature remained fully divine. The "emptying" (kenosis) of Philippians 2 was not a laying aside of deity but a voluntary non-use of divine prerogatives for the sake of incarnation.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
The hypostatic union is not an abstract doctrine — it is the foundation of your salvation. Because Christ is God, His sacrifice is infinite in value. Because Christ is man, His sacrifice counts for humanity. Only a mediator who is both God and man can reconcile you to God. Do not try to reduce Christ to what you can understand. Worship the mystery.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
In Christ, heaven and earth meet. The infinite and the finite are united. The Creator and the creature are joined in one person forever. He is uniquely qualified to be our Savior.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
The God-man is our advocate. He pleads for us as one who is both our Lord and our brother. He sits at the right hand of the Father as the one who has reconciled us.

## Lesson 4: The Trilemma

**Hook:**
"Jesus never intended to leave us the option of calling Him a good teacher. Good teachers don't claim to be God."

**Key Scripture:** John 14:6 — "Jesus said to him, 'I am the way, and the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father except through me.'"

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Jesus claimed to be the only way to God. He did not present Himself as one option among many. He claimed exclusive authority, exclusive knowledge of the Father, and exclusive power to save. These claims force us to a decision.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- John 14:6 — "I am the way, and the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father except through me"
- John 10:9 — "I am the door; if anyone enters through me, he will be saved"
- Acts 4:12 — "There is salvation in no one else"
- John 3:36 — "Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life; whoever does not obey the Son shall not see life"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
C.S. Lewis articulated the trilemma: Jesus's claims leave no room for neutrality. He is either Lord (who He claimed to be), Liar (knowingly deceived others), or Lunatic (sincerely deluded). He cannot merely be a good teacher while claiming to be God — a good teacher does not teach what is false about his own identity. The trilemma shows us that the modern view of Jesus — a wise moral teacher whose teachings we admire while rejecting His deity — is logically untenable. Jesus did not say "I will show you a way" — He said "I AM the way." Either He is who He claimed to be, or He is neither good nor a teacher.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
You cannot have Jesus on your own terms. You cannot accept His moral teaching while rejecting His divine authority. You cannot call Him "good" if you do not call Him "Lord." The question Jesus asked His disciples is the question He asks you: "Who do you say that I am?" Your answer determines everything.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Peter answered correctly: "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God." Jesus did not correct him. He blessed him. The confession of Christ's identity is the rock on which the church is built.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
If Jesus is Lord, then He is able to save completely. The One who claimed to be the way, the truth, and the life is exactly who He said He is. Trusting Him is not a gamble — it is the only sure foundation.

## Lesson 5: Christ the Creator

**Hook:**
"The hands that were nailed to the cross are the hands that hung the stars in place."

**Key Scripture:** Colossians 1:16 — "For by Him all things were created, in heaven and on earth, visible and invisible, whether thrones or dominions or rulers or authorities — all things were created through Him and for Him."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Jesus Christ is the Creator of all things. He is not a part of creation — He is the Creator. Every angel, every star, every atom, every life was made by Him and for Him. He is before all things, and in Him all things hold together.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- John 1:3 — "All things were made through Him, and without Him was not anything made that was made"
- Colossians 1:16-17 — All things created through Him, for Him; He is before all things; He holds all things together
- Hebrews 1:2-3 — God created the world through the Son; He upholds all things by His word
- 1 Corinthians 8:6 — "One Lord, Jesus Christ, through whom are all things"

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Christ's role as Creator establishes His absolute sovereignty. He is not merely the best among created beings — He is the Creator of all beings. This includes every spiritual power: thrones, dominions, rulers, authorities. Every angel, every demon, every human authority, every cosmic force was created by Christ and is subject to Him. He is not just the head of the church — He is the Lord of the universe. His role as Sustainer means that creation depends on Him moment by moment. If He withdrew His sustaining power, the universe would instantly cease to exist. This means that nothing in creation operates independently of Him.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
The One you worship is the One who created the universe. Your problems are not too big for Him — He spoke galaxies into being. Your life is not insignificant — the Creator of all things knows you by name and holds your life in His hands. You can trust the One who holds the universe together to hold your life together.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
The Creator became a creature. The One who made all things entered His own creation to redeem it. The power that created the universe was the power that raised Christ from the dead — and that same power is at work in you.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Because Christ is the Creator, He is also the Redeemer. The One who made you is able to remake you. The One who spoke light into existence can shine in your dark heart.

## Lesson 6: The Necessity of the God-Man

**Hook:**
"A mere man could not save you — His death would not be enough. A mere God could not die for you. Only the God-man could do both."

**Key Scripture:** 1 Timothy 2:5 — "For there is one God, and there is one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
For salvation to be possible, the mediator between God and man must be both God and man. He must be able to represent both parties. He must be able to bear the infinite weight of God's wrath while representing humanity. Only Jesus Christ satisfies these requirements.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- 1 Timothy 2:5 — One mediator
- Hebrews 2:14-18 — He shared in flesh and blood to destroy death
- Hebrews 9:11-14 — Christ's blood has eternal redemptive power
- Romans 5:12-19 — Christ as the last Adam

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Sin against an infinite God requires an infinite payment. A mere man, no matter how good, could not bear the wrath of God against sin because man is finite. Only God could satisfy God's justice. But the penalty must be paid by a man, for it was man who sinned. The incarnation solves this dilemma. As God, Christ's sacrifice has infinite value. As man, Christ can represent humanity. The incarnation also enables Christ to be a merciful high priest. He has experienced temptation, suffering, and death — so He is able to sympathize with our weaknesses. He is not a distant judge but a sympathetic Savior who has walked our path.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Do not underestimate what it cost God to save you. The incarnation was not a small adjustment — it was the Creator of the universe taking on the limitations of a creature. He did not come to observe but to suffer and die. Your salvation was purchased at the highest possible price — the life of the God-man.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ is the last Adam. The first Adam was a mere man who failed. The last Adam is the God-man who succeeded. Where Adam brought condemnation, Christ brings justification. Where Adam brought death, Christ brings life.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Because Christ is both God and man, His work is complete and sufficient. You do not need another mediator. You do not need additional sacrifices. Christ is all you need.

## Lesson 7: The Preeminence of Christ

**Hook:**
"Jesus is not one way among many. He is the way. He is not a truth among truths. He is the truth. He is not a life among lives. He is the life."

**Key Scripture:** Colossians 1:18 — "He is also the head of the body, the church; and He is the beginning, the firstborn from the dead, so that He Himself will come to have first place in everything."

**PREACH Format:**

**P — Point (The Doctrine)**
Christ is preeminent over all things. He has first place in creation, in the church, and in the new creation. He is supreme over all spiritual powers, all human authorities, and all cosmic forces. Nothing and no one is above Him.

**R — Read (Scripture Support)**
- Colossians 1:15-20 — Christ is preeminent over creation, the church, and the new creation
- Philippians 2:9-11 — God highly exalted Him; every knee will bow
- Ephesians 1:20-23 — Christ seated above all rule and authority
- Hebrews 1:4 — He is superior to angels

**E — Explain (Theological Meaning)**
Christ's preeminence means He is sufficient. The Colossian heresy suggested that Christ was good but not enough — that believers needed philosophy, legalism, mysticism, or angelic mediation in addition to Christ. Paul answered by exalting Christ as supreme and sufficient. In Christ, all the fullness of the Deity dwells bodily. Believers are complete in Him. Nothing can be added to Christ's finished work. His preeminence also means He is Lord. He is not just the Savior we accept — He is the Lord we obey. The term "Lord" (Kyrios) was the Greek translation of Yahweh in the Old Testament. To call Jesus "Lord" is to acknowledge His absolute authority. Every knee will bow — not might bow, but will bow.

**A — Apply (Life Application)**
Does Christ have first place in everything in your life? Not just in your theology, not just on Sunday, not just in your words — in everything. Your time, your money, your relationships, your career, your thoughts. If Christ is preeminent, He must be preeminent over all. He will not share His throne.

**C — Connect (To Christ)**
Christ emptied Himself to become a servant, but God has exalted Him to the highest place. The path to glory was through humility. The path to the throne was through the cross. His preeminence was earned through obedience unto death.

**H — Hope (The Promise)**
Because Christ is preeminent, His kingdom will prevail. No power can defeat Him. No force can overthrow Him. He is the King of Kings and Lord of Lords, and He will reign forever. Those who are in Him will reign with Him.
