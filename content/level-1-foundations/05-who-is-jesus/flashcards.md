# Flashcards: Who Is Jesus?
## 10 Q&A Pairs + Memory Verse Drill

---

### Q1 — What does the Bible mean when it says Jesus is God?

**A:** Jesus is fully divine — co-equal and co-eternal with the Father. He is not a created being or a lesser god. He possesses all divine attributes and is worthy of worship.

**Passages:** John 1:1; John 10:30; Colossians 2:9; Romans 9:5

---

### Q2 — How do we know Jesus was truly human?

**A:** He was born of a woman, grew in wisdom and stature, experienced hunger, thirst, fatigue, sorrow, and joy. He suffered and died a real death. He had a real human body after His resurrection.

**Passages:** John 1:14; Luke 2:52; Hebrews 2:14-18; Hebrews 4:15

---

### Q3 — What is the hypostatic union?

**A:** The union of Christ's divine and human natures in one person. Jesus is one person with two complete natures — fully God and fully man — without confusion, change, division, or separation.

**Passages:** Colossians 2:9; Philippians 2:5-8; Hebrews 1:3

---

### Q4 — What is the trilemma regarding Jesus?

**A:** C.S. Lewis's logical argument that Jesus must be Lord, Liar, or Lunatic. He claimed to be God, so He cannot merely be a good teacher. The evidence confirms He is Lord.

**Passages:** John 14:6; John 8:58; John 10:30

---

### Q5 — Why did Jesus have to be both God and man to save us?

**A:** As God, His sacrifice had infinite value to satisfy divine justice. As man, He could represent humanity and die in our place. Only the God-man could be the mediator between God and man.

**Passages:** 1 Timothy 2:5; Hebrews 2:14-18; Romans 5:12-19

---

### Q6 — What does it mean that Christ is the Creator?

**A:** All things — visible and invisible, spiritual and physical — were created by Christ, through Christ, and for Christ. He is before all things, and in Him all things hold together.

**Passages:** John 1:3; Colossians 1:16-17; Hebrews 1:2-3

---

### Q7 — How did Jesus demonstrate His deity through His works?

**A:** He created all things, sustains the universe, forgave sins (which only God can do), raised the dead, calmed storms, and accepted worship. He will judge all people.

**Passages:** Mark 2:5-7; John 11:43-44; Matthew 14:33; John 5:22

---

### Q8 — What did Jesus claim about Himself that caused the Jews to want to stone Him?

**A:** He claimed equality with God. He said "I and the Father are one" (John 10:30), applied the divine name "I AM" to Himself (John 8:58), and claimed to forgive sins.

**Passages:** John 10:31-33; John 8:58-59; Mark 2:5-7

---

### Q9 — What does Colossians teach about the sufficiency of Christ?

**A:** Christ is supreme over all things and completely sufficient. In Him all the fullness of the Deity dwells, and believers are complete in Him. Nothing can be added to Christ.

**Passages:** Colossians 1:15-20; Colossians 2:9-10; Colossians 2:6-8

---

### Q10 — What does it mean that Christ has preeminence?

**A:** Christ has first place over everything — creation, the church, and the new creation. He is supreme over all spiritual powers and human authorities. Every knee will bow to Him.

**Passages:** Colossians 1:18; Philippians 2:9-11; Ephesians 1:20-23

---

## Memory Verse Drill

**Prompt:** "In the beginning was the Word —"
**Response:** " — and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." (John 1:1)

**Prompt:** "And the Word became flesh —"
**Response:** " — and dwelt among us, and we have seen His glory." (John 1:14)

**Prompt:** "Truly, truly, I say to you —"
**Response:** " — before Abraham was, I AM." (John 8:58)

**Prompt:** "I am the way —"
**Response:** " — and the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father except through me." (John 14:6)

**Prompt:** "For by Him all things were created —"
**Response:** " — in heaven and on earth, visible and invisible... all things were created through Him and for Him." (Colossians 1:16)

**Prompt:** "For in Him all the fullness —"
**Response:** " — of the Deity dwells in bodily form." (Colossians 2:9)

**Prompt:** "God highly exalted Him —"
**Response:** " — so that every knee will bow... and every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord." (Philippians 2:9-11)
