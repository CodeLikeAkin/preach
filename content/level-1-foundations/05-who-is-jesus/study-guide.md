# Study Guide: Who Is Jesus?

## Module Overview
Module 05 — Who Is Jesus explores the person and nature of Christ: His full deity, His full humanity, the hypostatic union, biblical evidence for His divinity, and why the God-man was necessary for salvation.

---

## 1. The Deity of Christ

### Core Truth
Jesus Christ is fully God. He is not merely a good teacher, a prophet, or a moral example — He is God incarnate. The Scriptures declare His deity from beginning to end, and Jesus Himself claimed to be God.

### Jesus' Own Claims
Jesus explicitly claimed equality with God:
- "I and the Father are one" (John 10:30)
- "Before Abraham was, I AM" (John 8:58) — applying the divine name (Yahweh) to Himself
- "Whoever has seen me has seen the Father" (John 14:9)
- "I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me" (John 14:6)

The Jewish leaders understood exactly what He was claiming: "The Jews picked up stones again to stone him... because you, being a man, make yourself God" (John 10:31-33).

### The Trilemma
C.S. Lewis articulated the logical options Jesus forces upon us. A man who claims to be God cannot be merely a good teacher — He must be either:
- **Lord** — He is who He claimed to be
- **Liar** — He knew He was not God but deceived others
- **Lunatic** — He was sincerely deluded

The evidence of His sinless life, fulfilled prophecy, authoritative teaching, and resurrection from the dead confirms that He is Lord.

### Divine Titles and Attributes
- **The Word was God** (John 1:1) — explicit declaration
- **My Lord and my God** (John 20:28) — Thomas's confession
- **God over all** (Romans 9:5) — Paul's declaration
- **The exact representation of God's nature** (Hebrews 1:3)
- He accepted worship (Matthew 28:17; John 9:38) — something reserved for God alone

## 2. The Humanity of Christ

### Fully Man
Jesus was not God wearing a human costume. He became truly and fully human. "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us" (John 1:14). He took on a complete human nature — body, soul, and spirit.

### Evidence of His Humanity
- **Born of a woman** — conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the Virgin Mary (Luke 1:26-38)
- **Developed normally** — "Jesus increased in wisdom and stature" (Luke 2:52)
- **Experienced human limitations** — He grew tired (John 4:6), hungry (Matthew 4:2), thirsty (John 19:28)
- **Experienced human emotions** — He wept (John 11:35), was troubled (John 12:27), felt compassion (Matthew 9:36), knew anger (Mark 3:5)
- **Suffered and died** — a real physical death on the cross
- **Had a real human body after resurrection** — ate food, could be touched (Luke 24:39-43)

### Without Sin
Jesus was fully human yet without sin. He was "tempted in all things as we are, yet without sin" (Hebrews 4:15). His humanity was not diminished by His deity, nor was His deity compromised by His humanity.

---

## 3. The Hypostatic Union

### One Person, Two Natures
The hypostatic union is the theological term for the union of Christ's divine and human natures in one person. Jesus Christ is one person with two complete natures — fully God and fully man — without confusion, change, division, or separation.

### The Chalcedonian Definition (AD 451)
This historic creed defined the orthodox doctrine of Christ:
- Two natures: divine and human
- Without confusion — neither nature is mixed or blended
- Without change — neither nature is altered
- Without division — the one person is not split
- Without separation — the natures are eternally united in the one person

### Why Both Natures Are Necessary
- As **God**, Christ could bear the infinite weight of God's wrath against sin
- As **man**, Christ could represent humanity and die in our place
- Only the God-man could be the perfect mediator between God and man (1 Timothy 2:5)

### Implications
Jesus did not "become" God — He is the eternally begotten Son who took on human nature. His divine nature was not diminished by the incarnation. His human nature was not deified. The two natures remain distinct but are united in the one person forever.

## 4. Biblical Evidence for Christ's Divinity

### Old Testament Prophecies Fulfilled
The Old Testament anticipated a divine Messiah:
- **Isaiah 9:6** — "For to us a child is born... and His name will be called Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Eternal Father, Prince of Peace"
- **Micah 5:2** — "His goings forth are from long ago, from the days of eternity"
- **Psalm 110:1** — David's Lord, seated at God's right hand
- **Zechariah 12:10** — "They will look on Me whom they have pierced" — God speaking of Himself

### New Testament Testimony
- **John 1:1-3** — The Word (Logos) was with God and was God; all things made through Him
- **Colossians 1:15-20** — Christ is the image of the invisible God; all things created through Him and for Him; He is before all things; all fullness dwells in Him
- **Colossians 2:9** — "In Him all the fullness of the Deity dwells in bodily form"
- **Philippians 2:5-11** — Christ, existing in the form of God, emptied Himself to become a servant; exalted to the highest place
- **Hebrews 1:1-3** — The Son is the radiance of God's glory and exact representation of His nature; He upholds all things by His word

### Divine Works
Only God can do what Jesus did:
- **Created all things** (John 1:3; Colossians 1:16)
- **Sustains all things** (Hebrews 1:3; Colossians 1:17)
- **Forgives sins** (Mark 2:5-7) — the scribes rightly noted only God can forgive sins
- **Raised the dead** (John 11:43-44)
- **Will judge all people** (John 5:22; 2 Timothy 4:1)
- **Received worship** (Matthew 14:33; 28:9, 17)

## 5. Why Jesus Had to Be Both God and Man

### The Problem of Sin
Sin against an infinite God requires infinite punishment. A mere man could not bear the weight of God's wrath against sin. Only God Himself could satisfy divine justice. But man must pay the penalty for human sin. Therefore, the mediator must be both God and man.

### The Necessity of a Mediator
"For there is one God, and there is one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus" (1 Timothy 2:5). A mediator must be able to represent both parties. Christ represents God to man (revealing the Father) and man to God (as our high priest and sacrifice).

### The Incarnation's Purpose
- **To reveal God** — "No one has ever seen God; the only God, who is at the Father's side, He has made Him known" (John 1:18)
- **To fulfill the law** — Christ kept the law perfectly on our behalf (Matthew 5:17)
- **To die as our substitute** — As man, He could die; as God, His death had infinite value
- **To sympathize with our weakness** — He was tempted, so He can help those being tempted (Hebrews 2:17-18)

## 6. Christ as Creator and Sustainer

### The Agent of Creation
"All things were created through Him and for Him" (Colossians 1:16). Christ is not a part of creation — He is the Creator of all things. This includes "visible and invisible, whether thrones or dominions or rulers or authorities." Every spiritual power, every angel, every force in the universe was created by Christ and is subject to Him.

### The Sustainer of All Things
"He is before all things, and in Him all things hold together" (Colossians 1:17). Christ is not like a watchmaker who winds the universe and leaves it to run. He actively sustains every atom, every galaxy, every life. Without His sustaining power, creation would cease to exist.

### The Head of the Church
"He is the head of the body, the church" (Colossians 1:18). The same Christ who created the universe and sustains it is the head of His people. The church derives its life, direction, and unity from Him.

### The Goal of Creation
All things were created not only through Christ but "for Him." The whole universe exists for His glory. History is moving toward His ultimate preeminence. "That in all things He might have the preeminence" (Colossians 1:18).

---

## 7. The Sufficiency of Christ

### Christ Is Enough
The letter to the Colossians was written to combat false teaching that added to Christ — human philosophy, legalism, mysticism, angel worship. Paul's answer was to exalt Christ as supreme and sufficient. "In Him dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily, and you are complete in Him" (Colossians 2:9-10 NKJV).

### No Addition Needed
- **Not Christ + philosophy** — human wisdom cannot improve on divine revelation
- **Not Christ + legalism** — law-keeping cannot add to His finished work
- **Not Christ + mysticism** — spiritual experiences cannot supplement His sufficiency
- **Not Christ + angelic mediation** — Christ alone is the mediator

### Christ Is All and in All
"Christ is all and in all" (Colossians 3:11). He is not one among many. He is not the best option in a field of options. He is the only way, the only truth, the only life. To add anything to Christ is to lose Christ.
