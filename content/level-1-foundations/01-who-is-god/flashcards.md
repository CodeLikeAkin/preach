# Flashcards: Who Is God?

**Level:** Level 1 — Foundations
**Module:** 01 — Who Is God?

---

## Flashcard Q&A

### Card 1
**Q:** According to Jeremiah 9:23-24, what is the single most essential pursuit in life?
**A:** To know and understand God. Not wisdom, not might, not riches — but knowing God is the one thing worth boasting in above all else.

### Card 2
**Q:** What does it mean that God is holy?
**A:** God is set apart, distinct, and unique — in a category entirely His own. He is not like us just bigger and better; He is qualitatively different from everything He has made. His holiness pervades every one of His attributes.

### Card 3
**Q:** How do God's justice and love relate to each other?
**A:** They never contradict each other. God does not set aside one attribute to exercise another. At the cross, He satisfied His own justice through His own love — He was fully just and fully loving at the same time.

### Card 4
**Q:** What does God's sovereignty mean for believers who are suffering?
**A:** It means God hears their groaning, remembers His covenant, sees their affliction, and knows what they need. He is in control even when they cannot see His hand, and He always rescues His people — sometimes from the trial, sometimes through it, but always for His glory and their good.

### Card 5
**Q:** What does it mean that God is immutable?
**A:** God never changes in His nature, character, or purposes. He is the same yesterday, today, and forever. Every promise He has made is secure because it rests on His unchanging character, not on human performance.

### Card 6
**Q:** What is the difference between knowing about God and knowing God?
**A:** Knowing about God is accumulating facts and information. Knowing God is a personal, intimate, transforming relationship. You can know many facts about God and still not know Him — but when you truly know Him, it changes your life.

### Card 7
**Q:** How does knowing God change a person?
**A:** Real transformation comes from beholding God's character, not from trying harder to follow rules. The more you see of His beauty, glory, and excellency, the more your heart is drawn to Him and the more your life is transformed from the inside out.

### Card 8
**Q:** Why can we trust God's promises even when our circumstances are difficult?
**A:** Because God does not change. His promises rest on His unchanging character, not on our circumstances or performance. The same God who was faithful to Abraham, Moses, and Paul will be faithful to us.

### Card 9
**Q:** How should knowing God affect the way we worship?
**A:** Worship is the natural response to seeing God for who He truly is. It is not a performance we manufacture but a spontaneous outflow of a heart that has caught a glimpse of His holiness, love, and worth.

### Card 10
**Q:** How does knowing God fuel the mission of the church?
**A:** When you see that God is of infinite worth, you naturally want others to see it too. Mission is not a program or an obligation — it is the overflow of a heart captured by the beauty of God.
