## Lesson 1 — The Greatest Pursuit: Knowing God

### HOOK

If someone asked you what the single most important thing in life is, what would you say? Health? Family? Security? Purpose? Most of us would list good things, but none of them is the answer. The most important thing, the one thing that makes everything else make sense, is knowing God. Not knowing about Him. Knowing Him.

### CORE TEACHING

There is a difference between knowing about someone and actually knowing them. You can read a biography of a world leader, memorize their accomplishments, recite their speeches, and yet never know them as a person. The same is true with God. It is possible to fill your mind with facts about God — His attributes, His works, His names — and still not know Him. And the difference matters more than anything else.

Scripture is clear about what matters most. The prophet Jeremiah records God saying that the wise should not boast in their wisdom, the mighty should not boast in their might, and the rich should not boast in their riches. Instead, the one who boasts should boast in this: that they understand and know God. Think about what God is saying here. Wisdom is good. Strength has its place. Wealth is not evil. But none of them compare to knowing Him. Knowledge of God is not just one good thing among many. It is the essential thing.

This changes how you approach the Christian life. Many believers treat spiritual growth as a list of things to do — pray more, read the Bible, go to church, stop sinning. Those are all good, but they will not transform you on their own. The engine of real change is knowing God. Think of it like a marriage. You do not grow in love for your spouse by trying harder to love them. You grow in love as you discover more of who they are — their character, their kindness, their strength. The more you see, the more your heart is drawn. It works the same way with God. The more you see of His beauty, His glory, His goodness, the more your heart is captured by Him.

And this is not just for theologians or people with seminary degrees. It is for every believer. The preacher's most important job is not to give people rules or programs — it is to show them God. When people see God for who He truly is, things change. The sins they struggled to give up lose their appeal. The fears that paralyzed them shrink. The mission that seemed impossible becomes worth any cost. All of it flows from knowing Him.

This is why the gospel is the foundation of everything. The gospel is not just the door you walk through to get saved. It is the window through which you see God most clearly. At the cross, God revealed both His justice and His love, His holiness and His mercy. The more you understand the gospel, the more you understand God. And the more you understand God, the more you are changed.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Jeremiah 9:23-24 — "Thus says the LORD: 'Let not the wise man glory in his wisdom, let not the mighty man glory in his might, nor let the rich man glory in his riches; but let him who glories glory in this, that he understands and knows Me, that I am the LORD, exercising lovingkindness, judgment, and righteousness in the earth. For in these I delight,' says the LORD." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

John 17:3 — "And this is eternal life, that they may know You, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom You have sent." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, set aside 15 minutes each day to read one chapter of the Gospel of John. Before you read, ask God to show you who He is through the passage. After reading, write down one thing you learned about God's character — not about what you should do, but about who He is.

### SUMMARY

- Knowing God is not one good thing among many — it is the essential purpose of your life.
- Real spiritual change comes from seeing God's character, not from trying harder to follow rules.
- The gospel is the clearest revelation of who God is, and growing in knowledge of the gospel grows your knowledge of God.

## Lesson 2 — The God Who Is Holy: Set Apart and Unique

### HOOK

When you hear the word "holy," what comes to mind? Maybe a stained-glass window, a quiet church building, or someone who seems more religious than everyone else. But holiness is not really about any of those things. And once you understand what it actually means, it changes everything about how you see God.

### CORE TEACHING

Most people assume that holy simply means morally perfect or sinless. And it includes that, but it means so much more. At its root, the word holy means to be cut off, to be separate, to be distinct. When Scripture calls God holy, it is saying that He is in a category entirely His own. He is not like us just bigger and better. He is not like us at all.

Think about it this way. If you saw a seven-foot-tall man walking toward you, you would probably feel intimidated. He is bigger than you, stronger than you. But he is still a man. You understand him. You know what he is. Now imagine encountering something completely different from anything you have ever known — not just bigger or stronger, but entirely other. That is closer to what it means to encounter a holy God. He does not belong to the same category as His creation. He is completely separate, completely unique, completely set apart.

This is why the seraphim in Isaiah's vision do not cry out "powerful, powerful, powerful" or "loving, loving, loving." They cry out "holy, holy, holy." The three-fold repetition emphasizes that this is the attribute above all others. The holiness of God is not one attribute among many. It is the quality that pervades every one of His attributes. He has holy power, holy wisdom, holy love, holy justice. Everything about Him is set apart.

This truth should produce two responses in us. First, it should produce reverence. We have made God too familiar. We sing songs that make Him sound like a friend next door, and He is that. But He is also the God before whom the strongest angels cover their faces. The fear of the Lord is not a primitive idea we outgrew — it is the appropriate response to encountering the Holy One. Second, it should produce hope. The same God who is so far above us has reached down to us. He did not entrust us to an angel or a lesser being. He holds us in His own hand. The God who is holy beyond our comprehension is the same God who calls us His children.

The cross is where these two realities meet. At the cross, the holy God who cannot look upon sin did not turn away from sinners. He provided a way for unholy people to be brought near. The separation that our sin created was bridged by the sacrifice of His own Son. The holy God made a way for us to know Him.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Exodus 15:11 — "Who is like You, O LORD, among the gods? Who is like You, glorious in holiness, fearful in praises, doing wonders?" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Isaiah 6:3 — "And one cried to another and said: 'Holy, holy, holy is the LORD of hosts; the whole earth is full of His glory!'" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

1 Peter 1:15-16 — "But as He who called you is holy, you also be holy in all your conduct, because it is written, 'Be holy, for I am holy.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, write Isaiah 6:3 on a card and place it by your bed. Each morning, read it aloud before you start your day. Each evening, mark a simple check or X on the card: "Did I approach God with holy reverence today, or with casual familiarity?" Bring the card to the end of the week and see what it shows you.

### SUMMARY

- Holiness means God is set apart and unique — not a bigger version of us, but in a category entirely His own.
- Every attribute of God is a holy attribute — His power, love, and justice are all set apart and perfect.
- The cross is the ultimate demonstration of how the holy God made a way for unholy people to be brought near.

## Lesson 3 — The God Who Rules: Sovereign Over All

### HOOK

Have you ever felt like your life is spinning out of control? Maybe it is a health crisis, a financial setback, or a relationship that is falling apart. In those moments, it is easy to wonder if anyone is in charge. The biblical answer is both comforting and challenging: God is sovereign over all things, and His rule extends to every corner of your life.

### CORE TEACHING

When the people of Israel were enslaved in Egypt, they cried out to God for deliverance. But deliverance did not come immediately. Day after day, they remained in bondage. They groaned under the weight of their oppression, and from where they stood, it looked like God was not listening. That is exactly how we feel when we are in the middle of suffering. We cry out, we wait, and nothing seems to change.

But the Bible tells us that there was something happening that Israel could not see. While they were looking at their circumstances from a horizontal perspective — what they could see and feel — something was happening in the vertical realm that they were unaware of. Exodus tells us that God heard their groaning. He remembered His covenant with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. He saw the people of Israel. And He knew.

These four verbs are the anchor for every believer going through suffering. God hears. Even when it feels like He is silent, He hears. The natural tendency in suffering is to assume God is not listening, that He is distracted, that He does not care. But Scripture is clear: He hears the cries of His people. God remembers. He does not forget His promises. He remembers His covenant not because of our goodness but because of His faithfulness. If God gave us what we deserved, we would have no hope. But He remembers His covenant, not our sin. God sees. There is a strange comfort in believing that God might not see what is happening to us — because if He sees and allows it, then what does that mean? But the alternative is worse. If God does not see, then He is not in control. And if He is not in control, then your salvation is not secure. God sees everything, and He is not absent in your worst moments. God knows. This is not just about information. It means He loves, He pities, He knows what will maximize His glory and what you truly need. He knows what you can bear. He knows when your deliverer is coming.

This does not mean God will remove every trial. It means He is present in every trial. The cross is the ultimate proof. At the cross, God the Father heard the cry of His Son. He remembered His covenant. He saw the suffering. He knew the purpose. And He worked redemption through the darkest moment in history. If God can bring resurrection out of the cross, He can bring good out of whatever you are walking through. He always rescues His people — sometimes from the trial, sometimes through the trial, but always for His glory and for your good.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Exodus 2:24-25 — "So God heard their groaning, and God remembered His covenant with Abraham, with Isaac, and with Jacob. And God looked upon the children of Israel, and God acknowledged them." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Psalm 115:3 — "But our God is in heaven; He does whatever He pleases." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Romans 8:28 — "And we know that all things work together for good to those who love God, to those who are the called according to His purpose." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, when you feel anxious about something, take a piece of paper and write down the four verbs: He hears, He remembers, He sees, He knows. Next to each one, write one way you have seen God do that in your life. Keep the paper where you can see it.

### SUMMARY

- God hears your cries even when you cannot feel His presence — He is never distant from His people.
- God remembers His covenant based on His faithfulness, not your performance.
- God sees your suffering and knows His purpose in it, and He proved His commitment at the cross.

## Lesson 4 — The God Who Is Both Just and Loving

### HOOK

Have you ever struggled to reconcile two things that seem to contradict each other? Maybe you have heard someone say, "I could never love a God who sends people to hell." Or maybe you have wondered yourself how the same God who commands us to love our enemies could also judge sin so severely. The answer is not that one attribute wins over the other.

### CORE TEACHING

One of the most common mistakes people make about God is treating His attributes as if they compete with each other. The assumption goes like this: God is loving, but He is also just. Sometimes His love wins and He shows mercy. Other times His justice wins and He judges. This way of thinking creates a God who is divided against Himself, and that is not the God of Scripture.

The truth is that God never sets aside one attribute to exercise another. His love is never at odds with His justice. His mercy never cancels His holiness. Everything God does, He does as the fully integrated, perfectly consistent God that He is. When He shows mercy, He does so justly. When He judges, He does so in love.

The cross is the clearest demonstration of this. If God had simply forgiven sin without addressing it, He would have been loving but unjust. If He had punished every sinner without providing a way of escape, He would have been just but not loving. But at the cross, God did neither. He satisfied His own justice through His own love. He did not overlook sin. He did not compromise His righteousness. He took the punishment upon Himself in the person of His Son.

This means that God's justice and love are not in conflict. They are two sides of the same glorious reality. His justice demanded that sin be punished. His love provided the punishment. He did not merely forgive sin — He conquered it. He did not merely overlook your rebellion — He absorbed its consequences. This is why the gospel is such good news. God did not have to choose between being just and being loving. He was fully both at the same time, and He demonstrated it at the cross.

Understanding this changes how you relate to God. You do not have to worry that you might approach Him at a moment when His justice is active but His love is not. His love is not a mood He switches into. It is who He is. And His justice is not something to be feared by those who are in Christ, because the justice you deserved was fully satisfied at the cross. You can trust Him completely because He is not one thing one moment and something else the next. He is always, fully, completely who He is.



### KEY SCRIPTURES

Romans 3:25-26 — "Whom God set forth as a propitiation by His blood, through faith, to demonstrate His righteousness, because in His forbearance God had passed over the sins that were previously committed, to demonstrate at the present time His righteousness, that He might be just and the justifier of the one who has faith in Jesus." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

1 John 4:10 — "In this is love, not that we loved God, but that He loved us and sent His Son to be the propitiation for our sins." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, read Romans 3:21-26 every day. Each day, write down one answer to this question: "What does this passage teach me about God's character that I would not know without the cross?" By the end of the week, you will have seven insights about how God's justice and love work together.

### SUMMARY

- God's attributes never contradict each other — His justice and love are perfectly integrated, not competing.
- At the cross, God satisfied His own justice through His own love — He did not compromise either.
- Because of the cross, you can trust that God is fully just and fully loving toward you at all times.

## Lesson 5 — The God Who Does Not Change: Immutability and Trust

### HOOK

Have you ever made a promise you could not keep? Or had someone make a promise to you that they broke? It happens all the time. People change their minds. Circumstances change. Feelings change. But there is one being in the universe who has never changed His mind, broken a promise, or become someone different than He was before. That is who God is.

### CORE TEACHING

Everything around you changes. Your body grows older. Your circumstances shift. Your feelings about God fluctuate from day to day. Even the strongest relationships you have will experience change over time. But God does not change. He is immutable, which means His nature, His character, and His purposes remain exactly what they have always been and will always be.

This is not the same thing as saying God is static or inactive. He is not like a frozen statue that never moves. God is actively at work in His creation every moment. But His actions always flow from the same unchanging character. He does not have good days and bad days. He does not wake up in a different mood. He does not revise His standards or lower His expectations. He is the same yesterday, today, and forever.

Think about what this means for your life. Every promise God has made in Scripture is backed by an unchanging character. When He says He will never leave you nor forsake you, that promise does not depend on your performance. It depends on who He is. And He does not change. When He promises to work all things together for your good, that promise is not conditional on your circumstances lining up perfectly. It is rooted in the unchanging purpose of an unchanging God.

This is where the gospel meets your daily life. Your salvation does not rest on your ability to hold onto God. It rests on His ability to hold onto you. If God could change His mind about saving you, you would have reason to fear. But He cannot change His mind because He does not change. The same God who chose you before the foundation of the world, who sent His Son to die for you, who sealed you with His Spirit — that God is the same today as He was then. Nothing you do today will surprise Him or alter His commitment to you.

This truth is also a warning. Because God does not change, His judgment against sin is as real today as it was in the days of Noah. He does not become more lenient over time. The same holiness that required the cross still requires holiness from His people. But for those who are in Christ, this unchanging holiness is not a threat. It is an anchor. The God who was faithful to Abraham, to Moses, to David, to Paul — He will be faithful to you. He cannot be otherwise.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Malachi 3:6 — "For I am the LORD, I do not change; therefore you are not consumed, O sons of Jacob." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

James 1:17 — "Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and comes down from the Father of lights, with whom there is no variation or shadow of turning." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Hebrews 13:8 — "Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, today, and forever." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, pick one promise from Scripture that you struggle to believe is true for you. Write it on a card. On the back, write: "God does not change, so this promise does not change." Every time doubt creeps in, read both sides of the card out loud.

### SUMMARY

- God's character never changes — He is the same yesterday, today, and forever.
- Every promise God has made is secure because it rests on His unchanging nature, not your performance.
- Your salvation is secure because the God who saved you will never change His mind about keeping you.

## Lesson 6 — Knowing God vs. Knowing About God

### HOOK

Think about the person you know best in the world. Now think about a famous person you have read about or watched online. You know facts about the famous person. You know their name, their work, their story. But you do not know them. There is a difference, and it is the difference between religion and relationship with God.

### CORE TEACHING

It is possible to know a great deal about God and not know Him at all. You can memorize the attributes of God. You can recite the books of the Bible. You can explain the doctrine of justification. And still, your heart can be untouched. This is one of the most dangerous conditions in the Christian life — having the right facts without the living reality.

The Bible does not measure knowledge of God by how much information you have accumulated. It measures it by whether that knowledge has changed you. When Scripture says that eternal life is to know God, it is not talking about knowing facts about Him. It is talking about a personal, intimate, transforming relationship. It is the difference between reading a biography of a king and being invited into the king's throne room as a beloved child.

This is where many believers get stuck. They learn more and more about God, but their hearts remain cold. They attend Bible studies, listen to sermons, read books, and yet their love for God does not grow. The problem is not that they are learning too much. The problem is that they have mistaken the map for the destination. Studying about God is meant to lead you to God. When it becomes an end in itself, it becomes a form of idolatry. You can even study the doctrine of God in a way that makes you proud rather than humble, self-sufficient rather than dependent.

The path to really knowing God is not to stop studying. It is to study differently. It is to approach every truth about God as a door to walk through, not just a fact to file away. When you learn that God is holy, do not just agree with the doctrine. Let it drive you to your knees. When you learn that God is sovereign, do not just nod your head. Let it quiet your anxious heart. When you learn that God is loving, do not just check the box. Let it melt your fear.

This is what the preacher was talking about when he said the goal is not to give people moralisms or quaint stories. The goal is to exegete God — to reveal Him, to explain Him, to let people see His beauty and glory and power, so they walk away captured not just by what they are supposed to do but by who He is. The knowledge that transforms is the knowledge that moves from the head to the heart, from information to adoration.

The cross makes this possible. Without the cross, we could know facts about God from a distance, but we could never know Him personally. Our sin created a barrier that no amount of study could cross. But because of Christ, we are invited into the most intimate knowledge possible — to know God as our Father.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Jeremiah 9:23-24 — "Thus says the LORD: 'Let not the wise man glory in his wisdom, let not the mighty man glory in his might, nor let the rich man glory in his riches; but let him who glories glory in this, that he understands and knows Me, that I am the LORD, exercising lovingkindness, judgment, and righteousness in the earth. For in these I delight,' says the LORD." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

John 17:3 — "And this is eternal life, that they may know You, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom You have sent." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, take one attribute of God from the study guide — holiness, sovereignty, or love. Each day, spend ten minutes reading every Scripture you can find on that attribute. After you read, sit in silence for two minutes and ask God to make that truth real in your heart, not just clear in your mind.

### SUMMARY

- You can know facts about God without knowing God — the goal is a relationship, not a theology exam.
- True knowledge of God changes you; it moves from your head to your heart and reshapes your life.
- Studying about God is meant to lead you to God — if it does not, stop and ask Him to close the gap.

## Lesson 7 — Living in Light of Who God Is

### HOOK

Have you ever learned something that changed everything? Maybe you discovered a truth about your health that made you change your habits, or a truth about a relationship that changed how you treat the other person. Knowing God should do the same thing. It should not just inform your mind. It should transform your life.

### CORE TEACHING

Knowing who God is changes everything. It is not meant to be a collection of doctrines you affirm on Sunday and forget by Monday. The knowledge of God is the engine of the entire Christian life — your worship, your trust in suffering, your mission, your daily choices. When you truly know God, everything else falls into its proper place.

Start with suffering. Every person faces pain, disappointment, and loss. The question is not whether you will suffer but how you will respond when you do. If you know that God is sovereign, then your suffering is not random. It is not outside His control. If you know that God is good, then His purposes in your suffering are loving, even when you cannot see them. If you know that God is faithful, then He will bring you through whatever He brings you to. This does not make suffering easy. But it makes it bearable. The difference between a believer who crumbles under trial and one who endures is not personality. It is whether they know their God.

Now consider worship. Worship is not a song you sing on Sunday morning. It is the natural response of a heart that has seen who God is. When you catch even a glimpse of His holiness, your response is the same as Isaiah's — "Woe is me." When you see His love, your heart melts. When you understand His sovereignty, you rest. True worship is not manufactured. It flows spontaneously from the knowledge of God. If your worship feels empty, the problem is probably not your worship style. It may be that you have lost sight of how great God is.

Then consider mission. Why would anyone leave the comfort of home to take the gospel to the ends of the earth? Why would anyone risk rejection to speak the truth to a neighbor? The answer is not guilt or pressure. It is knowing God. When you see that God is of infinite worth, you naturally want others to see it too. Mission is not a program you sign up for. It is the overflow of a heart that has been captured by the beauty of God. The greatest missionary force in the world is not strategy or resources. It is men and women who know their God.

The cross ties all of this together. The same God who demonstrated His love and justice at the cross is the God you can trust in suffering, worship in spirit and truth, and proclaim to the nations. You do not need to become a different person to live this way. You need to see the God who is already there, already sovereign, already faithful, already worthy. The more you know Him, the more your life will reflect Him.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Psalm 145:3 — "Great is the LORD, and greatly to be praised; and His greatness is unsearchable." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Daniel 11:32 — "The people who know their God shall be strong, and carry out great exploits." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, choose one area of your life where you are struggling to trust God — it could be a financial worry, a relationship issue, a health concern, or a fear about the future. Each day, write down one truth about God from this module that applies directly to that situation. By Friday, write a short prayer thanking God for who He is in the middle of that struggle.

### SUMMARY

- Knowing God changes how you face suffering — His sovereignty and goodness give you anchor when life feels unstable.
- Worship is the natural response to seeing God for who He truly is, not a performance you manufacture.
- Mission flows from knowing God's infinite worth — when you see His value, you naturally want others to see it too.
