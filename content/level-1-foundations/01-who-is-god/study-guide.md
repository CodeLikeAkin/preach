# Study Guide: Who Is God?

**Level:** Level 1 — Foundations
**Module:** 01 — Who Is God?

---

## 1. Core Definition

God is the self-existent, eternal, and infinite Creator of all things who exists as one God in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. He is holy, righteous, loving, faithful, and sovereign over all creation. He is not like us magnified; He is qualitatively different from everything He has made — completely other, set apart, and worthy of all worship. To know Him is the essential purpose of human existence, and the depth of our love for Him grows only as we grow in the knowledge of who He truly is.

---

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

### 2.1 God Is Knowable Through His Self-Revelation

God has made Himself known through creation, through His Word, and supremely through His Son, Jesus Christ. The knowledge of God is not an optional pursuit for the spiritually ambitious — it is the essential foundation of the Christian life. Jeremiah 9:23-24 declares that the only true boast is to understand and know God. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.2 God Is Holy — Completely Other

The holiness of God means He is set apart, distinct, and unique — not merely sinless but in a category entirely His own. Exodus 15:11 asks, "Who is like You, O Lord, among the gods?" and the answer is no one. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV] His holiness pervades every one of His attributes — His power is holy power, His love is holy love, His justice is holy justice. There is no one like Him.

### 2.3 God Is Both Righteous and Loving — Never at Odds

God never sets aside one attribute to exercise another. His righteousness demands justice for sin, and His love provides the sacrifice that satisfies that justice. At the cross, God did not compromise His righteousness to show love; He satisfied His righteousness through love. Romans 3:25-26 shows that God presented Christ as a propitiation to demonstrate His righteousness. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.4 God Is Sovereign — He Rules Over All

God's sovereignty means He rules over everything — not just the good things but also suffering, trials, and opposition. He hears the groaning of His people, remembers His covenant, sees their affliction, and knows what they need. Exodus 2:24-25 reveals that God heard, remembered, saw, and knew — four verbs that anchor His people in His sovereign care even when deliverance does not come immediately. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.5 God Is Triune — One God in Three Persons

The Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are each fully God, yet there is one God. The Trinity is not a contradiction but a mystery revealed in Scripture. In redemption, the Father sends the Son, born of a woman, born under the law, and sends the Spirit of His Son into our hearts crying, "Abba, Father" (Galatians 4:4-6). [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 2.6 God Works Through His Word and Spirit to Transform

Transformation does not come through human effort, moralism, or self-help techniques. It comes through the renewing of the mind by the Word of God as the Holy Spirit works through that Word. Romans 12:2 calls believers to be transformed by the renewing of their minds. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV] The knowledge of God is the engine of genuine change.

## 3. Common Misconceptions

### 3.1 God Is Like Us, Only Bigger

Many people imagine God as a super-powered version of a human being — someone who thinks, feels, and acts as we do, just on a larger scale. Scripture teaches that God is not quantitatively different (more of the same) but qualitatively different (completely other). He does not belong to the same category as His creation. As He declares in Exodus 3:14, "I AM WHO I AM" — self-defined, not measured against anything else. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 3.2 God's Love Cancels His Justice

A common assumption is that because God is loving, He overlooks sin. This misconception treats God's attributes as competing forces — as though His love wins out over His justice. Scripture teaches that God's love and justice work together. The cross is the proof: God did not cancel His justice; He satisfied it through the sacrifice of His Son.

### 3.3 Knowing About God Is the Same as Knowing God

Many believers assume that accumulating theological information is equivalent to knowing God. Scripture consistently distinguishes between knowing about God and knowing Him personally and relationally. The knowledge of God that matters is not merely intellectual — it transforms the heart, reshapes affections, and produces obedience. As Jeremiah 9:23-24 emphasizes, the boast is not in knowing facts about God but in understanding and knowing Him. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### 3.4 If God Is Sovereign, He Is Responsible for Evil

Some conclude that God's sovereignty over all things makes Him the author of sin. Scripture affirms both God's absolute sovereignty and human moral responsibility without contradiction. God works all things according to His will (Ephesians 1:11), yet He is holy and cannot be tempted with evil (James 1:13). [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV] His sovereignty is a comfort to His people, not a license to blame Him for sin.

### 3.5 If God Loved Me, He Would Not Allow Suffering

The assumption that love and suffering are incompatible contradicts the entire biblical witness. God's love does not guarantee a pain-free life; it guarantees His presence in suffering. The cross — the supreme demonstration of God's love — was also the supreme moment of suffering. Scripture consistently shows that God uses suffering for His glory and our good.

## 4. Practical Application Angles

### 4.1 Study the Attributes of God Intentionally

The single most practical thing a believer can do is to intentionally study who God is. This is not academic theology for scholars — it is the engine of worship, obedience, and mission. A believer who knows God deeply will love Him more, trust Him more, and serve Him more than a believer who merely knows rules and principles.

### 4.2 Let the Knowledge of God Drive Change

Most attempts at spiritual growth focus on external behavior modification. The biblical approach is different: look at God, and transformation follows. The more you see of His beauty, glory, and excellency, the more your affections are drawn toward Him and the more empowered you become to live for Him. Change flows from beholding, not striving.

### 4.3 Trust God's Character in Suffering

When suffering comes, the natural response is to assume God doesn't hear, doesn't care, or isn't in control. The biblical response is to anchor yourself in what Scripture reveals about God: He hears your groaning, He remembers His covenant, He sees your affliction, and He knows what you need. Your feelings may say otherwise, but God's character does not change.

### 4.4 Turn Knowledge into Worship

The purpose of knowing God is not to have correct theology but to worship Him rightly. Every truth about God should lead to praise. When you learn that God is holy, worship Him. When you learn that He is sovereign, trust Him. When you learn that He is faithful, rest in Him. Knowledge that does not produce worship has missed its goal.

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Holy** | Set apart, distinct, unique — God is in a category entirely His own, qualitatively different from all creation |
| **Sovereign** | God's absolute rule and authority over all things — nothing happens outside His control |
| **Omnipotent** | All-powerful — God can do whatever He wills, consistent with His character |
| **Omniscient** | All-knowing — God knows all things, past, present, and future, including every thought and intention |
| **Omnipresent** | Present everywhere — no place is outside God's presence |
| **Immutable** | Unchangeable — God's nature, character, and purposes do not change |
| **Self-Existent** | God derives His existence from no one and nothing — He is the uncaused Cause |
| **Eternal** | Without beginning or end — God exists outside of time |
| **Righteous** | Perfectly just and morally pure — God always does what is right and always judges rightly |
| **Grace** | God's unmerited favor toward sinners — giving us what we do not deserve |
| **Mercy** | God's compassion toward the miserable — not giving us what we do deserve |
| **Trinity** | One God in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — each fully God, yet one God |
