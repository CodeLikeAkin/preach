# Flashcards: Salvation & Grace

## 10 Question & Answer Pairs

### 1
**Q: What is salvation?**
A: Salvation is God's rescue of sinners from sin, death, and judgment through Jesus Christ. It includes forgiveness, new life, and being made whole in every way that matters.

### 2
**Q: What is grace?**
A: Grace is the unearned, undeserved favor of God. Mercy is not getting what you deserve (punishment). Grace is getting what you do not deserve (forgiveness, eternal life, adoption as God's child).

### 3
**Q: What is justification?**
A: Justification is the legal declaration by God that a sinner is righteous through faith in Christ. It is not based on the sinner's own goodness but on Christ's righteousness being credited to them.

### 4
**Q: How can God be both just and justify the wicked?**
A: God remains just because the penalty for sin was fully paid by Jesus Christ on the cross. Our sin was placed on Christ, and His righteousness is credited to us. Justice was served, and mercy is offered.

### 5
**Q: What is repentance?**
A: Repentance is a change of mind about sin and self that leads to turning away from sin and toward God. It is not merely feeling sorry for sin but changing direction.

### 6
**Q: What is saving faith?**
A: Saving faith is not just agreeing that the facts of the gospel are true. It is personally trusting in Christ alone for salvation — putting your full weight on Him and His finished work.

### 7
**Q: What is regeneration?**
A: Regeneration is the new birth. It is the supernatural work of the Holy Spirit by which a spiritually dead person is made alive in Christ and becomes a new creation (2 Corinthians 5:17).

### 8
**Q: Can a person be saved by being a good person?**
A: No. No amount of good works can earn salvation. Even our best deeds fall short of God's perfect standard. Salvation is a gift received through faith, not a wage earned by effort.

### 9
**Q: What is the difference between being "saved by grace" and "saved by a prayer"?**
A: Grace is God's work; a prayer is a human action. Many people pray a prayer but are not truly saved because they never repented and believed. Salvation comes through genuine repentance and faith, not reciting words.

### 10
**Q: Can a believer know for certain that they are saved?**
A: Yes. The Bible was written so that believers "may know that they have eternal life" (1 John 5:13). Assurance is based on God's promises, the witness of the Holy Spirit, and the evidence of a changed life.
