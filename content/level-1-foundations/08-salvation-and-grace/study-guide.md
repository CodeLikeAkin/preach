# Study Guide: Salvation & Grace

## 1. Core Definition

Salvation is the work of God in which He rescues sinners from sin, death, and judgment through the person and work of Jesus Christ and brings them into a right relationship with Himself. It is entirely a gift of grace — unearned, undeserved, and received through repentance and faith. Salvation is not something a person achieves; it is something God accomplishes. It includes justification (being declared righteous), regeneration (being born again), and the assurance of eternal life.

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

- **Salvation Is by Grace Through Faith** — Grace means God gives us what we do not deserve. Faith is how we receive it. Ephesians 2:8-9 says, "For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast." Salvation cannot be earned, merited, or achieved by human effort. It is received by trusting in Christ alone [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

- **Justification Is a Legal Declaration** — When a person believes in Christ, God declares them righteous. This is not because they have become morally perfect, but because the righteousness of Christ is credited to them and their sin was credited to Christ on the cross. Romans 3:26 says God is "just and the justifier of the one who has faith in Jesus." Justification is a one-time legal declaration that can never be reversed [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

- **Repentance and Faith Are Two Sides of the Same Response** — Repentance is a change of mind about sin and self that leads to turning away from sin and toward God. Faith is trusting in Christ alone for salvation. They are not two separate steps but one response: turning from sin and trusting Christ. Jesus preached, "Repent, and believe in the gospel" (Mark 1:15). Paul preached "repentance toward God and faith toward our Lord Jesus Christ" (Acts 20:21) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

- **Regeneration Is the New Birth** — Salvation includes being born again by the Holy Spirit. This is not a person improving their old life; it is God making them a new creation. "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new" (2 Corinthians 5:17). Regeneration is the supernatural work of God that gives spiritual life to a person who was spiritually dead [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

- **Assurance of Salvation Is Possible** — God does not want believers to live in doubt about their salvation. The Holy Spirit bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God (Romans 8:16). John wrote his gospel so that readers "may believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that believing you may have life in His name" (John 20:31). Assurance is based on the promises of God, not on feelings or performance [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

- **The Gospel Is the Power of God for Salvation** — Paul declared, "I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ, for it is the power of God to salvation for everyone who believes" (Romans 1:16). The gospel is not just information about salvation; it is the means by which God saves people. When the message of Christ's death, burial, and resurrection is preached, the Holy Spirit uses it to bring people to faith [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

## 3. Common Misconceptions

- **"Salvation is about being a good person."** — No amount of good works can earn salvation. The Bible teaches that even our best deeds are tainted by sin. Salvation is entirely by grace through faith, not by works.

- **"I was saved by praying a prayer."** — A prayer does not save anyone. Salvation comes through repentance and faith in Christ. Many people pray a prayer but show no evidence of regeneration. Genuine salvation produces a transformed life.

- **"Once saved, always saved — so I can live however I want."** — True salvation produces a new heart that desires to follow Christ. If a person claims to be saved but lives in persistent, unrepentant sin, their profession of faith is called into question. Good works do not save, but they do accompany salvation.

- **"I need to clean up my life before I come to God."** — This misunderstands grace. God does not save good people; He saves sinners. You do not clean up to come to Christ; you come to Christ to be cleaned up.

- **"Repentance is just feeling sorry for your sins."** — Biblical repentance is more than remorse. It is a change of mind that leads to a change of direction. The Bible distinguishes between "godly sorrow" that produces repentance and "worldly sorrow" that produces only regret (2 Corinthians 7:10).

- **"Grace means God overlooks sin."** — Grace does not mean God ignores sin. It means God provided a substitute — Jesus Christ — who bore the full penalty of sin so that sinners could be forgiven justly (Romans 3:25-26).

## 4. Practical Application Angles

- **Responding to the gospel** — Understanding what salvation actually is helps a person know whether they have truly responded to Christ or merely performed a religious ritual.

- **Sharing the gospel clearly** — When believers understand the essential elements of salvation (grace, faith, repentance, justification), they can share it accurately with others instead of presenting a watered-down version.

- **Building assurance** — Many believers struggle with doubt. Understanding that assurance is based on God's promises rather than feelings grounds confidence in something solid.

- **Battling legalism** — A clear understanding of grace frees believers from the exhausting treadmill of trying to earn God's favor through performance.

- **Avoiding false assurance** — Not everyone who claims to be saved is saved. Understanding the marks of genuine conversion helps pastors, small group leaders, and individuals discern genuine faith from counterfeit faith.

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Grace | The unearned, undeserved favor of God toward sinners, giving them what they do not deserve and cannot earn (Ephesians 2:8-9) |
| Justification | The legal declaration by God that a sinner is righteous through faith in Christ, based on Christ's righteousness being credited to them (Romans 3:21-26) |
| Repentance | A change of mind about sin and self that results in turning away from sin and toward God (Acts 20:21) |
| Faith | Trusting in Christ alone for salvation, resting on His finished work rather than personal merit (Ephesians 2:8-9) |
| Regeneration | The supernatural work of the Holy Spirit by which a person is made spiritually alive and becomes a new creation (John 3:3-8; 2 Corinthians 5:17) |
| New Birth | Another term for regeneration — the beginning of spiritual life in a formerly dead sinner (John 3:3) |
| Assurance of Salvation | The confidence that a believer can have that they are genuinely saved, based on God's promises and the witness of the Holy Spirit (Romans 8:16; 1 John 5:13) |
| Gospel | The good news that Christ died for our sins, was buried, and rose again on the third day according to the Scriptures (1 Corinthians 15:1-4) |
| Imputation | The act of God by which our sin was credited to Christ and His righteousness is credited to us (2 Corinthians 5:21) |
| Soteriology | The theological study of salvation, from the Greek word soteria |
