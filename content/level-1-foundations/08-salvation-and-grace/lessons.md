# Salvation & Grace — 7 Lessons

---

## Lesson 1 — What Is Salvation?

### HOOK
Imagine someone trapped in a burning building with no way out. The smoke is thick. The floor is collapsing. Death is certain. Then a rescuer breaks through the wall, grabs them, and carries them to safety. That is salvation — not a theory, but a rescue. And every person on earth needs it more than they realize.

### CORE TEACHING
Salvation comes from a word that means rescue, deliverance, and wholeness. In the Bible, when a person is saved, they are rescued from sin, delivered from death, and made whole in every way that matters. It is not about becoming religious or cleaning up your life. It is about being pulled out of a disaster that you could never escape on your own.

The word for salvation in the original language is "soteria." It includes safety, health, deliverance, and preservation. When the Bible says "salvation," it is not just talking about going to heaven after you die — although that is part of it. It is talking about God breaking into your life right now to save you from what sin has done to you.

Think about it this way. Sin is like a disease that infects every part of a person — the mind, the will, the emotions, the body. Salvation is God's complete cure. It does not just treat the symptoms; it removes the disease and makes you whole. That is why the same Greek word can be translated "saved" or "healed" or "made whole." They are all describing the same reality.

The gospel is the good news that God has done this for us through Jesus Christ. First Corinthians 15 summarizes it: Christ died for our sins, was buried, and rose again on the third day. That is the rescue mission. Jesus went into the burning building of our sin and death, carried us out, and rose to prove the rescue was complete.

You do not save yourself. You do not help with the rescue. You are the one being rescued. All you do is stop fighting the rescuer and let Him carry you out.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- 1 Corinthians 15:1-4 — "Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, and that He was buried, and that He rose again the third day according to the Scriptures." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Acts 4:12 — "Nor is there salvation in any other, for there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 10:9-10 — "If you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, take a piece of paper and write the word "SALVATION" at the top. Under it, write down every word that comes to your mind when you think about what salvation means — rescue, forgiveness, new life, freedom, whatever God brings to your heart. Keep the paper where you will see it and add to it throughout the week.

### SUMMARY
- Salvation is a rescue from sin and death that only God can accomplish.
- It includes not just going to heaven but being made whole in every part of your life.
- The gospel announces that God has accomplished this rescue through the death, burial, and resurrection of Jesus Christ.

## Lesson 2 — Why We Need to Be Saved

### HOOK
A person only cries out for a rescue when they know they are trapped. The difficulty is that most people do not realize how desperate their situation really is. They think they are fine, or at least fine enough. But the Bible paints a very different picture of the human condition.

### CORE TEACHING
To understand why we need salvation, we need to understand two things: who God is and who we are. God is holy — completely set apart from sin, perfectly pure, absolutely just. He cannot overlook sin, ignore it, or pretend it does not matter. His holiness means He must deal with sin. His justice means He must punish it.

And here is where the problem becomes clear. Every one of us has sinned. The Bible says, "All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God" (Romans 3:23). This is not about comparing ourselves to other people. You may be a better person than your neighbor, but that is not the standard. The standard is the perfect holiness of God, and compared to that, every person falls short.

Sin is not just bad behavior. It is rebellion against the Creator. It is the creature saying to the King, "I will live my way, not Yours." And the penalty for that rebellion is spiritual death — separation from God. Not just physical death, but an eternal separation from the source of all life and goodness.

The human situation is worse than a drowning man who cannot swim. It is like a dead man who cannot even call for help. Apart from God's intervention, people are spiritually dead, unable to save themselves, unable even to want to be saved. The Bible says the natural mind is hostile toward God and does not submit to His law (Romans 8:7).

This is why salvation must come from outside of us. If salvation depended on human effort, no one would be saved. If it depended on being good enough, heaven would be empty. The bad news prepares us to appreciate the good news. The depth of our need magnifies the greatness of God's grace.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Romans 3:10-12 — "There is none righteous, no, not one; there is none who understands; there is none who seeks after God. They have all turned aside." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 3:23 — "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 6:23 — "For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, read Romans chapters 1 through 3 slowly over several days. As you read, write down every verse that describes the human condition apart from God. At the end, summarize in one sentence why you personally needed a Savior.

### SUMMARY
- God is holy and just — He cannot ignore or overlook sin.
- Every person has sinned and falls short of God's standard, making them spiritually dead and unable to save themselves.
- Understanding the depth of our need helps us appreciate the greatness of God's grace.

## Lesson 3 — Grace: The Unearned Gift

### HOOK
Think about the last time someone gave you something you knew you did not deserve. Maybe they forgave you when you had no right to ask for forgiveness. Maybe they helped you when you had nothing to offer in return. That feeling — the mixture of gratitude and unworthiness — is a faint echo of what grace really means.

### CORE TEACHING
Grace is one of the most misunderstood words in Christianity. Many people think grace means God is lenient, that He overlooks sin, or that He lowers His standards so that we can meet them. None of that is true. Grace is not God relaxing His standards. Grace is God meeting His own standards on our behalf.

A simple definition helps: mercy is not getting what you deserve; grace is getting what you do not deserve. We deserve judgment. Grace gives us forgiveness. We deserve separation. Grace gives us adoption. We deserve death. Grace gives us life.

The most famous verse about grace is Ephesians 2:8-9: "For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast." Paul could not have been clearer. Salvation is a gift. You do not work for a gift. You do not earn a gift. You do not deserve a gift. You receive it.

This is hard for the human heart to accept because we are wired to earn things. From childhood, we are told that if we work hard, we get rewarded. But the gospel turns that upside down. You do not work to be saved; you are saved, and then you work out of gratitude.

Imagine you owe a debt you cannot pay — millions of dollars. The creditor says, "I will cancel your debt, and I will deposit enough money in your account to cover all your future needs." You do not pay a penny. You do not sign a payment plan. You simply accept the gift. That is grace.

Grace is also a teacher. Paul says grace "teaches us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly in the present age" (Titus 2:11-12). Grace does not give us permission to sin. It liberates us from the love of sin and empowers us to live for God.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Ephesians 2:8-9 — "For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 5:8 — "But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Titus 2:11-12 — "For the grace of God that brings salvation has appeared to all men, teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly in the present age." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, take a small object — a coin, a stone, or any small item — and put it in your pocket. Every time you feel it, say to yourself, "Saved by grace. Nothing in my hands I bring." Let it remind you throughout the day that your salvation is a gift, not something you earned.

### SUMMARY
- Grace is not God lowering His standards but meeting them Himself on our behalf.
- Salvation is a free gift received through faith, not earned by works.
- Grace frees us from trying to earn God's favor and teaches us to live for Him out of gratitude.

## Lesson 4 — Justification: Declared Righteous

### HOOK
Imagine standing before a judge, guilty of a crime that carries a life sentence. The evidence is overwhelming. The verdict is certain. Then the judge steps down from the bench, takes off his robe, and pays your fine himself. You walk out of the courtroom free — not because justice was ignored, but because someone else bore the penalty.

### CORE TEACHING
Justification is the legal declaration by God that a sinner is righteous. It happens the moment a person places their faith in Jesus Christ. God does not make the sinner righteous in character instantly — that is sanctification, which happens over a lifetime. Justification is a one-time legal declaration that changes the sinner's standing before God forever.

To understand justification, we need to understand what God cannot do. He cannot simply declare a guilty person innocent without compromising His justice. The Old Testament says, "He who justifies the wicked, and he who condemns the just, both of them alike are an abomination to the Lord" (Proverbs 17:15). A judge who declares a guilty criminal innocent is corrupt. So how can God, who is perfectly just, declare guilty sinners righteous?

The answer is the cross. On the cross, sin was dealt with fully. Our sin was placed upon Christ. He bore the penalty that we deserved. Justice was served — fully, completely, finally. Because Christ paid the debt, God can now declare the believing sinner righteous and remain perfectly just in doing so. As Paul puts it, God is "just and the justifier of the one who has faith in Jesus" (Romans 3:26).

Justification has two sides. First, our sin is imputed — credited — to Christ. He who knew no sin became sin for us (2 Corinthians 5:21). Second, His righteousness is imputed to us. We are not made righteous by our own efforts; we are given Christ's righteousness as a gift. When God looks at a justified sinner, He sees the perfect righteousness of His Son.

This means justification is not something you work toward. It is something you receive the moment you believe. It cannot be lost, undone, or reversed. If you are in Christ, you stand before God forever declared righteous.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Romans 3:21-26 — God demonstrates His righteousness so that He might be "just and the justifier of the one who has faith in Jesus." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 5:1 — "Therefore, having been justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 2 Corinthians 5:21 — "For He made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us, that we might become the righteousness of God in Him." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write Romans 5:1 on a note card and place it where you will see it every morning. "Having been justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ." Each time you read it, remind yourself: because of Christ, I stand before God at peace, not condemned.

### SUMMARY
- Justification is the legal declaration that a sinner is righteous through faith in Christ.
- God remains just because the penalty for sin was fully paid by Christ on the cross.
- Our sin was credited to Christ; His righteousness is credited to us — a complete exchange.

## Lesson 5 — Repentance: Turning from Sin

### HOOK
A man driving toward a cliff will keep going until he realizes his direction is wrong. The moment he understands the danger, he does not just feel afraid — he turns the car around. That is repentance. It is not merely feeling bad about sin. It is changing direction.

### CORE TEACHING
Repentance is one of the most misunderstood words in the Christian vocabulary. Many people think it means feeling sorry for sin. Others think it means punishing yourself or making promises you cannot keep. Biblical repentance is simpler and deeper. It means changing your mind — your mind about sin, about God, and about yourself — and that change of mind results in a change of direction.

In the original language, repentance means a change of mind that leads to a change of action. When you truly repent, you do not just regret your sin; you turn away from it. You stop running toward the cliff and start running in the opposite direction.

Think about the apostle Paul before his conversion. He genuinely believed he was doing the right thing by persecuting Christians. He thought Jesus was a false prophet and that Christians were enemies of God. Then on the road to Damascus, Jesus appeared to him. In that moment, his mind changed about everything. Everything he thought was true was wrong. Everything he thought was important was worthless. He did not just feel bad about his sin. He completely changed direction. He went from hunting Christians to preaching Christ.

This is what repentance looks like. It is not a one-time emotional experience. It is a fundamental reorientation of your life. The person who repents no longer makes peace with sin. They no longer excuse it, hide it, or cling to it. They turn from it and turn to God.

Repentance and faith are two sides of the same coin. You cannot have one without the other. To turn to Christ is to turn away from sin. To trust in Christ is to abandon trust in yourself. John the Baptist preached repentance. Jesus preached repentance. Peter preached repentance. Paul preached repentance. It is not optional. It is the doorway into the kingdom.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Acts 3:19 — "Repent therefore and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out, so that times of refreshing may come from the presence of the Lord." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Acts 20:21 — Paul summarizes his message as "repentance toward God and faith toward our Lord Jesus Christ." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 2 Corinthians 7:10 — "For godly sorrow produces repentance leading to salvation, not to be regretted; but the sorrow of the world produces death." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, identify one specific sin you have been making excuses for — something you know displeases God but have been unwilling to address. Write it down. Then write a specific, practical step you will take this week to turn away from it. Do not just feel sorry about it. Take action.

### SUMMARY
- Repentance is a change of mind about sin that leads to a change of direction.
- It is not optional — it is the doorway to salvation and the ongoing posture of the Christian life.
- Genuine repentance always produces fruit — a changed life that demonstrates the change of heart.

## Lesson 6 — Faith: Trusting in Christ Alone

### HOOK
Imagine you are on a sinking ship. The lifeboat has been lowered. A rescuer calls to you, "Jump! I will catch you." You can stand on the deck and analyze the rescuer's qualifications, study the lifeboat's design, and calculate the distance to the water. But none of that saves you. You are saved only when you stop analyzing and jump.

### CORE TEACHING
Faith is the hand that receives the gift of salvation. Ephesians 2:8-9 says we are saved by grace through faith. Grace is what God gives. Faith is how we receive it. But what exactly is faith?

Biblical faith is not believing something in the absence of evidence. It is not a blind leap in the dark. Faith is trust. It is confidence that what God has said is true and that Christ is sufficient to save. It is relying on His finished work rather than your own performance.

Many people confuse faith with intellectual agreement. They say, "I believe Jesus died for my sins," in the same way they would say, "I believe the earth is round." But agreeing with a fact is not saving faith. Even the demons believe that God exists — and they shudder (James 2:19). Believing facts about Jesus is not the same as trusting in Jesus.

Saving faith has three elements. First, knowledge — you must know the facts of the gospel: Christ died, was buried, and rose again. Second, you must be convinced that those facts are true. Third, you must personally trust in Christ alone for your salvation. The first two without the third leave you lost.

Think of it like a chair. You can know the chair was built well. You can be convinced it will hold your weight. But you are not actually sitting until you put your full weight on it. Saving faith is putting your full weight on Christ. It is saying with your whole heart, "I have nothing to offer. I have no righteousness of my own. I am entirely depending on what Jesus did for me."

This kind of faith transforms a person. When you truly trust Christ, your affections change. Sin that you once loved becomes distasteful. Christ that you once ignored becomes precious. You do not trust in your faith; you trust in Christ. Your faith is only as strong as its object, and its object is Jesus Christ.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Hebrews 11:1 — "Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 10:17 — "So then faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- James 2:17 — "Thus also faith by itself, if it does not have works, is dead." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, take five minutes each day to say this to God out loud: "I have nothing in my hands to bring to You. I am trusting in Christ alone — not my good deeds, not my religious background, not my efforts. He is my only hope." Let the words sink into your heart until you feel the weight of your dependence on Him.

### SUMMARY
- Faith is not agreeing with facts; it is personally trusting in Christ alone for salvation.
- Saving faith includes knowledge of the gospel, conviction that it is true, and personal dependence on Christ.
- Faith transforms a person — a genuine trust in Christ always produces a changed life.

## Lesson 7 — Assurance of Salvation: Knowing You Are Saved

### HOOK
A child who has been adopted into a loving family does not spend every day wondering if they really belong. They may have questions at first, but over time, the love and security of the family becomes more real than their doubts. God wants His children to have that same settled confidence about their salvation.

### CORE TEACHING
Assurance of salvation is the confidence that you are genuinely saved and that you will remain saved forever. It is not arrogance. It is not presumption. It is the gift of certainty that God wants every believer to have.

The Bible was written so that believers could have assurance. John says plainly: "These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life" (1 John 5:13). Notice the word "know." John did not say "hope" or "guess" or "feel like." He said "know." Assurance is based on the promises of God, not on feelings.

But how do you know if you are truly saved? The Bible gives several evidences. First, there is the internal witness of the Holy Spirit. "The Spirit Himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God" (Romans 8:16). There is a deep, inward confidence that comes from the Spirit.

Second, there is the evidence of a changed life. If you are in Christ, you are a new creation. Old things pass away. You may not be perfect, but you are not the same person you used to be. Sin that you once enjoyed now troubles you. Christ that you once ignored now matters to you. These are signs of genuine conversion.

Third, there is perseverance. Jesus said, "He who endures to the end shall be saved" (Matthew 24:13). True believers keep believing. They may stumble, they may fall, but they do not walk away permanently. God preserves His people, and His people persevere.

Assurance can be weakened by unconfessed sin, neglect of Scripture, or comparing yourself to others. When you feel doubt, do not look inward at your feelings. Look outward at Christ and the promises of God. Your feelings change. His promises do not. You are not saved because you feel saved. You are saved because Christ saved you, and He does not lose those He saves.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- 1 John 5:11-13 — "And this is the testimony: that God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son... These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Romans 8:16 — "The Spirit Himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Philippians 1:6 — "Being confident of this very thing, that He who has begun a good work in you will complete it until the day of Jesus Christ." (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write down 1 John 5:13 on a card and place it in your Bible. Then take five minutes to list three specific ways your life has changed since you began following Christ. When doubt creeps in, read the verse and look at your list. Let God's promises — not your feelings — be the anchor of your assurance.

### SUMMARY
- Assurance of salvation is not presumption; it is confidence based on God's promises.
- The Holy Spirit bears witness with our spirit, a changed life confirms the work of God, and perseverance proves the reality of faith.
- When doubt comes, look to Christ and His promises, not to your feelings.
