# Flashcards — Understanding Who You're Talking To

## Q&A Pairs

### Q1: Why is it important to understand who you are talking to before sharing the gospel?
**A1:** Every person has a unique background, heart condition, and level of spiritual awareness. Understanding these things allows you to tailor your approach so the truth reaches the heart instead of bouncing off pride, confusion, or false assumptions.

---

### Q2: What are the three broad categories of people you will encounter in evangelism?
**A2:** Nominal Christians (cultural Christians who have never truly repented), religious people (those devoted to another faith or system of works), and sceptics (atheists, agnostics, and those angry at God or the church).

---

### Q3: What is a nominal Christian?
**A3:** A nominal Christian is someone who identifies as Christian by culture, family tradition, or nationality but has no personal repentance, faith, or relationship with Jesus Christ. They think they are saved but show no evidence of conversion.

---

### Q4: According to the Parable of the Sower, what are the four types of soil (heart conditions)?
**A4:** The wayside (hard heart where the seed is snatched away), the stony ground (immediate joy but no root — falls away under trial), the thorny ground (choked by cares of the world and riches), and the good soil (hears, understands, and bears fruit).

---

### Q5: What three diagnostic questions help reveal a person's spiritual condition?
**A5:** (1) "Do you think there is an afterlife?" (2) "Do you consider yourself a good person?" (3) "Are you afraid of dying?" The answers reveal whether a person is proud, humble, self-righteous, or open.

---

### Q6: How do you identify a proud person in an evangelistic conversation?
**A6:** A proud person will typically deny or dismiss the afterlife, insist they are a good person, and show no fear of death. They are often argumentative and resistant to the idea that they need a Saviour.

---

### Q7: What is the difference between true conversion and false conversion?
**A7:** True conversion produces lasting fruit — repentance, thankfulness, good works, fruit of the Spirit, and righteousness. False conversion shows initial joy or belief but withers under tribulation, temptation, or persecution because there is no root of genuine repentance.

---

### Q8: What three things expose a false convert?
**A8:** Tribulation (trials and difficulties), temptation (opportunities to sin), and persecution (rejection for their faith). These are like sunlight that reveals whether the root is genuine.

---

### Q9: How should you tailor your approach for a proud sceptic versus a humble seeker?
**A9:** For a proud sceptic, use the law of God (the Ten Commandments) to expose their sin and strip away self-righteousness. For a humble seeker who already knows they are a sinner, move quickly to the grace of the cross and the free gift of salvation.

---

### Q10: What four signals should you watch for when reading your audience in real time?
**A10:** Defensiveness (back up and ask diagnostic questions), openness (move toward the cross), distraction (plant a seed and pray for later), and emotional response (discern whether it is conviction or discomfort).
