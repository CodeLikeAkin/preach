# Study Guide — Sharing Your Testimony

## 1. Core Definition

Sharing your testimony means giving a personal account of how you came to know Jesus Christ and how He has changed your life. It is not a biography of your sin or a platform for your accomplishments. A testimony is a tool — a bridge that connects your personal experience of God's grace to the unchanging truth of the gospel. The goal is not to make people admire your story but to make them want the Saviour your story points to. A well-crafted testimony has three movements: life before Christ, the moment of conversion, and life after Christ. Each movement serves a single purpose — to glorify God and make Christ visible.

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

- **Your testimony overcomes the enemy.** Revelation 12:11 declares that believers overcome Satan "by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of their testimony." Your story is not just encouraging — it is spiritual warfare. Every time you tell what God has done, you declare that His power is greater than the darkness you were rescued from. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Paul's testimony is the biblical model.** In Acts 26, Paul stands before King Agrippa and gives a full testimony: his religious background (verses 4-5), his persecution of Christians (verses 9-11), his encounter with Christ on the road to Damascus (verses 12-18), his obedience to the heavenly vision (verses 19-20), and his appeal for Agrippa to respond (verses 27-29). This structure — past life, conversion, transformed life, gospel appeal — is the pattern every believer can follow. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Conversion is a supernatural new creation.** Ezekiel 36:26-27 promises that God will remove a heart of stone and give a heart of flesh. Second Corinthians 5:17 confirms that anyone in Christ is a new creation. The change in your life is not self-improvement; it is divine regeneration. Your testimony should reflect that God did the work, not you. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Humility must mark your witness.** Proverbs 27:2 warns, "Let another man praise you, and not your own mouth." Your testimony should never sound like boasting. It is a report of what God has done, not a display of your spiritual resume. First Peter 3:15 instructs believers to give an answer with "meekness and fear" — gentle and reverent, not arrogant or pushy. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

## 3. Common Misconceptions

- **"My testimony isn't dramatic enough to matter."** Many believers think only former criminals or addicts have powerful testimonies. But the Bible says every person was dead in trespasses and sins (Ephesians 2:1). Whether God saved you from a life of visible rebellion or from quiet religious self-righteousness, the miracle is the same — a dead sinner made alive in Christ. Both are powerful.

- **"I should focus on how bad I was before."** Some believers spend too much time describing their past sin in detail. This can glorify the very thing God rescued you from and make people curious about the wrong things. The goal is to show the need for a Saviour, not to entertain with your former life. Be honest but brief.

- **"My testimony is about me."** The most common mistake is making yourself the hero of your own story. Your testimony is about Jesus. You are the witness, not the star. If people walk away impressed by you, you have missed the point. They should walk away wanting Him.

- **"I only need to share it once."** A testimony is not a one-time speech you prepare for a church event. It is a tool you use in everyday conversations with neighbours, coworkers, friends, and family. The more you share it, the more natural it becomes and the more God uses it.

## 4. Practical Application Angles

- **Craft your testimony in writing.** Write out your story in three parts — before Christ, the turning point, after Christ. Keep it to 300-500 words (roughly 3-5 minutes spoken). Edit ruthlessly. Cut anything that does not point to Jesus.

- **Practice with a trusted believer.** Share your written testimony aloud with another Christian and ask for honest feedback. Does it centre on Christ? Is it clear? Does it feel authentic? Use their input to refine it.

- **Identify your gospel bridge.** Every testimony needs a moment where you clearly connect your personal story to the death and resurrection of Jesus. This is the bridge from your experience to the universal truth of the gospel. Without it, people hear a nice story but miss the Saviour.

- **Prepare for different audiences.** You may need a longer version for a friend who is genuinely interested and a shorter version (60-90 seconds) for a casual conversation. Having both ready means you are never caught without an answer.

- **End with a question or an invitation.** Paul did not finish his testimony in Acts 26 without asking Agrippa a direct question. Your testimony should naturally lead to a response — not pressure, but an open door for the other person to consider Christ.

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Testimony** | A personal account of how God saved you through Jesus Christ and changed your life |
| **Before Christ** | The honest description of your life, attitudes, and spiritual state prior to conversion |
| **Conversion** | The moment when you repented of sin and put your faith in Jesus Christ for salvation |
| **After Christ** | The ongoing evidence of God's transforming work in your life since conversion |
| **Gospel Bridge** | The deliberate connection between your personal story and the death and resurrection of Jesus |
| **Brevity** | Keeping your testimony short enough (3-5 minutes) to hold attention and leave room for conversation |
| **Humility** | Telling your story in a way that gives glory to God, not attention to yourself |
| **Personal Appeal** | The natural question or invitation at the end of your testimony that turns your story into a conversation about the other person's relationship with God |
