# Flashcards — Sharing Your Testimony

## 10 Question & Answer Pairs

### Q1: What is a personal testimony in evangelism?
**A:** A personal testimony is your account of how you came to know Jesus Christ and how He has changed your life. It is a bridge that connects your experience of God's grace to the truth of the gospel.

### Q2: What are the three main parts of a testimony?
**A:** The three parts are life before Christ (your spiritual need), the moment of conversion (how you repented and believed), and life after Christ (the difference He has made). Some also add a fourth part: an invitation for the listener to respond.

### Q3: Why should the "before Christ" section be kept brief?
**A:** Because the goal is not to glorify sin or make yourself the centre of attention. A few honest sentences are enough to establish your need for a Saviour. The focus should remain on God's grace, not on the details of your past.

### Q4: What makes a testimony gospel-centred rather than self-centred?
**A:** A gospel-centred testimony keeps the spotlight on what God did, not on what you did. It presents conversion as God's work (new creation, new heart) and directs the listener's attention to the death and resurrection of Jesus, not to your personal growth.

### Q5: How does Paul's testimony in Acts 26 serve as a model?
**A:** Paul's testimony includes a gracious introduction, his past life (both good and bad), the circumstances of his conversion, the results of his conversion, a clear gospel message, and a direct personal appeal to his listener. It covers all the essential elements.

### Q6: What is a gospel bridge and why is it important?
**A:** A gospel bridge is a sentence or two that connects your personal story to the death and resurrection of Jesus. It is the moment you transition from "what God did for me" to "what God did for the whole world through Christ." Without it, your testimony remains a good story that does not lead to salvation.

### Q7: How long should a testimony be when shared in conversation?
**A:** A testimony should take no more than three to five minutes to deliver. This is long enough to share the essential elements but short enough to hold attention and leave room for the other person to respond or ask questions.

### Q8: Why is humility important when sharing your testimony?
**A:** Humility ensures that the listener sees Jesus, not you. Your testimony should sound like gratitude for what God has done, not pride in your transformation. First Peter 3:15 says to share with meekness and fear, not with arrogance or superiority.

### Q9: How should a testimony end?
**A:** A testimony should end with a question or invitation that turns your story into a conversation. Paul asked Agrippa directly, "Do you believe the prophets?" You might ask, "Has anything like that ever happened to you?" or "What do you think about Jesus?"

### Q10: What if my testimony is not dramatic?
**A:** Every testimony is powerful because every conversion is a miracle. Scripture says you were dead in trespasses and sins and God made you alive. Whether you were saved from visible rebellion or quiet self-righteousness, the miracle is the same — a dead sinner made alive in Christ.
