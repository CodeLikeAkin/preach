# Flashcards: Starting the Conversation

## Q&A Pairs

### Card 1
**Q: What is the single greatest barrier most believers face when trying to share their faith?**
**A:** The fear of the first sentence. Most believers know what they believe but freeze when it comes time to say the first words of a spiritual conversation.

---

### Card 2
**Q: What is the best way to overcome the fear of starting a spiritual conversation?**
**A:** Practice. The first time is terrifying, but after ten or twenty attempts, the brain learns that nothing catastrophic happens. Practicing on telemarketers or strangers you will not see again is a useful strategy.

---

### Card 3
**Q: What kind of openers are most effective for starting a spiritual conversation?**
**A:** Simple, natural openers that flow from the moment — an observation about a watch, a news headline, a beautiful sunset, or a comment about someone's child. The best openers feel like an extension of the conversation you are already having.

---

### Card 4
**Q: Why are diagnostic questions important in evangelism?**
**A:** Diagnostic questions like "What do you think happens after we die?" or "Do you have a spiritual background?" reveal a person's worldview, show respect for their perspective, and help you know where to go next in the conversation.

---

### Card 5
**Q: Why is listening more important than talking in evangelism?**
**A:** Listening communicates that the person matters, earns you the right to be heard in return, and reveals what the person actually believes. James 1:19 says we should be swift to hear and slow to speak [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

---

### Card 6
**Q: How can you naturally transition from an everyday topic to a spiritual one?**
**A:** Follow the trail the other person has already laid. If they mention death, ask about the afterlife. If they mention injustice, ask about ultimate justice. If they express gratitude, ask about the Giver. The conscience and universal human experiences are natural hinges.

---

### Card 7
**Q: What do the letters WDJD stand for?**
**A:** W — Would you consider yourself a good person? D — Do you think you have kept the Ten Commandments? J — Judgment: would you be innocent or guilty? D — Destiny: would you go to heaven or hell?

---

### Card 8
**Q: Why does the WDJD framework begin with a question about being a good person?**
**A:** Because most people measure themselves against other people and consider themselves good. Their answer reveals whether they are relying on their own goodness or whether they already understand their need for a Savior.

---

### Card 9
**Q: What is the role of the law in a gospel conversation?**
**A:** The law acts as a mirror — it does not clean the dirt but reveals it. By asking about specific commandments (lying, stealing, lust, blasphemy), the person's conscience is awakened to their true condition before a holy God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 3:19-20].

---

### Card 10
**Q: What is the ultimate motivation for starting spiritual conversations?**
**A:** Love — love for God and love for people. The love of Christ compels us [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 2 Corinthians 5:14]. Love casts out fear and frees us from the burden of results, because we sow the seed and God gives the increase.

---

## Memory Verse Drill

### Primary Verse (should be memorized word-perfect)
1 Peter 3:15 — "But sanctify the Lord God in your hearts, and always be ready to give a defense to everyone who asks you a reason for the hope that is in you, with meekness and fear." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Secondary Verse (familiarity target)
Colossians 4:6 — "Let your speech always be with grace, seasoned with salt, that you may know how you ought to answer each one." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Drill Instructions

1. **Week 1:** Memorize 1 Peter 3:15. Say it aloud ten times per day. Write it from memory each evening.
2. **Week 2:** Review 1 Peter 3:15 and add Colossians 4:6. Recite both in sequence daily.
3. **Week 3:** Practice quoting both verses from memory without looking. Then explain the meaning of each verse in your own words to another believer.
4. **Ongoing:** Review both verses weekly. Set a reminder to recite them before any planned evangelistic outing or conversation.
