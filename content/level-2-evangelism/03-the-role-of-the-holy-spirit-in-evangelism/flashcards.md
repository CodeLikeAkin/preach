# Flashcards: The Role of the Holy Spirit in Evangelism

## Q&A Pairs

### Card 1
**Q: Why is evangelism impossible without the Holy Spirit?**
**A:** Because no one can be saved through human effort, persuasion, or argument alone. The Holy Spirit is the one who convicts the world of sin, regenerates dead hearts, and opens people's eyes to the truth of the gospel. Our words are tools, but the Spirit is the power.

---

### Card 2
**Q: What does it mean that the Holy Spirit convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment?**
**A:** The Spirit exposes a person's guilt before God (sin), reveals their lack of the righteousness God requires (righteousness), and warns them of the coming judgment (judgment). This conviction is necessary preparation for the gospel — it makes a person aware of their need before they can receive the solution.

---

### Card 3
**Q: How does the Holy Spirit empower believers with boldness in evangelism?**
**A:** When believers pray for boldness and step out in faith, the Holy Spirit fills them with supernatural courage to speak. This is not the absence of fear but the strength to speak despite it. The early church prayed for boldness, and the Spirit answered by giving them the words and the courage to speak.

---

### Card 4
**Q: How does the Holy Spirit guide believers in evangelistic conversations?**
**A:** The Spirit leads believers through their human spirit — through an inward witness, a sense of peace or lack of it, and quiet prompting. The spirit of man is the lamp of the Lord. As believers learn to be sensitive to the Spirit's leading, they receive guidance on what to say, when to speak, and when to be silent.

---

### Card 5
**Q: Why does Jesus' promise of the Spirit as a permanent Helper matter for evangelism?**
**A:** Jesus promised that the Spirit would abide with believers forever, not merely visit occasionally. This means the Spirit is always present, always available, and always ready to work through us. We do not need to beg the Spirit to come — we need to acknowledge that He is already here and depend on Him.

---

### Card 6
**Q: How does prayer connect to the Holy Spirit's work in evangelism?**
**A:** Prayer is the engine of evangelism. Through prayer, we ask the Spirit to prepare hearts, open doors, and guide our words. The Spirit also helps us pray — both with understanding and by praying through us in the Spirit, which builds up the believer and brings guidance from within.

---

### Card 7
**Q: What changed between the Old Covenant and the New Covenant regarding the Spirit's presence?**
**A:** In the Old Covenant, the Spirit came upon specific people (priests, kings, prophets) for specific tasks, and the presence of God dwelt in the temple behind the veil. In the New Covenant, every believer is the temple of the Holy Spirit. God dwells in us individually and collectively, not in a building.

---

### Card 8
**Q: Who can be used by the Holy Spirit in evangelism?**
**A:** Every believer. The Spirit distributes gifts as He wills, but every born-again person has the Holy Spirit dwelling in them. You do not need to be a pastor, evangelist, or theologian. The Spirit works through ordinary, available people who are willing to open their mouths and trust Him.

---

### Card 9
**Q: What is the relationship between the believer and the Holy Spirit in evangelism?**
**A:** We are workers together with Him. The Holy Spirit does not do the speaking for us, but He speaks through us. He does not force words out of our mouths, but He gives utterance as we yield. He supplies the power; we supply the voice. We go, and He works through our going.

---

### Card 10
**Q: What happens when the Holy Spirit opens someone's heart to the gospel?**
**A:** The Spirit does the supernatural work of regeneration — making a spiritually dead person alive in Christ. The person who was blind to the truth suddenly sees. The cross becomes the power of God to them. They respond with repentance and faith, not because they were argued into it, but because the Spirit gave them new life and opened their eyes.

---

## Memory Verse Drill

### Primary verse
Acts 1:8 — "But you shall receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you; and you shall be witnesses to Me in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Secondary verse
John 16:8 — "And when He has come, He will convict the world of sin, and of righteousness, and of judgment." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Drill Instructions
1. **Week 1:** Memorize Acts 1:8 word-perfect. Say it aloud ten times per day.
2. **Week 2:** Review Acts 1:8 and add John 16:8. Recite both in sequence daily.
3. **Week 3:** Write both verses from memory once per day. Check against the NKJV text.
4. **Week 4:** Recite both verses from memory without looking. Add 1 Corinthians 6:19 and Romans 8:14 for ongoing review.
