# Lessons: The Role of the Holy Spirit in Evangelism

---

## Lesson 1: Evangelism Is Impossible Without the Spirit

### Hook
Imagine trying to start a fire with wet wood and no matches. You can arrange the logs perfectly, stack them just so, and blow on them until you are dizzy — but without a flame, nothing will catch. That is what evangelism looks like when we try to do it in our own strength. We can arrange all the right words and present all the right arguments, but only the Holy Spirit can light the fire.

### Core Teaching
There is a fundamental truth that every believer must grasp before they can be effective in evangelism: you cannot save anyone, and you cannot convince anyone. That work belongs to the Holy Spirit alone. This is not a discouraging thought — it is liberating. The pressure is off your shoulders and on the Spirit, where it belongs. Jesus made this clear when He told His disciples to wait in Jerusalem until they received power from on high. He did not send them out immediately after His resurrection. He told them to wait. Why? Because they were not ready. They had been with Him for three years. They had seen His miracles. They had heard His teaching. But they did not yet have the power they needed to be His witnesses from Jerusalem to the ends of the earth. That power came when the Holy Spirit descended at Pentecost, and everything changed. Before Pentecost, Peter denied Christ before a servant girl. After Pentecost, the same Peter stood before thousands and preached with a boldness that landed him in prison. The difference was not better training or a better outline. The difference was the Holy Spirit. You carry the same Spirit inside you right now. The same power that raised Christ from the dead lives in you. When you share your faith, you are not going alone — you are going with the resident presence of Almighty God living inside you. This is the foundation of all evangelism: it is not your words, your persuasion, or your courage that matters most. It is the Spirit of God working through you. He convicts. He opens eyes. He regenerates dead hearts. Your job is simply to speak and trust Him to do what only He can do.

### Key Scriptures
- Acts 1:8 — "But you shall receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you; and you shall be witnesses to Me in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Zechariah 4:6 — "'Not by might nor by power, but by My Spirit,' says the LORD of hosts." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- John 15:5 — "I am the vine, you are the branches. He who abides in Me, and I in him, bears much fruit; for without Me you can do nothing." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Practical Application
This week, before you speak to anyone about Christ, pause and pray this exact prayer aloud: "Holy Spirit, I cannot convince anyone of anything. I am depending on You to do what only You can do. Open their ears. Open their heart. Give me the right words at the right time." Pray this before you leave your home each morning, and before any conversation where you sense an opportunity to share your faith. Keep a journal of how many times you pray this prayer and note what happens each time.

### Summary
- Evangelism is a supernatural work that requires the Holy Spirit's power, not human ability.
- Jesus commanded His disciples to wait for the Spirit before they began their witness.
- The same Spirit who empowered the early church lives in every believer today and is ready to work through you.

## Lesson 2: The Spirit's Work of Conviction

### Hook
Have you ever had a moment where something you had been ignoring suddenly became impossible to ignore? Maybe it was a health symptom you had been brushing off, or a problem in a relationship you had been pretending was fine. Suddenly the truth was right there in front of you, and you could not look away. That unsettling clarity — that moment when denial crumbles and the truth becomes unbearable — is a faint echo of what the Holy Spirit does in the heart of someone He is drawing to Christ.

### Core Teaching
Jesus promised that when the Holy Spirit came, He would convict the world of sin, of righteousness, and of judgment. This is one of the most important truths about evangelism that you must understand. Conviction is the Holy Spirit's work of opening a person's eyes to their true spiritual condition. Before the Spirit works, a person can hear the gospel a hundred times and it will bounce off them like water off stone. They may agree intellectually that Jesus died on a cross. They may even believe that God exists. But they do not feel the weight of their sin. They do not see their need for a Savior. Something is missing. What is missing is conviction. The Holy Spirit takes the truth you speak and drives it like an arrow into the conscience. He makes the sinner feel, for the first time, the reality of their guilt before a holy God. This is not a pleasant feeling. It is an uncomfortable, unsettling, disturbing work. But it is necessary. A person who has never been convicted of sin has never truly understood the gospel. The Spirit also convicts of righteousness — not their own righteousness, but Christ's righteousness, which they lack. A person begins to realize that compared to God's standard, they do not measure up and never will on their own. And the Spirit convicts of judgment — the sobering reality that there is a day coming when every person will give an account. This conviction is a gift. It is the Spirit's way of preparing a person to receive the good news. Without it, the gospel sounds like a solution to a problem the person does not believe they have. When you share your faith, you may not always see the Spirit's convicting work happening. It often takes place beneath the surface, like roots growing underground. But you can trust that as you faithfully speak the truth, the Spirit is working. Your part is to speak. His part is to convict.

### Key Scriptures
- John 16:8 — "And when He has come, He will convict the world of sin, and of righteousness, and of judgment." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 1 Corinthians 14:24-25 — "And thus the secrets of his heart are revealed; and so, falling down on his face, he will worship God and report that God is truly among you." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Acts 2:37 — "Now when they heard this, they were cut to the heart, and said to Peter and the rest of the apostles, 'Men and brethren, what shall we do?'" [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Practical Application
This week, choose one person you know who does not yet know Christ. Write their name on a card. Every day this week, pray specifically: "Holy Spirit, please convict [name] of sin, of righteousness, and of judgment. Do whatever it takes to open their eyes to the truth." Do not try to share the gospel with them yet unless the Spirit clearly leads. Simply pray and watch for evidence that the Spirit is working in their heart. Write down anything they say or do that suggests the Spirit may be stirring.

### Summary
- Conviction is the Holy Spirit's work of making a person aware of their sin, their need for Christ's righteousness, and the reality of coming judgment.
- Without conviction, the gospel will never feel like good news because the person does not know they are in danger.
- Your job is to speak the truth faithfully; the Spirit's job is to drive it home in the conscience.

## Lesson 3: The Spirit Empowers Boldness in Witness

### Hook
Think of the most terrifying thing you could possibly be asked to do. For many people, speaking to a stranger about something deeply personal ranks near the top of the list. Now imagine doing that when you know the person might reject you, mock you, or get angry with you. The natural response is fear. But there is a supernatural response available to every believer, and it is called boldness.

### Core Teaching
The early church prayed for boldness, and God answered by filling them with the Holy Spirit. This was not a one-time event for a special group of people. It is a pattern for every believer in every generation. When the Holy Spirit fills a person, fear loses its grip. This does not mean you will never feel nervous. The apostles certainly faced opposition, threats, and imprisonment. But they spoke anyway. Something inside them was stronger than the fear outside them. That something was the Holy Spirit. The same Spirit who raised Christ from the dead lives inside you. When you open your mouth to speak for Christ, you are not speaking alone. The Spirit who empowered Peter to preach to thousands on the day of Pentecost is the same Spirit empowering you in your conversation with one person over coffee. Consider how God works: the Holy Spirit does not typically appear visibly and speak to the lost directly. He works through believers. As one teacher put it, you never read in Scripture where the Holy Spirit met someone on the street and healed them. You read where men and women full of the Holy Spirit did. We are workers together with Him. The Spirit supplies the power; we supply the voice. This partnership is why boldness matters. If you remain silent because of fear, the Spirit's power is not being released through you. But when you speak despite your fear, you discover that the Spirit rises up within you and gives you words you did not plan and courage you did not have. Boldness is not the absence of fear. Boldness is speaking anyway because the One in you is greater than the one opposing you. Every time you open your mouth for Christ, you are stepping into a supernatural partnership with the Holy Spirit.

### Key Scriptures
- Acts 4:31 — "And when they had prayed, the place where they were assembled together was shaken; and they were all filled with the Holy Spirit, and they spoke the word of God with boldness." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 2 Timothy 1:7 — "For God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power and of love and of a sound mind." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Acts 4:13 — "Now when they saw the boldness of Peter and John, and perceived that they were uneducated and untrained men, they marveled. And they realized that they had been with Jesus." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Practical Application
This week, identify one situation where you tend to stay silent about your faith out of fear — perhaps with a coworker, a family member, or a neighbor. Write down exactly what you are afraid of (rejection, looking foolish, damaging the relationship, etc.). Then write a one-sentence prayer asking the Holy Spirit for boldness in that specific situation. Sometime this week, place yourself in that situation intentionally and speak one sentence about what Christ means to you. Afterward, write down what happened and how you felt the Spirit helping you.

### Summary
- Boldness is a supernatural gift of the Holy Spirit that empowers believers to speak despite fear.
- The Holy Spirit works through believers, not apart from them — our voice and His power working together.
- When we pray for boldness and step out in faith, the Spirit meets us and gives us the words and courage we need.

## Lesson 4: Being Led by the Spirit in Evangelistic Encounters

### Hook
Imagine setting out on a journey through a dense forest with no map, no guide, and no path. Every direction looks the same, and every step could lead you further into danger. Now imagine you have a guide who knows every tree and every trail, who has walked this forest a thousand times, and who is with you every step of the way. The only question is whether you will listen to His directions.

### Core Teaching
One of the most practical ways the Holy Spirit helps us in evangelism is by guiding us in the moment. Scripture says that as many as are led by the Spirit of God are sons of God, and the spirit of man is the lamp of the Lord. This means that God guides us through our human spirits. He leads us through an inward knowing, a sense of peace or a lack of it, a quiet prompting that tells us what to do. In evangelism, this guidance is invaluable. The Spirit knows exactly what the person you are speaking to needs to hear. He knows whether they need truth or tenderness, whether they need the law to expose their sin or grace to draw them in. He knows when to press further and when to stop. Learning to follow the Spirit's leading in conversations is a skill developed over time. It begins with a simple practice: before you speak, pause inwardly and ask the Spirit to guide you. As you speak, pay attention to what rises up inside you. Sometimes a Scripture will come to mind that you had not planned to share. Sometimes you will feel a check in your spirit that says, do not say that. Sometimes you will sense an urgency to speak when you would rather stay silent. The more you practice this, the more natural it becomes. The Spirit does not usually shout. He leads through a quiet inward witness. This is why it is important to cultivate sensitivity to your own spirit. Spend time in prayer. Spend time in the Word. Learn to distinguish the voice of the Spirit from your own thoughts and emotions. Being led by the Spirit does not mean you will never make mistakes. You will. But it means you are learning to walk in a relationship where the Spirit directs your steps. Every evangelistic conversation becomes an adventure when you are following the Spirit's leading.

### Key Scriptures
- Romans 8:14 — "For as many as are led by the Spirit of God, these are sons of God." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Proverbs 20:27 — "The spirit of a man is the lamp of the LORD, searching all the inward parts of his being." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- John 10:27 — "My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Practical Application
This week, practice pausing before every conversation you have — whether with a family member, a colleague, or a stranger. Take a silent three-second breath and pray inwardly: "Holy Spirit, guide my words." After each conversation, reflect: Did any words come to mind that surprised you? Did you feel a check about something you almost said? Did you sense the Spirit prompting you to speak or to stay silent? Write down one observation at the end of each day.

### Summary
- The Holy Spirit leads believers through their human spirit, using inward witness, peace, and prompting to guide.
- In evangelism, the Spirit knows exactly what each person needs to hear and when they need to hear it.
- Learning to be led by the Spirit in conversations requires practice, prayer, and growing sensitivity to His quiet voice.

## Lesson 5: Prayer Dependence in Evangelism

### Hook
Imagine you are about to give a gift to someone, but you are not sure what they need. You could guess. You could give them what you would want. But you would rather ask someone who knows them perfectly what they actually need. In evangelism, we have that Someone. The Holy Spirit knows every heart perfectly, and when we pray, we tap into His wisdom and His power for the people we are reaching.

### Core Teaching
Prayer and evangelism belong together. The early church did not separate the two. They prayed for boldness, and the Spirit filled them. They prayed for the lost, and the Spirit opened hearts. The Spirit helps us pray in two significant ways. First, He helps us pray with understanding — giving us words and burdens for specific people. Second, He helps us pray in the Spirit, which includes praying in tongues as the Spirit gives utterance. The apostle Paul said that when he prayed in an unknown tongue, his spirit prayed but his understanding was unfruitful. This kind of prayer builds up the believer on the inside and opens channels for the Spirit's guidance. Many believers who are actively reaching the lost have found that praying in the Spirit during times of intercession brings clarity and direction. While they are praying, insight rises up from deep within their spirit — a Scripture, a name, a strategy that they had not considered. This is the spirit of man functioning as the lamp of the Lord. The Spirit illuminates the believer's spirit with what they need to know. Prayer also prepares the ground before you ever speak to someone. You can pray for a person for weeks or months before you have a conversation with them. You can ask the Spirit to go ahead of you, to soften their heart, to create opportunities, to remove obstacles. When you finally do speak, you are walking into a situation where the Spirit has already been working. This is not manipulation. It is cooperation with the Spirit, who is already at work in the world drawing people to Christ. Prayer is not a ritual you perform before evangelism. It is the engine of evangelism.

### Key Scriptures
- Romans 8:26-27 — "Likewise the Spirit also helps in our weaknesses. For we do not know what we should pray for as we ought, but the Spirit Himself makes intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 1 Corinthians 14:14-15 — "For if I pray in a tongue, my spirit prays, but my understanding is unfruitful. What is the result then? I will pray with the spirit, and I will also pray with the understanding." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Ephesians 6:18 — "Praying always with all prayer and supplication in the Spirit, being watchful to this end with all perseverance and supplication for all the saints." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Practical Application
This week, set aside fifteen minutes each day to pray specifically for people you know who do not yet know Christ. Use a list of three to five names. Spend the first five minutes praying for each person in your own words — ask the Spirit to open their eyes, prepare their heart, and create opportunities. Spend the next five minutes praying in the Spirit, letting the Spirit pray through you. Spend the final five minutes in silence before God, asking: "Holy Spirit, is there anything You want me to do or say to any of these people this week?" Write down anything that comes to mind.

### Summary
- Prayer is essential for evangelism because the Spirit uses prayer to prepare hearts, guide believers, and release power.
- Praying in the Spirit builds up the believer and opens channels for the Spirit's guidance and insight.
- When we pray before we speak, we walk into situations where the Spirit has already been working.

## Lesson 6: The Spirit Works Through Ordinary Believers

### Hook
A young child who has just learned to swim can still save a drowning person if a lifeguard jumps in and guides their hands. Alone, the child would fail. But with a strong partner doing the real work, even the weakest helper can be part of a rescue. This is how the Holy Spirit works through you. You are not the rescuer. He is. But He chooses to use your voice, your hands, and your presence to accomplish His work.

### Core Teaching
One of the most encouraging truths about the Holy Spirit's role in evangelism is that He works through ordinary believers. You do not need to be a pastor, an evangelist, or a theological expert. You need to be a believer filled with the Spirit, willing to open your mouth. The Spirit distributes gifts and manifestations as He wills, but every believer has the Spirit residing in them. This means every believer can be used in evangelism. In the Old Testament, the Spirit came upon specific people for specific tasks — priests, kings, and prophets. The ordinary Israelite did not have the Spirit dwelling in them. But under the New Covenant, everything changed. When Christ died, the veil of the temple was torn from top to bottom. The presence of God moved out of the earthly holy of holies and took up residence in the bodies of believers. You are now the temple of the living God. God dwells in you. This is not a metaphor. It is a reality. Because the Spirit lives in you, you have everything you need for the evangelistic encounters you face. You do not need to beg God to show up — He is already in you. You do not need to pray for the Spirit to come — He is already abiding. What you need to do is acknowledge His presence, depend on His power, and open your mouth. The Spirit does not do the speaking for you. You speak, and He gives the words. You go, and He works through your going. You are a worker together with Him. This partnership is the heart of New Testament evangelism. The Spirit is not looking for impressive people. He is looking for available people. If you have been born again, you qualify. The greater One lives in you. That is enough.

### Key Scriptures
- 2 Corinthians 6:16 — "For you are the temple of the living God. As God has said: 'I will dwell in them and walk among them. I will be their God, and they shall be My people.'" [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 1 Corinthians 3:16 — "Do you not know that you are the temple of God and that the Spirit of God dwells in you?" [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 1 John 4:4 — "He who is in you is greater than he who is in the world." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 1 Corinthians 12:11 — "But one and the same Spirit works all these things, distributing to each one individually as He wills." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Practical Application
This week, write down three things you believe disqualify you from being used in evangelism (for example: I am too young, I do not know enough Scripture, I am too shy, I have a past of sin). Then, next to each one, write a truth from Scripture that contradicts that disqualification. For example, next to "I am too young," write 1 Timothy 4:12. Next to "I do not know enough," write Acts 4:13. Read your list aloud each morning and then thank the Holy Spirit that He chooses to work through you anyway. Ask Him for one specific opportunity to speak for Him this week.

### Summary
- Every believer is the temple of the Holy Spirit and has the resident presence of God living in them.
- The Spirit works through ordinary, available believers — not just trained ministers or spiritual elites.
- Greater is the Spirit who is in you than any force opposing you in evangelism.

## Lesson 7: The Spirit Opens Hearts to the Gospel

### Hook
You can stand outside a locked door all day, knocking and calling, but you will never enter until someone opens it from the inside. This is the condition of every human heart before the Spirit works. The gospel can be proclaimed clearly, passionately, and accurately, but if the Spirit does not open the heart, the message will never enter. The most important work of all happens behind the closed door of the human soul.

### Core Teaching
The ultimate reason anyone is saved is that the Holy Spirit opens their heart to receive the gospel. This is the culminating work of the Spirit in evangelism. He convicts of sin. He empowers the witness. He guides the conversation. He helps in prayer. But at the moment of conversion, He does something that only He can do: He regenerates a dead spirit and gives new life. Jesus told Nicodemus that unless a person is born of water and the Spirit, they cannot enter the kingdom of God. The new birth is not something a person achieves by making a decision. It is something the Spirit does in response to faith. When the Spirit opens a person's heart, they suddenly see the truth they were blind to. The cross, which seemed like foolishness, becomes the power of God. Christ, who was a distant religious figure, becomes a living Savior. The weight of sin that they were carrying becomes the very thing they want to leave at the cross. This is the work of the Spirit. When the Spirit opens the heart, the response of repentance and faith becomes not just possible but natural. The person does not need to be argued into the kingdom. They need to be brought to life. As one source put it, the Holy Spirit recreates the human spirit in the new birth. It is not reformation. It is resurrection. A person who was spiritually dead becomes spiritually alive. This is why you do not need to pressure people or manipulate them into a decision. You present the truth. You pray. You trust the Spirit to do the opening. The same Spirit who opened Lydia's heart by the riverside in Philippi is still opening hearts today. The same Spirit who brought the Ethiopian eunuch to faith through Philip's explanation of Isaiah is still guiding conversations. Your part is to be faithful and available. The Spirit's part is to open hearts and give new life. When you understand this, evangelism becomes less about your performance and more about your participation in what the Spirit is already doing.

### Key Scriptures
- John 3:5-6 — "Jesus answered, 'Most assuredly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God. That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit.'" [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Acts 16:14 — "Now a certain woman named Lydia heard us. She was a seller of purple from the city of Thyatira, who worshiped God. The Lord opened her heart to heed the things spoken by Paul." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- 1 Corinthians 2:14 — "But the natural man does not receive the things of the Spirit of God, for they are foolishness to him; nor can he know them, because they are spiritually discerned." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- Ezekiel 36:26-27 — "I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit within you; I will take the heart of stone out of your flesh and give you a heart of flesh. I will put My Spirit within you and cause you to walk in My statutes." [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### Practical Application
This week, identify one person you have been praying for who has not yet responded to the gospel. Write down their name and the specific things you have already said to them about Christ. Then write this prayer: "Holy Spirit, only You can open this heart. I ask You to do what I cannot do. Open their eyes. Open their understanding. Give them new life. I trust You to do the work in Your timing." Pray this prayer every day this week, and ask the Spirit to show you when and how to speak to this person next. If the Spirit prompts you, reach out to them before the week ends.

### Summary
- The new birth is a supernatural work of the Holy Spirit, not a human achievement or decision.
- The Spirit opens hearts to receive the gospel in a way that no argument or persuasion can accomplish.
- Our job is to faithfully present the truth and trust the Spirit to do the opening and the regenerating.
