# Study Guide: The Role of the Holy Spirit in Evangelism

## 1. Core Definition

The Holy Spirit is the divine agent who makes evangelism possible. Without His work, no one would be saved and no one could effectively witness. Jesus promised the Spirit would empower His followers to be witnesses, and it is the Spirit who convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment. The Holy Spirit dwells in every believer, making our bodies His temple, and He works through us to reach the lost. Evangelism is not a human project that God blesses — it is a divine project in which God works through yielded believers. The Spirit prepares hearts before we speak, empowers our words as we speak, and applies the truth after we speak. From start to finish, evangelism depends on the Holy Spirit.

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

### A. The Spirit Indwells Every Believer
- The believer's body is the temple of the Holy Spirit who dwells in us [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 Corinthians 6:19]
- The Spirit of God dwells in the church collectively and in each believer individually [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 Corinthians 3:16]
- Greater is He who is in us than he who is in the world [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 John 4:4]
- The Spirit makes His home in the believer permanently [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — John 14:16-17]

### B. The Spirit's Role in Conviction
- The Spirit convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — John 16:8]
- The Spirit bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 8:16]

### C. The Spirit Empowers Witness
- Jesus promised the Spirit would empower His followers to be witnesses [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Acts 1:8]
- The Spirit gives utterance and boldness in speaking God's word [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Acts 4:31]

### D. The Spirit Guides Believers
- As many as are led by the Spirit of God are sons of God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 8:14]
- The spirit of man is the lamp of the Lord, searching the inward parts [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Proverbs 20:27]
- The Spirit teaches us all things and guides us into truth [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — John 14:26]

### E. The Spirit Helps in Prayer
- The Spirit helps our weaknesses and makes intercession for us [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 8:26]
- The Spirit works through believers to accomplish God's purposes [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 Corinthians 12:11]

### F. The Spirit Regenerates
- The Spirit gives new birth [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — John 3:5-6]
- The Spirit recreates the human spirit in the new birth

### G. The Old Covenant vs New Covenant
- Under the Old Covenant the Spirit came upon specific people for specific tasks; under the New Covenant He indwells every believer permanently [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 2 Corinthians 6:16]
- At Christ's death the veil was torn, and God moved out of the earthly holy of holies to dwell in His people

## 3. Common Misconceptions

### A. Evangelism Can Be Done in Human Strength
Many believers treat evangelism as a skill to be learned or a technique to be mastered. They rely on persuasive arguments, polished presentations, or personal charisma. But Scripture teaches that no one comes to Christ unless the Spirit draws them. Human effort alone produces human results at best — only the Spirit produces spiritual birth.

### B. The Holy Spirit Only Works Through Trained Ministers
The source material emphasizes that the Holy Spirit works through every believer, not just clergy or full-time ministers. The same Spirit who worked through apostles and prophets indwells every child of God. A newly saved believer has the same resident Spirit as a seasoned pastor.

### C. Conviction Is the Same as Condemnation
Some confuse the Spirit's convicting work with condemnation. Conviction is the Spirit's work to expose sin and lead to repentance. Condemnation is the enemy's work to crush and discourage. The Spirit's conviction always points toward the cross and the hope of forgiveness.

### D. The Spirit's Leading Is Only Through External Signs
Some expect the Spirit to lead only through dramatic signs, visions, or audible voices. While God can use these, the primary way the Spirit leads is through the human spirit — through inward witness, peace, and the Word of God.

### E. We Must Pray for the Spirit to Come Before He Works
A common error is believing we must pray for the Spirit to visit or "come down" in power. Jesus promised the Spirit would abide with us forever. He is already present in every believer. Our task is to acknowledge His presence and depend on Him, not to beg Him to arrive.

## 4. Practical Application Angles

- **Prayer Dependence:** Before every evangelistic conversation, pray that the Spirit would prepare hearts and guide your words
- **Spirit Sensitivity:** Practice being sensitive to the inward witness of the Spirit — that quiet prompting that tells you when to speak, when to listen, when to press in, and when to step back
- **Confidence in the Spirit's Work:** Trust that the Spirit is already at work in the person you are speaking to — your role is to cooperate, not manufacture results
- **Boldness Through the Spirit:** When fear rises before sharing the gospel, ask the Spirit for boldness and remember that the greater One lives in you
- **Praying in the Spirit:** Develop a habit of praying in the Spirit to build yourself up and receive guidance for evangelistic encounters

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Conviction | The Holy Spirit's work of convincing a person of their sin, their need for righteousness, and the reality of judgment |
| Indwelling | The permanent residence of the Holy Spirit in every believer, making our bodies His temple |
| Being Led by the Spirit | The process by which the Holy Spirit guides a believer through their human spirit — through inward witness, peace, and alignment with Scripture |
| Regeneration | The supernatural work of the Holy Spirit by which a spiritually dead person is made alive in Christ |
| Boldness | Supernatural courage given by the Spirit to speak the gospel without fear of man |
| Intercession | The Spirit's work of praying through and for believers, including praying in tongues as the Spirit gives utterance |
| Witness | A believer empowered by the Spirit to testify about Christ; both the act and the identity of the one who testifies |
| Worker Together | The partnership between the Holy Spirit and the believer in accomplishing God's purposes |
| Anointing | The enablement of the Holy Spirit on a believer for specific ministry tasks |
| Temple of God | The collective body of Christ and each individual believer as the dwelling place of the Holy Spirit |
