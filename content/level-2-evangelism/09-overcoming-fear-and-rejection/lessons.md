# Module 09 — Overcoming Fear & Rejection

---

## Lesson 1 — The Fear That Paralyzes

### HOOK
You know you should speak. You feel the moment is right. But something inside you tightens — your heart pounds, your mouth goes dry, and you stay silent. The moment passes, and you walk away feeling guilty, frustrated, and wondering why it is so hard to say something that matters so much.

### CORE TEACHING
The fear that keeps us from sharing our faith is one of the most universal experiences among believers. It does not matter how long you have been a Christian or how much you know about the Bible — the fear of man can hit anyone at any time. Even the apostle Paul admitted he came to the Corinthians "in weakness, in fear, and in much trembling." If the greatest evangelist in church history felt fear, then fear itself is not the problem. The problem is what we do with it. The fear of man is a snare, as Proverbs says. It traps us in the prison of other people's opinions. We imagine what they will think of us. We picture them laughing, arguing, or walking away. We rehearse every possible negative outcome, and by the time we are done imagining, we have talked ourselves out of speaking at all. But here is what we often miss: the fear we feel is not from God. Second Timothy tells us plainly that God has not given us a spirit of fear. That fear comes from our flesh, from the enemy, and from a mistaken belief that our comfort and reputation are more important than someone's eternal soul. Think of it like standing at the edge of a swimming pool on a cold day. The water looks freezing. Your whole body recoils at the thought of jumping in. But once you jump, you realize the water was not as bad as you imagined, and after a few seconds, you are glad you did it. Evangelism feels the same way. The anticipation is almost always worse than the reality. Most people respond far better than we expect. And even when they do not, we survive, and we learn, and we grow stronger. The first step to overcoming fear is to admit it is there and to stop waiting for it to go away. It may never fully disappear. But it can stop controlling you.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Proverbs 29:25 — "The fear of man brings a snare, but whoever trusts in the Lord shall be safe." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- 2 Timothy 1:7 — "For God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power and of love and of a sound mind." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- 1 Corinthians 2:3-4 — "I was with you in weakness, in fear, and in much trembling. And my speech and my preaching were not with persuasive words of human wisdom, but in demonstration of the Spirit and of power." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, identify one person you have been avoiding talking to about Jesus. Write their name down. Then pray specifically for boldness regarding that person every day this week.

### SUMMARY
- Fear in evangelism is normal and universal — even Paul experienced it.
- The fear of man is a snare, but God has not given us a spirit of fear.
- The anticipation of sharing your faith is almost always worse than the reality.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
Now that we have identified the problem of fear, we need to discover the one fear that actually sets us free — the fear of God.

## Lesson 2 — The Fear of God That Sets Us Free

### HOOK
Imagine two guards standing outside a door. One is a security guard at a shopping mall — you barely notice him. The other is a special forces soldier with a weapon aimed at you. Which one do you pay attention to? Which one changes how you act? The fear that matters most is the one that carries the most weight.

### CORE TEACHING
There is only one fear that can cure every other fear, and that is the fear of God. When Jesus taught His disciples about fear, He said something startling: "Do not fear those who kill the body but cannot kill the soul. But rather fear Him who is able to destroy both soul and body in hell." Jesus was not saying we should live in terror of God. He was saying that compared to God, every other threat is tiny. A person can mock you, reject you, insult you, or even harm your body — but they cannot touch your soul. Only God can. When you truly understand who God is — His holiness, His power, His authority as the Judge of all the earth — the fear of what people think shrinks to its proper size. It is like standing in front of a massive mountain and then looking at a small pebble at your feet. The pebble is real, but compared to the mountain, it is nothing. The fear of God does not make you cowardly toward people. It does the opposite. It makes you bold, because you know that the one whose opinion ultimately matters has already approved you in Christ. You are not living for the applause of the crowd. You are living for the approval of the King. This is why the early apostles could stand before the Sanhedrin — the most powerful religious council in Israel — and say, "We ought to obey God rather than men." They feared God more than they feared the highest human authorities. The fear of God is not a burden that weighs you down. It is the key that unlocks every other chain of fear. When you fear God rightly, you are freed from the slavery of human opinion. You can speak the truth in love because you care more about what God thinks than what anyone else thinks. And that freedom is the foundation of all boldness in evangelism.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Luke 12:4-5 — "And I say to you, My friends, do not be afraid of those who kill the body, and after that have no more that they can do. But I will show you whom you should fear: Fear Him who, after He has killed, has power to cast into hell; yes, I say to you, fear Him!" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Acts 5:29 — "But Peter and the other apostles answered and said: 'We ought to obey God rather than men.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Proverbs 14:26 — "In the fear of the Lord there is strong confidence, and His children will have a place of refuge." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, take five minutes each morning to remind yourself of one attribute of God — His holiness, His power, His sovereignty, or His role as Judge. Before you face any person, silently repeat: "I fear God more than I fear people."

### SUMMARY
- The fear of God is the only fear that can cure every other fear.
- Compared to God, every human threat is insignificant.
- Fearing God rightly frees you from the slavery of human opinion and gives you boldness.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
But knowing the fear of God is only half the answer — the other half is learning how love drives out fear entirely.

## Lesson 3 — Love: The Antidote to Fear

### HOOK
If someone told you a child was drowning in an icy pond, would you hesitate to jump in because the water was cold? Of course not. The love for that child would overpower every instinct of self-preservation. Love does something to fear: it silences it.

### CORE TEACHING
There is a principle in Scripture that is so powerful it can transform your entire experience of evangelism. First John 4:18 says, "Perfect love casts out fear." This is not a suggestion. It is a spiritual law. When love increases, fear decreases. They cannot coexist in the same space for long. Most of our fear in evangelism is actually self-focused. We are afraid of how we will look, how we will feel, whether we will be embarrassed, whether we will be rejected. It is all about us. But when genuine love for the other person fills our heart, the focus shifts from ourselves to them. Suddenly, it is not about whether we are comfortable. It is about whether they hear the truth that could save their soul. Imagine standing in a burning building. Someone standing next to you is about to die. Would you worry about whether your voice sounds nervous? Would you rehearse the perfect words? No. You would grab them and pull them toward the exit because the urgency of love overrides every hesitation. The problem is not that we have too much fear. The problem is that we have too little love. When you truly see people as Jesus sees them — as sheep without a shepherd, as souls facing eternity without hope, as people for whom Christ died — something changes in your heart. You stop seeing them as threats to your comfort and start seeing them as people in desperate need. One practical way to grow in this love is to pray for the lost by name. When you pray for someone regularly, God gives you His heart for them. You begin to care about them the way He does. And when love for them grows, fear of them shrinks. The waters of evangelism may be cold, but love for the drowning child makes you jump in without thinking twice. That is the power of perfect love driving out fear.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- 1 John 4:18 — "There is no fear in love; but perfect love casts out fear, because fear involves torment. But he who fears has not been made perfect in love." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Matthew 9:36 — "But when He saw the multitudes, He was moved with compassion for them, because they were weary and scattered, like sheep having no shepherd." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Romans 5:5 — "Now hope does not disappoint, because the love of God has been poured out in our hearts by the Holy Spirit who was given to us." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, pick one unsaved person you know and pray for them by name every day. Each time you pray, ask God to give you His love for that person. At the end of the week, look for an opportunity to speak with them.

### SUMMARY
- Fear in evangelism is rooted in self-focus; love shifts the focus to the other person.
- Perfect love casts out fear — they cannot coexist.
- Praying for the lost gives you God's heart for them and weakens your fear of them.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
But love alone is not enough — we also need power from above to speak with boldness, which is exactly what the Holy Spirit provides.

## Lesson 4 — Boldness Through the Holy Spirit

### HOOK
Think about Peter for a moment. A few weeks before Pentecost, he was so afraid of a teenage servant girl that he denied even knowing Jesus. A few weeks later, he stood before thousands of people and declared the gospel with a courage that shook Jerusalem. What changed? The answer is not that Peter tried harder. The answer is that the Holy Spirit came.

### CORE TEACHING
The book of Acts is filled with ordinary people doing extraordinary things, and the secret is always the same: the Holy Spirit. Before His ascension, Jesus told His disciples to wait in Jerusalem until they received power from on high. He did not tell them to study harder, prepare better arguments, or overcome their fears through sheer willpower. He told them to wait for the Spirit. When the Spirit came at Pentecost, everything changed. Peter, who had crumbled under pressure, stood up and preached a sermon that led to three thousand conversions. The same Peter who could not face a servant girl now faced the entire Sanhedrin and said, "We must obey God rather than men." This is not because Peter became naturally courageous. It is because the Holy Spirit filled him with supernatural boldness. This same Spirit lives in every believer today. If you have trusted in Christ, the Holy Spirit dwells in you. You do not need to pray for Him to come; you need to pray for Him to be unleashed in your life. The early church understood this. When they faced threats in Acts 4, they did not pray for safety. They prayed, "Lord, grant to Your servants that with all boldness they may speak Your word." And the place was shaken, and they were all filled with the Holy Spirit, and they spoke the word with boldness. The pattern is clear: boldness comes through the Spirit, and the Spirit responds to prayer. If you lack boldness, you do not need a personality transplant. You need to get on your knees. Ask the Holy Spirit to fill you, to give you words to speak, and to override your natural timidity with His supernatural power. The same Spirit who emboldened Peter is available to you right now. You do not have to do this in your own strength.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Acts 1:8 — "But you shall receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you; and you shall be witnesses to Me in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Acts 4:29-31 — "Now, Lord, look on their threats, and grant to Your servants that with all boldness they may speak Your word... And when they had prayed, the place where they were assembled together was shaken; and they were all filled with the Holy Spirit, and they spoke the word of God with boldness." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Ephesians 6:18-19 — "praying always with all prayer and supplication in the Spirit... and for me, that utterance may be given to me, that I may open my mouth boldly to make known the mystery of the gospel." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, set aside ten minutes each day to pray specifically for the Holy Spirit to fill you with boldness. Use the prayer from Acts 4:29 as your model: "Lord, give me boldness to speak Your word."

### SUMMARY
- Boldness in evangelism comes from the Holy Spirit, not from human effort.
- The early church prayed for boldness, and God answered by filling them with the Spirit.
- The same Spirit who emboldened Peter lives in every believer and responds to prayer.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
Even with the Spirit's power, we will face rejection — but how we handle it can actually strengthen our witness rather than discourage us.

## Lesson 5 — Learning from Rejection

### HOOK
A farmer scatters seed across a field. Some of it falls on the path and is eaten by birds. Some falls on rocky ground and withers. Some falls among thorns and is choked. But some falls on good soil and produces a harvest. The farmer does not stop sowing just because some seed is wasted. He keeps scattering.

### CORE TEACHING
Rejection is an unavoidable part of evangelism. Jesus Himself was rejected — by His own hometown, by the religious leaders, and ultimately by the crowds who cried for His crucifixion. If the perfect Son of God was rejected, we should expect nothing less. The problem is not that we face rejection; it is that we take it personally and let it discourage us from continuing. There is a vital truth that frees us from the sting of rejection: when people reject the gospel, they are not rejecting you. Jesus told His disciples, "He who hears you hears Me, he who rejects you rejects Me, and he who rejects Me rejects Him who sent Me." The rejection goes past you and lands on Christ. You are simply the messenger. If a delivery person hands you a letter and you throw it away, you are not rejecting the delivery person — you are rejecting the one who sent the letter. This distinction is crucial because it takes the weight off your shoulders. You are not responsible for how people respond. You are only responsible for faithfully delivering the message. Some people will reject it. That is between them and God. Your job is to sow the seed, not to guarantee the harvest. Another helpful perspective is to remember that rejection is not necessarily failure. The apostle Paul planted, Apollos watered, but God gave the increase. A person who rejects the gospel today may remember your words months or years later when the Holy Spirit opens their heart. Many people who eventually came to Christ can point back to someone who shared with them long before they were ready to respond. Do not measure success by immediate response. Measure it by faithfulness. Keep sowing. Keep praying. Keep loving. The harvest belongs to God.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Luke 10:16 — "He who hears you hears Me, he who rejects you rejects Me, and he who rejects Me rejects Him who sent Me." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- 1 Corinthians 3:6-7 — "I planted, Apollos watered, but God gave the increase. So then neither he who plants is anything, nor he who waters, but God who gives the increase." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Isaiah 55:11 — "So shall My word be that goes forth from My mouth; it shall not return to Me void, but it shall accomplish what I please, and it shall prosper in the thing for which I sent it." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, prepare a one-sentence response for when someone rejects the gospel, such as: "Thank you for being honest with me. I respect that. If you ever want to talk about it, I am here." Practice saying it until it feels natural.

### SUMMARY
- Rejection of the gospel is not rejection of you personally — it goes to Christ.
- You are responsible for faithfulness, not for results.
- Seed planted today may produce a harvest long after the conversation ends.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
Knowing all this is helpful, but the real question is where to start — and the answer may be much simpler than you think.

## Lesson 6 — Starting Where You Are: Faithful in Small Things

### HOOK
A man once carried a small booklet in his pocket for three months, praying for the courage to give it to his neighbor. Eighteen months passed before he finally knocked on the door. But when he did, a conversation began that might never have happened if he had waited until he felt ready.

### CORE TEACHING
One of the biggest mistakes believers make is thinking they need to be fully trained and completely fearless before they can share their faith. That is like saying you will not learn to swim until you are already an expert swimmer. It does not work that way. Boldness grows through action, not before it. The most effective way to overcome fear is to start small and build momentum. You do not have to preach on a street corner. You do not have to lead someone to Christ in your first conversation. You just need to take one small step. Maybe that step is giving a gospel tract to a cashier. Maybe it is asking a coworker, "Do you ever think about what happens after we die?" Maybe it is inviting a neighbor to church. The size of the step does not matter. What matters is that you take it. When you step out in obedience, two things happen. First, you discover that the reality is almost never as bad as your imagination predicted. The worst-case scenario that played out in your head did not happen. Second, God honors your obedience and gives you more courage for the next step. Think of it like building a fire. You do not start with a large log. You start with small twigs and kindling. Once those catch fire, you add slightly larger pieces, and gradually the fire grows strong enough to burn the largest logs. Your boldness works the same way. Start with small, low-pressure conversations. Let those small successes build your confidence. Then take slightly bigger steps. Over time, what once terrified you becomes almost natural. Even the most experienced evangelists still feel fear. But they have learned that obedience precedes courage. You do not wait until you are brave to act. You act, and the bravery follows.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Zechariah 4:10 — "For who has despised the day of small things?" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Matthew 25:21 — "His lord said to him, 'Well done, good and faithful servant; you were faithful over a few things, I will make you ruler over many things. Enter into the joy of your lord.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Luke 16:10 — "He who is faithful in what is least is faithful also in much; and he who is unjust in what is least is unjust also in much." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, set a small, measurable goal: have one spiritual conversation with someone. It does not need to be the full gospel — just one question about faith, eternity, or church. Write down when and how it went afterward.

### SUMMARY
- Boldness grows through action, not waiting until you feel ready.
- Start with small steps — a question, a tract, an invitation.
- Each step of obedience builds courage for the next step.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
There is one final truth that overcomes every fear and makes every risk worth taking — the infinite value of knowing Christ.

## Lesson 7 — He Is Worth It: How the Value of Christ Overcomes Every Fear

### HOOK
A man standing at the edge of a cliff is terrified of the drop. But if someone offers him a fortune greater than he could ever imagine just for stepping forward, suddenly the fear does not disappear, but it is outweighed. The value of what is offered changes everything.

### CORE TEACHING
At the end of the day, overcoming fear in evangelism comes down to one question: how much is Jesus worth to you? When you truly believe that Jesus is the greatest treasure in the universe — that knowing Him is better than anything the world can offer, that He is the pearl of great price for which it is worth selling everything — then the things you fear begin to look small by comparison. What is the worst that can happen if you share your faith? Someone might laugh at you. They might think you are strange. They might reject you. But compare that to the glory of seeing someone come to know Christ. Compare that to hearing Jesus say, "Well done, good and faithful servant." Compare that to the joy of spending eternity with someone who is in heaven because you were willing to speak. The apostle Paul understood this better than anyone. He suffered more for the gospel than most of us can imagine — beaten, stoned, shipwrecked, imprisoned, and eventually executed. Yet he wrote, "For I consider that the sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory which shall be revealed in us." He also said, "For to me, to live is Christ, and to die is gain." When Christ is that valuable, fear loses its power. You can face rejection, embarrassment, and even persecution because you know that what you have in Christ is infinitely greater than anything you could lose. Take time to write down why Jesus matters to you. Write down what He has saved you from. Write down what He has given you. Write down the hope you have because of His death and resurrection. When you remind yourself regularly of the infinite value of knowing Christ, your fears will shrink. They may not disappear entirely, but they will be outweighed. And when the worth of Christ overshadows every fear, you will speak — not because you are brave, but because He is worth it.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Philippians 3:7-8 — "But what things were gain to me, these I have counted loss for Christ. Yet indeed I also count all things loss for the excellence of the knowledge of Christ Jesus my Lord, for whom I have suffered the loss of all things, and count them as rubbish, that I may gain Christ." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Romans 8:18 — "For I consider that the sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory which shall be revealed in us." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Matthew 13:44 — "Again, the kingdom of heaven is like treasure hidden in a field, which a man found and hid; and for joy over it he goes and sells all that he has and buys that field." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write down a list titled "Why Jesus Is Worth More Than My Fear." List at least five specific things Christ has done for you. Keep this list in your pocket or phone and read it before any opportunity to share your faith.

### SUMMARY
- The ultimate answer to fear is the infinite value of knowing Christ.
- When Jesus is your greatest treasure, every other loss becomes small.
- Take time to remind yourself regularly of what Christ has done for you and why He is worth every risk.
