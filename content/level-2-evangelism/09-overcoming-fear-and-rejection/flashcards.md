# Flashcards — Overcoming Fear & Rejection

## Q&A Pairs

**1. Q: What is the fear of man, and why is it a problem in evangelism?**
A: The fear of man is excessive concern for what others think of us. It is a snare (Proverbs 29:25) that keeps believers silent because they value human approval more than obedience to God.

**2. Q: How does the fear of God cure the fear of man?**
A: When you fear God rightly — recognizing His holiness, power, and authority as Judge — every human threat becomes small by comparison. Fearing God more than people frees you to speak boldly.

**3. Q: What does "perfect love casts out fear" mean practically?**
A: When genuine love for the lost fills your heart, your focus shifts from self (how you look, how you feel) to them (their eternal need). Love for the drowning child makes you jump into the cold water without hesitation.

**4. Q: Does the Holy Spirit give boldness for evangelism?**
A: Yes. Acts 1:8 promises power when the Holy Spirit comes. The early church prayed for boldness in Acts 4, and they were filled with the Spirit and spoke with boldness. The same Spirit lives in every believer.

**5. Q: How did Peter change from denying Christ to preaching boldly?**
A: Peter was filled with the Holy Spirit at Pentecost. The change was not natural courage but supernatural empowerment. The Spirit transformed a fearful fisherman into a fearless witness.

**6. Q: How should a believer handle rejection when sharing the gospel?**
A: Rejection is not personal. Jesus said, "He who rejects you rejects Me" (Luke 10:16). You are the messenger, not the message. Be faithful to sow; the harvest belongs to God.

**7. Q: What is the best way to start overcoming the fear of evangelism?**
A: Start small. Take one low-pressure step — a question, a tract, an invitation. Each act of obedience builds courage. Boldness grows through action, not waiting until you feel ready.

**8. Q: Does God expect you to have all the answers before you share your faith?**
A: No. You only need to know the gospel and your own testimony. The Holy Spirit gives wisdom in the moment. Paul said he came "in weakness, in fear, and in much trembling" but relied on the Spirit's power.

**9. Q: What role does prayer play in overcoming fear?**
A: Prayer is essential. When you cannot talk to people about God, talk to God about people. Pray for open doors (Colossians 4:3), for boldness (Acts 4:29), and for love for the lost.

**10. Q: Why is Christ worth the risk of sharing your faith?**
A: Christ is the pearl of great price. Knowing Him is infinitely more valuable than anything you could lose — reputation, comfort, or approval. Paul counted everything as loss for the excellence of knowing Christ.
