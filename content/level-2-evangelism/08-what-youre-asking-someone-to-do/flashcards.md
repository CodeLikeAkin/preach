# Flashcards — What You're Asking Someone to Do

## Q&A Pairs

**1. Q: What are you actually asking someone to do when you share the gospel?**
A: You are asking them to repent of their sins, trust in Jesus Christ alone for salvation, and surrender everything to His lordship. The call is not an addition to life but a complete reorientation around Christ.

**2. Q: What is repentance?**
A: Repentance is a change of mind about God, sin, and self that results in a changed direction of life. It is turning from sin and turning to God. It is not merely feeling sorry for sin but fundamentally changing what you trust and love.

**3. Q: What is the difference between mere belief and saving faith?**
A: Mere belief means agreeing that certain facts about Jesus are true. Saving faith includes knowledge, assent, and personal trust — actually relying on Christ alone for salvation the way you rely on a parachute when you jump out of a plane.

**4. Q: Did Jesus tell people to count the cost before following Him?**
A: Yes. In Luke 14:25-33, Jesus gave two parables — a man building a tower and a king going to war — to illustrate that people should consider carefully what following Him will cost before they commit.

**5. Q: What does surrender mean in the Christian life?**
A: Surrender means giving God the right to control every part of your life — your time, body, money, relationships, and future. It is presenting yourself to God as a living sacrifice.

**6. Q: What is the motivation for surrender?**
A: The motivation is the mercies of God (Romans 12:1). Because Christ gave everything for us — dying for us while we were still His enemies — the only reasonable response is to give everything to Him.

**7. Q: What did Jesus mean by saying you must "hate" your family to follow Him?**
A: This is a Semitic idiom meaning to love less by comparison. Jesus must be first. Your love for Him must be so great that your love for even your closest family looks like hatred in comparison.

**8. Q: What is the paradox of dying to self?**
A: Jesus said that whoever tries to save their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life for His sake will find it. Clinging to your life shrinks it; releasing it to Christ multiplies it.

**9. Q: Why is following Jesus worth the cost?**
A: Jesus is the pearl of great price. He is worth infinitely more than anything you could give up. The sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory that will be revealed in us.

**10. Q: Does surrender mean you lose your freedom?**
A: No. Surrender to Christ is the path to true freedom. Living for yourself leads to slavery to sin, fear, and death. Living surrendered to Christ is the only life that is truly free.
