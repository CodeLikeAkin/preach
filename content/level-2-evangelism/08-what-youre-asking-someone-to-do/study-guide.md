# Study Guide — What You're Asking Someone to Do

## 1. Core Definition

When you share the gospel, you are not simply asking someone to pray a prayer, agree to a set of facts, or add Jesus to their existing life. You are asking them to repent of their sins, place their full trust in Jesus Christ alone for salvation, and surrender everything they are and have to His lordship. The call to follow Christ is a call to everything — there is no area of life that remains untouched. This is why Jesus Himself told people to count the cost before following Him. The gospel is a free gift, but it demands everything you have. The offer is grace; the terms are total surrender.

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

- **God is both just and the justifier of the sinner who trusts in Christ.** The cross is the only place where God's holiness and His mercy meet. He does not simply overlook sin; He punished it in His Son so that He could remain just while justifying the ungodly (Romans 3:26). This is the foundation of everything you are asking someone to do — they must understand that their sin demands judgment, and Christ took that judgment in their place. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Repentance is the non-negotiable turning from sin to God.** Jesus began His ministry preaching, "Repent, and believe in the gospel" (Mark 1:15). Repentance is not merely feeling sorry for sin; it is a fundamental change of mind about God, sin, and self that results in a changed life. Without repentance, there is no salvation (Acts 3:19). [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Saving faith is personal trust in Christ alone, not intellectual agreement.** Even the demons believe the facts about God, but they are not saved (James 2:19). Saving faith includes knowledge of the gospel, assent that it is true, and personal reliance on Christ's finished work as the sole basis of salvation. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Jesus requires first place over every human relationship and over life itself.** In Luke 14:26, Jesus said that anyone who comes to Him must "hate" father, mother, wife, children, brothers, sisters, and even their own life in comparison to their love for Him. This is a Semitic idiom meaning to love less — but the point is unmistakable: Christ must be first. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Counting the cost is required before following Jesus.** In Luke 14:28-33, Jesus gave two illustrations — a man building a tower and a king going to war — to show that people should consider carefully what it means to follow Him. He concluded, "Whoever of you does not forsake all that he has cannot be My disciple." The call is not to a comfortable addition to life but to total reorientation. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **The mercies of God are the motivation for total surrender.** Paul's appeal in Romans 12:1 is based on "the mercies of God" — not fear, not duty, not pressure. Because of what God has done in Christ, the only reasonable response is to present your whole self as a living sacrifice. Surrender is not a burden; it is a joyful response to grace. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Dying to self is the path to true life.** Jesus said, "Whoever desires to save his life will lose it, but whoever loses his life for My sake will save it" (Luke 9:24). The paradox of the Christian life is that real life is found not in self-preservation but in self-surrender. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

## 3. Common Misconceptions

- **"The gospel is just an addition to your life."** Many people think they can add Jesus to their existing priorities — career, family, comfort, ambitions — and keep everything running as before. But the gospel does not add to your life; it reorders everything around Christ. He is not a supplement; He is the center.

- **"Counting the cost is optional."** Some treat the cost of discipleship as a topic for mature believers, not something that should be mentioned to someone considering conversion. But Jesus Himself raised the cost upfront. To omit it is to present a gospel that Jesus never preached.

- **"Surrender means you lose your freedom."** The world tells us that giving up control is weakness. In reality, surrender to Christ is the path to true freedom. A life lived for yourself is a life of slavery to sin, fear, and death. A life surrendered to Christ is the only life that is truly free.

- **"Repentance is just turning from bad habits."** Repentance is far deeper than behavior modification. It is a complete change of mind about who God is, who you are, and what you live for. A person can stop drinking and still be lost; repentance changes the heart, not just the habits.

- **"Faith is just believing the right facts."** Intellectual belief is necessary but not sufficient. Saving faith is trust — the kind of trust that makes a person rest their entire eternal future on Christ alone, not on their own goodness, religion, or decisions.

## 4. Practical Application Angles

- **Be honest about the cost when you share the gospel.** Do not hide what following Jesus demands. If you omit the cost to get a quick decision, you may be building a foundation that will collapse under pressure. Be upfront that Jesus calls people to surrender everything.

- **Learn to explain repentance clearly.** Many people have never heard a clear definition. Practice explaining: "Repentance means you agree with God about your sin, you turn from it, and you turn to Christ. It is not just feeling bad — it is changing direction."

- **Distinguish between saving faith and mere belief.** When someone says they "believe" in Jesus, ask gentle questions to determine whether they are trusting in facts or trusting in a Person. Saving faith always results in a changed life.

- **Prepare a simple explanation of what surrender means.** Surrender is not about doing more for God; it is about giving God the right to do whatever He wants with your life. It is putting yourself at His disposal.

- **Connect the cost to the cross.** The reason surrender makes sense is because of what Christ has already done. He gave everything for you; giving everything to Him is the only reasonable response.

- **Help new believers see that the cost is outweighed by the value of Christ.** Jesus is the pearl of great price. When someone truly sees His worth, the cost does not feel like a sacrifice — it feels like a privilege.

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

- **Repentance (metanoia):** A change of mind and heart about God, sin, and self that results in a changed direction of life. It is turning from sin and turning to God. It is not a one-time event but an ongoing posture of the believer.

- **Faith (pistis):** Personal trust in and reliance upon Jesus Christ alone for salvation. It includes intellectual agreement with the truth of the gospel (notitia), personal acceptance of that truth (assensus), and wholehearted trust in Christ (fiducia).

- **Surrender:** The act of yielding one's entire life — including plans, possessions, relationships, and ambitions — to the lordship of Jesus Christ. It is not passive resignation but active submission.

- **Counting the cost:** The deliberate consideration Jesus requires before someone decides to follow Him. Based on Luke 14:25-33, it means understanding what discipleship will demand before making a commitment.

- **The call to everything:** The biblical reality that following Christ is not an add-on to life but a complete reorientation of life around Him. No area remains untouched.

- **Cheap grace:** Grace that is proclaimed without requiring repentance, faith, or surrender. It offers forgiveness without demanding transformation. This is not the grace of the New Testament.

- **Lord:** The title that signifies Jesus' authority over every aspect of a believer's life. To call Jesus "Lord" is to submit to His commands, not merely to acknowledge His existence.

- **Living sacrifice:** Paul's description in Romans 12:1 of the believer's response to God's mercy — offering one's entire self (body, mind, will) as an act of worship.
