# Module 08 — What You're Asking Someone to Do

---

## Lesson 1 — The Call Is Not an Addition; It Is a Complete Reorientation

### HOOK
Imagine asking someone to marry you but never mentioning that marriage means sharing everything — your home, your money, your future, your daily life. You just ask them to say "I do" without explaining what they are agreeing to. That would be dishonest. Yet this is exactly what many of us do when we share the gospel.

### CORE TEACHING
When you invite someone to follow Christ, you are not offering them a helpful addition to their existing life. You are asking them to surrender everything they are and have to the lordship of Jesus. This is not because God wants to take good things away from us. It is because He knows that we were made for Him, and we will never be truly alive until He is at the center of everything. The problem is that every one of us, by nature, lives with ourselves at the center. We build our lives around our plans, our comfort, our relationships, our ambitions. Then we hear the gospel and think, "I can add Jesus to this and keep everything else running." But that is not how it works. Jesus does not accept a place in your life. He demands the place. Think of it like a home that has many rooms. You might be willing to give Jesus one room — maybe the Sunday room or the emergency room. But He will not move into a room. He will not even move into most of the rooms. He is the owner of the house, and He is asking for the keys. The moment a person truly understands this, everything changes. They realize that the gospel is not about getting fire insurance or improving their marriage. It is about a complete transfer of ownership. You are asking someone to say, "I am no longer my own. I belong to Jesus." This is why the early Christians did not call themselves followers of a philosophy. They called themselves slaves of Christ. That language feels extreme to us today, but it captures exactly what the gospel demands. Jesus did not die to make you better. He died to make you His.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Luke 9:23 — "Then He said to them all, 'If anyone desires to come after Me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross daily, and follow Me.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Romans 14:7-8 — "For none of us lives to himself, and no one dies to himself. For if we live, we live to the Lord; and if we die, we die to the Lord. Therefore, whether we live or die, we are the Lord's." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- 1 Corinthians 6:19-20 — "Or do you not know that your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit who is in you, whom you have from God, and you are not your own? For you were bought at a price; therefore glorify God in your body and in your spirit, which are God's." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write down three areas of your life that you have been keeping under your own control rather than surrendering to Christ. For each one, write a specific prayer giving God authority over that area.

### SUMMARY
- The gospel does not add Jesus to your existing life; it reorients everything around Him.
- Jesus demands not a room in your house but the keys to the whole house.
- To follow Christ is to say, "I am no longer my own — I belong to Him."

## Lesson 2 — Repentance: The Turn That Changes Everything

### HOOK
A man stood at a crossroads holding a map that showed he had been walking in the wrong direction for miles. He had two choices: keep going and get further from his destination, or turn around and walk the other way. There was no third option. He could not keep walking the same direction and still expect to reach the right place.

### CORE TEACHING
Repentance is the most misunderstood word in modern evangelism. Many people hear it and think of a miserable person trying to give up sins to earn God's favor. But that is not repentance; that is moralism, and it is a trap. True repentance is far better. The simplest definition is a change of mind that results in a changed direction. The word itself means to think differently after an event — to have your mind turned around. When the Holy Spirit opens a person's eyes to see God for who He really is, their mind changes about everything. They realize that God is holy and that they are not. They realize that sin is not just a mistake but a rebellion against their Creator. They realize that they have been living as though they were God, deciding what is right and wrong for themselves. And when they see this, the only reasonable response is to turn. This is not a work. A person does not repent to be saved; they repent because they have seen the truth and cannot unsee it. Imagine you thought you were heading toward a beautiful city, but someone shows you that you are actually heading toward a cliff. Your decision to stop walking toward the cliff is not a "good work" that earns you a reward. It is simply the rational response to seeing the truth. Repentance is like that. When you see sin for what it really is — an offense against a holy God that cost the life of His Son — you stop loving it. You turn from it. You do not clean up your life so God will accept you. You turn from your sin because you have seen something more beautiful than your sin: Jesus Christ. This is what you are asking someone to do. Not to clean themselves up enough to be worthy of God, but to stop running from God and turn toward Him. It is the most freeing decision a person will ever make.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Mark 1:15 — "and saying, 'The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand. Repent, and believe in the gospel.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Acts 3:19 — "Repent therefore and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out, so that times of refreshing may come from the presence of the Lord." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Acts 26:20 — "but declared first to those in Damascus and in Jerusalem, and throughout all the region of Judea, and then to the Gentiles, that they should repent, turn to God, and do works befitting repentance." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write a one-paragraph explanation of repentance that you could say to someone in under sixty seconds. Practice it aloud until you can say it without reading from a page.

### SUMMARY
- Repentance is a change of mind about God, sin, and self that results in a changed direction of life.
- It is not a work done to earn salvation but the natural response to seeing the truth.
- When a person truly sees Christ, they do not need to be forced to turn — they want to.

## Lesson 3 — Faith: Trust That Moves You

### HOOK
A man once stood at the edge of a frozen river. He had read the temperature reports and studied the ice thickness. He believed the ice could hold him. But when his foot hovered over the surface, he could not bring himself to step. His belief was real, but it was not enough to move his feet.

### CORE TEACHING
Saving faith is far more than believing that certain facts about Jesus are true. Even the demons believe that Jesus is the Son of God. They know the truth better than any human does. But their belief does not save them because they do not trust Him. They fear Him. Saving faith has three elements that must all be present. The first is knowledge. A person must know who Jesus is and what He did. They must understand that Christ died for their sins, was buried, and rose again on the third day. This is the content of faith. There is nothing to believe without it. The second is assent. A person must agree that these facts are true. They must be convinced that the gospel is not a myth or a metaphor but actual history with eternal consequences. Many people in churches around the world have this much faith. They believe the facts of the gospel the way they believe historical facts about an ancient battle. It is true, but it does not change how they live. The third element is where everything becomes real. It is trust. Trust means personally relying on Jesus Christ alone for your salvation. It is one thing to believe that a parachute can save you. It is an entirely different thing to put it on and jump out of the plane. That is saving faith. When you ask someone to believe in Jesus, you are asking them to stop trusting in everything else — their own goodness, their church attendance, their baptism, their good intentions — and rest entirely on what Christ has done. This is not blind faith. It is faith based on evidence: the historical resurrection, the fulfilled prophecies, the changed lives of millions of believers. But it is faith that goes beyond agreement to personal commitment. The person who has saving faith does not just say, "I believe Jesus died for sins." They say, "I believe Jesus died for my sins. I am trusting Him alone to save me."

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- James 2:19 — "You believe that there is one God. You do well. Even the demons believe — and tremble!" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Romans 10:9-10 — "that if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved. For with the heart one believes unto righteousness, and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- John 3:16 — "For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, identify one area where you have been trusting in your own effort rather than in Christ alone — whether in your work, your relationships, or your spiritual life. Write it down, then pray specifically confessing that trust and asking God to help you rest in Christ.

### SUMMARY
- Saving faith requires knowledge of the gospel, agreement that it is true, and personal trust in Christ alone.
- Intellectual belief without trust is no different from the faith of demons.
- Faith is not believing facts about Jesus; it is trusting Jesus with your whole life.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
Once a person understands repentance and faith, there is something else Jesus insisted on before they follow — counting the cost.

## Lesson 4 — Counting the Cost: Why Jesus Told People to Think Twice

### HOOK
A man once started building a house without calculating what it would cost. He laid the foundation, ran out of money, and ended up living in a half-finished shell. Everyone who passed by laughed at him. Jesus used this picture to warn people not to follow Him without understanding what they were agreeing to.

### CORE TEACHING
One of the most striking things about Jesus' ministry is how He treated crowds. When thousands of people followed Him, He did not make it easy for them to stay. He told them that following Him meant hating their families and carrying a cross. He told them to sit down first and count the cost. This is almost the opposite of how we often do evangelism. We want to remove every obstacle so people will say yes. Jesus seemed to put obstacles in the way. But He did this because He loved them. He knew that a person who starts the Christian life without understanding what it demands will likely fall away, and their fall will be worse than if they had never started. In Luke 14, Jesus gave two illustrations. The first is a man building a tower. Before he builds, he sits down and calculates whether he has enough to finish. If he starts and cannot finish, everyone mocks him. The second is a king going to war. He considers whether his ten thousand soldiers can defeat the twenty thousand coming against him. If not, he negotiates peace before the battle begins. Jesus' point is simple: do not start what you cannot finish. Following Christ will cost you. It may cost you relationships. It may cost you your reputation. It may cost you your comfort or your plans. It may cost you your life. In many parts of the world, it regularly does. Jesus wanted people to know this before they committed, not after. This does not mean a person must count the cost perfectly before they can be saved. Many believers came to Christ with almost no understanding and grew into costly discipleship over time. But the principle remains: we should be honest. When you share the gospel, you should not hide what following Jesus involves. You are not doing someone a favor by making the path sound easy. You are setting them up to stumble.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Luke 14:28-30 — "For which of you, intending to build a tower, does not sit down first and count the cost, whether he has enough to finish it — lest, after he has laid the foundation, and is not able to finish, all who see it begin to mock him, saying, 'This man began to build and was not able to finish.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Luke 14:31-33 — "Or what king, going to make war against another king, does not sit down first and consider whether he is able with ten thousand to meet him who comes against him with twenty thousand? Or else, while the other is still a great way off, he sends a delegation and asks conditions of peace. So likewise, whoever of you does not forsake all that he has cannot be My disciple." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Matthew 10:37-39 — "He who loves father or mother more than Me is not worthy of Me. And he who loves son or daughter more than Me is not worthy of Me. And he who does not take his cross and follow after Me is not worthy of Me. He who finds his life will lose it, and he who loses his life for My sake will find it." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write down three specific costs that someone in your life might face if they decided to follow Jesus with full commitment. Pray for them by name, asking God to give them the courage to count the cost and still choose Christ.

### SUMMARY
- Jesus insisted that people understand the cost of following Him before they commit.
- The two parables of the tower and the war show that Jesus values genuine, counted commitment over quick decisions.
- Being honest about the cost is more loving than making the path sound easy.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
Once the cost is counted, the next step is one word that changes everything — surrender.

## Lesson 5 — Surrender: Giving Up the Right to Be Your Own

### HOOK
A driver once insisted on keeping control of the wheel while someone else tried to steer from the passenger seat. The car swerved, the directions conflicted, and the journey was a mess. There is only one way to travel with a guide: you have to let them drive.

### CORE TEACHING
Surrender is the word that makes the modern world uncomfortable. Everything around us tells us to take control, to be our own authority, to live on our own terms. But the gospel calls us to do the opposite. It calls us to lay down our right to be our own master and submit to Jesus Christ as Lord. This is not a suggestion for advanced believers. It is the heart of what it means to become a Christian. The apostle Paul put it simply in Romans 12:1: "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice." Notice that word — bodies. Paul did not say present your hearts, your spirits, or your intentions. He said present your bodies. He meant your whole physical life: your time, your energy, your money, your relationships, your career, your sexuality, your future. Everything that your body touches in the course of a day is to be offered to God as an act of worship. This sounds like a heavy burden until you understand the motivation. Paul did not say, "Present your bodies because God will punish you if you do not." He said, "By the mercies of God." Look at what God has done. He created you. You rebelled against Him. You deserved His judgment. But instead of giving you what you deserve, He gave His Son. Christ died for you while you were still His enemy. He rose again. He intercedes for you at the right hand of God. You have been forgiven, adopted, and given eternal life. When you truly understand those mercies, surrender does not feel like a sacrifice. It feels like the only reasonable response. This is what you are asking someone to do. Not to try harder, not to obey better, but to look at the cross and say, "You gave everything for me. I give everything to You."

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Romans 12:1 — "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, which is your reasonable service." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Matthew 16:24-25 — "Then Jesus said to His disciples, 'If anyone desires to come after Me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross, and follow Me. For whoever desires to save his life will lose it, but whoever loses his life for My sake will find it.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Ephesians 5:2 — "And walk in love, as Christ also has loved us and given Himself for us, an offering and a sacrifice to God for a sweet-smelling aroma." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, choose one hour each day to intentionally pause and ask God, "What do You want me to do with this hour?" Write down each answer and follow through, treating your time as a resource God owns.

### SUMMARY
- Surrender means giving God the right to control every part of your life — your time, body, money, and future.
- The motivation for surrender is not fear but the mercies of God demonstrated at the cross.
- When you truly see what Christ has done for you, giving everything to Him feels like the only reasonable response.

### NEXT LESSON PREVIEW
But surrender is not a burden to bear — it is actually the path to the most joyful life possible, as we will see next.

## Lesson 6 — Dying to Self: The Paradox of Losing Your Life to Find It

### HOOK
A man once gripped a branch hanging over the edge of a cliff, afraid to let go. Below him was solid ground only a few feet down, but he was terrified of falling. He clung to the branch until his arms gave out, and when he finally let go, he landed safely. He spent hours in fear and pain only to discover that letting go was exactly what he needed.

### CORE TEACHING
There is a paradox at the heart of Christianity that the world cannot understand. Jesus said, "Whoever desires to save his life will lose it, but whoever loses his life for My sake will find it." This is not a poetic saying. It is a literal description of how the Christian life works. Every human being, by nature, is trying to save their life. We protect our comfort, guard our reputations, hold onto our possessions, and fight for our rights. We think that holding onto these things is the way to be happy. But Jesus says the opposite. The more you cling to your life, the more it slips through your fingers. The more you release it to Him, the more you actually live. Think of a seed. A grain of wheat looks like a small, hard, dead thing. But if you bury it in the ground and let it die, it produces a stalk with many more seeds. If you keep it in your hand, it remains one grain. If you let it go, it multiplies. Your life is the same way. When you try to hold onto your life — your plans, your dreams, your right to yourself — you end up with a small, shriveled existence. But when you release everything to Christ, He gives you back a life that is bigger, richer, and more meaningful than anything you could have built on your own. This is what you are asking someone to do. You are asking them to die. Not physically, necessarily, but in a way that is just as real. You are asking them to bury their old life — their old identity, their old ambitions, their old way of thinking — so that God can raise them to new life. It sounds terrifying until you understand what is on the other side. The people who have given up the most for Christ would tell you they have received far more than they lost. The joy of knowing Christ dwarfs every sacrifice.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Luke 9:23-24 — "Then He said to them all, 'If anyone desires to come after Me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross daily, and follow Me. For whoever desires to save his life will lose it, but whoever loses his life for My sake will save it.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- John 12:24-25 — "Most assuredly, I say to you, unless a grain of wheat falls into the ground and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it produces much grain. He who loves his life will lose it, and he who hates his life in this world will keep it for eternal life." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Galatians 2:20 — "I have been crucified with Christ; it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me; and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave Himself for me." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, identify one possession, relationship, or ambition you have been holding onto too tightly. Write it on a piece of paper, pray over it, and tear the paper as a physical act of releasing it to God.

### SUMMARY
- The paradox of Christianity is that losing your life for Christ is the only way to truly find it.
- Like a grain of wheat, your life produces much more when you release it than when you cling to it.
- The joy of knowing Christ far outweighs every sacrifice you could make for Him.

Now that we understand the cost, the surrender, and the joy, we will look at one final truth — why the whole call is worth everything you have.

## Lesson 7 — The Pearl of Great Price: Why He Is Worth Everything

### HOOK
A merchant spent his entire life searching for the finest pearls. One day he found one so perfect, so beautiful, that he sold everything he owned to buy it. People watching thought he was a fool for giving up so much. But he knew something they did not: the pearl was worth more than everything he gave up combined.

### CORE TEACHING
When you ask someone to follow Jesus, you are asking them to give up everything. But you are not asking them to give up everything for nothing. You are asking them to trade their small, broken, temporary life for a life that is infinite in value. Jesus told a parable about a man who found a treasure hidden in a field. In his joy, he sold everything he had to buy that field. He did not do it reluctantly. He did it with joy, because he knew the treasure was worth more than everything he owned. This is the heart of what you are asking someone to understand. The cost of following Jesus is real. It may cost them relationships, reputation, comfort, and even life itself. But the value of Jesus Christ is so great that the cost becomes insignificant by comparison. Think about it this way. Who is Jesus? He is the eternal Son of God who created the universe. He is the one who holds all things together by His power. He is the King of kings and the Lord of lords. And He loved you enough to leave heaven, become a man, and die a criminal's death so that you could be forgiven. He rose from the dead and defeated death forever. He offers you forgiveness, adoption into God's family, the indwelling Holy Spirit, a purpose that will never end, and eternal life in the presence of God. What you are asking someone to give up cannot compare with what you are asking them to gain. The person who sees Jesus for who He is does not count the cost as a burden. They count it as a privilege. They do not say, "Look at everything I lost for Jesus." They say, "Look at everything I gained in Jesus." This is what made the early Christians sing while they were being tortured. This is what makes believers in persecuted countries today refuse to deny Christ even when their lives are on the line. They have seen the pearl. They know it is worth everything.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Matthew 13:44-46 — "Again, the kingdom of heaven is like treasure hidden in a field, which a man found and hid; and for joy over it he goes and sells all that he has and buys that field. Again, the kingdom of heaven is like a merchant seeking beautiful pearls, who, when he had found one pearl of great price, went and sold all that he had and bought it." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Philippians 3:7-8 — "But what things were gain to me, these I have counted loss for Christ. Yet indeed I also count all things loss for the excellence of the knowledge of Christ Jesus my Lord, for whom I have suffered the loss of all things, and count them as rubbish, that I may gain Christ." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Romans 8:18 — "For I consider that the sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory which shall be revealed in us." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write down a list of ten specific things you have gained or experienced since coming to Christ — forgiveness, peace, purpose, hope, or anything else. Thank God for each one. This list will remind you that He is worth everything you have given up.

### SUMMARY
- Following Jesus costs everything, but He is worth infinitely more than anything you could give up.
- The parable of the pearl of great price shows that the kingdom is so valuable it is worth selling everything to possess it.
- Those who truly see Jesus do not count the cost as a loss — they count it as a privilege.
