# Flashcards: What Is the Gospel?

## Q&A Pairs

### Card 1
**Q: What is the gospel in one sentence?**
**A:** The gospel is the good news that God, through the person and work of Jesus Christ, has done everything necessary to save sinners.

---

### Card 2
**Q: Why must the gospel begin with God rather than with man?**
**A:** Because God is the central figure of the entire biblical narrative. His holiness, justice, and love form the foundation upon which everything else rests. A gospel that starts with man's felt needs rather than God's glory is a different gospel.

---

### Card 3
**Q: What does Scripture teach about the condition of humanity apart from grace?**
**A:** All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 3:23]. Humanity is spiritually dead in trespasses and sins [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Ephesians 2:1], by nature children of wrath [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Ephesians 2:3], with hearts that are deceitful and desperately wicked [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Jeremiah 17:9].

---

### Card 4
**Q: What is the purpose of the law in relation to the gospel?**
**A:** The law was never given as a means of salvation but to show us our sin, silence every excuse, and hold the whole world guilty before God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 3:19-20]. It serves as a tutor to lead us to Christ by revealing our inability to save ourselves [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Galatians 3:24].

---

### Card 5
**Q: What did Jesus Christ accomplish on the cross?**
**A:** Jesus Christ died as a substitutionary sacrifice for sinners. He bore the full weight of divine wrath in the place of His people, satisfying the justice of God. God made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us, so that we might become the righteousness of God in Him [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 2 Corinthians 5:21].

---

### Card 6
**Q: What is the significance of the resurrection?**
**A:** The resurrection is God's declaration that Christ's sacrifice was accepted, that sin has been fully atoned for, and that death has been defeated. Without the resurrection, there is no assurance that Christ's work was sufficient [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 Corinthians 15:17].

---

### Card 7
**Q: What is biblical repentance?**
**A:** Biblical repentance is a God-given turning from sin to God involving the mind (recognizing sin as rebellion), the emotions (godly sorrow over sin), and the will (deliberately turning away from sin). It is not merely feeling sorry but a change of direction, and it is both a command from God and a gift from God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Acts 17:30, Acts 11:18].

---

### Card 8
**Q: What is saving faith?**
**A:** Saving faith has three elements: knowledge (understanding the gospel content), assent (agreeing that it is true), and trust (personally resting upon Christ alone for salvation). It is not the strength of faith that saves but the object of faith. Faith is a gift of God, not a human work [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Ephesians 2:8-9].

---

### Card 9
**Q: How do repentance and faith relate to each other?**
**A:** Repentance and faith are two sides of the same response to the gospel. Repentance is turning from sin and self, and faith is turning to Christ. Both are gifts from God, both are commands from God, and both are necessary for salvation. One cannot exist without the other.

---

### Card 10
**Q: What evidence should be present in the life of someone who has been born again?**
**A:** The new birth produces observable evidence: a changed relationship to sin (no longer living in it as a settled pattern), a changed relationship to God (desiring Him as Father), a changed relationship to other believers (genuine love for the family of God), perseverance through trials, and a love for the truth of the gospel [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 John 3:9, 1 John 2:19, Romans 8:16].

---

## Memory Verse Drill

### Primary Verses (should be memorized word-perfect)

| Reference | Key Phrase | Theme |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Romans 6:23 | "the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord" | The gospel in miniature |
| 2 Corinthians 5:21 | "He made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us, that we might become the righteousness of God in Him" | Substitution |
| Ephesians 2:8-9 | "by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works" | Salvation by grace alone |
| Romans 10:9 | "if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved" | The response required |
| John 3:16 | "God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life" | God's love and the offer |

### Secondary Verses (familiarity target)

| Reference | Key Phrase | Theme |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Romans 3:23 | "all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God" | Universal sin |
| 1 Corinthians 15:3-4 | "Christ died for our sins... was buried... rose again the third day" | The gospel facts |
| Acts 17:30 | "God now commands all men everywhere to repent" | The command to repent |
| Isaiah 53:5-6 | "He was wounded for our transgressions... the LORD has laid on Him the iniquity of us all" | Penal substitution |
| John 3:3 | "unless one is born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God" | Necessity of regeneration |
| 1 Peter 1:18-19 | "redeemed... with the precious blood of Christ" | The price of redemption |

### Drill Instructions

1. **Week 1:** Memorize Romans 6:23 and 2 Corinthians 5:21. Say each verse aloud ten times per day.
2. **Week 2:** Review previous verses and add Ephesians 2:8-9. Recite all three in sequence daily.
3. **Week 3:** Review all previous and add Romans 10:9. Write each verse from memory once per day.
4. **Week 4:** Review all previous and add John 3:16. Recite all five primary verses from memory without looking.
5. **Ongoing:** Review all primary verses weekly. Add one secondary verse per week for ten weeks.
