# Study Guide: What Is the Gospel?

## 1. Core Definition

The gospel is the good news that God, through the person and work of Jesus Christ, has done everything necessary to save sinners. It begins not with man's need but with God's character — His holiness, justice, and love. It reveals that humanity, created in God's image, has rebelled against its Creator, fallen into total spiritual deadness, and stands under the just wrath of God. The law of God was given not as a means of salvation but to silence every mouth and hold the whole world guilty before Him [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 3:19]. In His great love, God the Son became flesh, lived a perfectly righteous life, and died on the cross as the substitutionary sacrifice for sin, bearing the full weight of divine wrath in the place of sinners. He was raised from the dead on the third day, victorious over sin and death, and now commands all people everywhere to repent and believe this good news. All who turn from their sins and trust in Christ alone are declared righteous by God, born again to a living hope, and granted eternal life — not by their works but entirely by grace through faith. This is the gospel, and it is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 1:16].

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

### A. The Character of God
- God is holy, separate from sin, and perfect in all His ways [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Isaiah 6:3]
- God is just — He must and will punish sin [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Nahum 1:3]
- God is love — He acts to save sinners consistent with His justice [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 John 4:8]
- God is the Creator and therefore has rightful authority over every human life [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Genesis 1:27]

### B. The Condition of Man
- All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 3:23]
- The wages of sin is death — physical, spiritual, and eternal [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 6:23]
- By nature, all are children of wrath [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Ephesians 2:3]
- No one seeks after God on their own [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 3:11]
- The heart is deceitful and desperately wicked [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Jeremiah 17:9]
- Man is spiritually dead and unable to respond to God apart from regeneration [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Ephesians 2:1]

### C. The Person and Work of Christ
- Jesus Christ is fully God and fully man [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Colossians 2:9]
- He lived a sinless life, fulfilling all righteousness [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 2 Corinthians 5:21]
- He died on the cross as a substitutionary sacrifice, bearing God's wrath for sinners [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Isaiah 53:5-6]
- He was buried and rose again on the third day [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 Corinthians 15:3-4]
- He ascended to the right hand of the Father and intercedes for His people [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 8:34]

### D. The Response Required
- Repentance: turning from sin and self-reliance [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Acts 17:30]
- Faith: trusting in Christ alone for salvation [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Acts 16:31]
- Both repentance and faith are gifts from God, not human achievements [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Ephesians 2:8-9]

### E. The Results of Salvation
- Justification: declared righteous before God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 5:1]
- Regeneration: born again to new life [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — John 3:3]
- Adoption: made children of God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Galatians 4:4-5]
- Assurance: the Holy Spirit bears witness with our spirit [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Romans 8:16]
- Perseverance: kept by the power of God [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — 1 Peter 1:5]

## 3. Common Misconceptions

### A. The Gospel Is Not Self-Help
Many treat the gospel as a set of principles for living a better life. But the gospel is news about what Christ has done, not advice about what we must do to improve ourselves. It addresses sin and judgment, not merely unhappiness or unfulfillment.

### B. The Gospel Is Not Moralism
The gospel is not "try harder" or "do better." Christianity is not a system of ethics where good people are rewarded and bad people are punished. It is the announcement that Christ lived and died for sinners who cannot save themselves.

### C. The Gospel Is Not the Same as Religion
The gospel is not church attendance, religious rituals, or moral behavior. These can be expressions of faith, but they are not the gospel itself. A person can be religious and still lost.

### D. The Gospel Is Not a Fire Escape
Some reduce the gospel to a way to avoid hell while continuing to live for self. But the gospel calls for a complete surrender to the lordship of Christ, not merely fire insurance.

### E. The Gospel Is Not Universalism
Not all will be saved. The gospel must be believed and responded to with repentance and faith. Those who reject Christ remain under condemnation [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — John 3:36].

### F. The Gospel Is Not About Human Effort
Some present salvation as God doing His part (sending Christ) and man doing his part (being good enough). But salvation is entirely of the Lord from beginning to end [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV — Jonah 2:9].

## 4. Practical Application Angles

- **Evangelism Training:** Help believers articulate the gospel in one minute, five minutes, and thirty minutes by focusing on God, man, Christ, and response.
- **Personal Assurance:** Use the elements of the gospel to help believers examine whether they have truly repented and believed, distinguishing between genuine conversion and mere religious activity.
- **Gospel Fluency:** Encourage daily rehearsing of the gospel to oneself so that it remains central, not just for initial conversion but for ongoing sanctification.
- **Correcting Reductionism:** Train believers to recognize and gently correct common reductions of the gospel (moralism, self-help, fire insurance) when they encounter them.
- **Worship Fuel:** Connect the doctrinal content of the gospel to doxology — the gospel is not merely to be understood but to be celebrated.

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Gospel | The good news that God saves sinners through the person and work of Jesus Christ |
| Sin | Any failure to conform to the moral law of God in act, attitude, or nature |
| Repentance | A genuine turning from sin to God, involving a change of mind, heart, and direction |
| Faith | Trust in Christ alone for salvation, resting on His finished work rather than personal merit |
| Justification | The act by which God declares a sinner righteous on the basis of Christ's righteousness credited through faith |
| Regeneration | The supernatural work of God by which He imparts new spiritual life to a dead soul |
| Substitution | Christ's taking the place of sinners, bearing the punishment they deserved |
| Atonement | The work of Christ in satisfying the justice of God through His sacrificial death |
| Propitiation | The turning away of God's wrath through the sacrifice of Christ |
| Sanctification | The ongoing work of God by which believers are conformed to the image of Christ |
| Grace | Unmerited favor from God toward sinners who deserve judgment |
| Wrath of God | The righteous judgment of God against sin, both now and in eternal punishment |
