# Study Guide — Praying for the Lost + Follow-Up & Discipleship

## 1. Core Definition

Praying for the lost and following up new believers are two inseparable halves of the same evangelistic commission. Intercession for the unsaved is the spiritual foundation upon which all evangelistic effort rests — without prayer, we work in human strength and produce human results at best. Follow-up and discipleship are the natural continuation of the evangelistic process, ensuring that those who respond to the gospel are established in their faith, connected to the local church, and guided toward maturity in Christ. This module teaches believers to pray with specificity and persistence for the lost, to walk alongside new converts in their first weeks and months of faith, to connect them to a healthy local church, and to understand that the goal of evangelism is not a decision but a disciple. Module 07 — Leading Someone to Christ covered the moment of conversion; this module equips believers for everything that happens before (prayer) and after (follow-up) that moment.

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

**Prayer is the foundation of all evangelism.** Without the Holy Spirit's work, which is released through prayer, no amount of human effort can produce genuine conversion. Scripture commands that supplications, prayers, intercessions, and giving of thanks be made for all men, including those in authority, because God desires all men to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth (1 Timothy 2:1-4) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. Prayer is not a prelude to evangelism; it is the primary work.

**Christ Himself is our intercessor, and we participate in His intercession.** Jesus lives forever to make intercession for His people (Hebrews 7:25) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. As members of His body, believers are invited to join in this intercessory work, not because God is reluctant but because prayer aligns us with God's will. Jesus prayed for those who would believe through the apostles' message (John 17:20-21) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV], and He taught His disciples to pray for laborers to be sent into the harvest (Luke 10:2) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

**Follow-up begins the moment someone responds to the gospel.** The apostles did not simply count decisions; they established new believers. In Acts 14:21-22 [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV], Paul and Barnabas returned to cities where people had been converted, strengthening the souls of the disciples and exhorting them to continue in the faith. A newborn believer needs spiritual milk — the pure Word — to grow (1 Peter 2:2) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. The first days and weeks after conversion are the most critical for establishing a new believer in their faith.

**The local church is God's plan for discipleship.** From the day of Pentecost onward, new believers were added to the church, devoted to the apostles' teaching, fellowship, breaking of bread, and prayers (Acts 2:42) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. Connecting a new believer to a healthy local church is not optional; it is essential for their growth, protection, and long-term fruitfulness. The body of Christ provides the teaching, community, accountability, and opportunities for service that a believer needs to mature.

**The goal of evangelism is not a decision but a disciple.** The Great Commission commands believers to make disciples, teaching them to observe all that Jesus commanded (Matthew 28:19-20) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. A decision without follow-up is like a birth without care — the infant will not survive. True evangelism includes the commitment to walk with new believers toward maturity in Christ.

## 3. Common Misconceptions

**"I can share the gospel without praying — the Word is powerful on its own."** While Scripture is powerful, Jesus Himself modeled prayer as essential to ministry. He prayed before choosing disciples, before His arrest, and taught His disciples to pray for the harvest. Prayer is the atmosphere in which the Word is most effective, and the Spirit moves in response to the prayers of God's people.

**"Follow-up is the pastor's job, not mine."** Scripturally, every believer is called to make disciples. The Great Commission was not given to pastors alone but to all who follow Christ. While the local church provides the context for growth, the person who led someone to Christ should take personal responsibility for that new believer's initial establishment in the faith.

**"If someone is truly saved, they will grow on their own without my help."** Salvation is the work of God, but growth happens in community. The New Testament is filled with examples of believers investing in other believers — Paul in Timothy, Barnabas in Mark, the elders in the new converts. God uses people to grow people. Leaving a new believer to grow alone is like leaving a newborn to feed itself.

**"Praying for the lost is enough; I do not need to speak to them."** Prayer and proclamation are partners, not alternatives. We pray because we cannot save anyone, and we speak because God uses our words to reach the lost. To pray without speaking is to halt at the starting line. To speak without praying is to run in our own strength. Both are necessary.

**"Baptism and church membership can wait until the new believer is ready."** The early church did not delay. On the day of Pentecost, those who received the word were baptized immediately (Acts 2:41) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. Baptism is the first step of obedience and public identification with Christ. It should not be delayed. Church membership and regular fellowship should begin as soon as possible.

## 4. Practical Application Angles

- Maintain a written prayer list of specific unsaved people and pray through it daily, asking God to open their hearts and provide opportunities to share the gospel
- Create a simple follow-up plan for new believers covering the first 30 days: meet weekly, read through a Gospel together, help them understand their assurance of salvation
- Prepare a "next steps" checklist for a new convert: read the Gospel of John, begin daily prayer, attend church, get baptized, meet other believers
- Introduce a new believer to at least three mature Christians in the local church within the first week of their conversion
- Learn how to use a simple discipleship resource (such as a Gospel booklet or a basic study guide) to walk a new believer through the foundations of the faith
- Practice praying aloud with a new believer in your first follow-up meeting, modeling how to talk to God naturally
- Commit to walking with one new believer for at least six months after their conversion, meeting regularly for prayer, study, and accountability

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

- **Intercession**: Praying on behalf of others, especially for their salvation; standing in the gap between the lost and a holy God
- **Follow-Up**: The intentional process of guiding a new believer through their first steps of faith after conversion, including prayer, Scripture reading, assurance of salvation, and connection to the church
- **Discipleship**: The ongoing process of helping a believer grow toward maturity in Christ through teaching, modeling, accountability, and community
- **Assurance of Salvation**: The confidence that a person is truly saved, based on the promises of Scripture rather than feelings, including the witness of the Holy Spirit (Romans 8:16) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- **Baptism**: The first act of obedience for a new believer, publicly identifying them with Christ's death, burial, and resurrection (Romans 6:3-4) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- **Local Church**: The community of believers in a specific location, governed by Scripture, where the Word is preached, the ordinances are observed, and believers are equipped for ministry
- **Spiritual Milk**: The foundational truths of the Christian faith that new believers need first, including the gospel, the authority of Scripture, prayer, and basic Christian living (1 Peter 2:2) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- **The Great Commission**: Christ's command to His followers to go into all the world and make disciples, baptizing them and teaching them to obey everything He commanded (Matthew 28:19-20) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- **Harvest**: A biblical metaphor for the souls of lost people who are ready to respond to the gospel; Jesus said the harvest is plentiful but the laborers are few (Luke 10:2) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
- **New Creature**: The biblical description of a person who has been born again — old things have passed away, and all things have become new (2 Corinthians 5:17) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]
