# Flashcards — Praying for the Lost + Follow-Up & Discipleship

## 10 Question & Answer Pairs

### Card 1
**Q: Why is prayer the foundation of all evangelism rather than just a preparation for it?**
**A:** Because no human argument can open a heart that God has not opened — the Lord opened Lydia's heart before Paul spoke. Prayer aligns us with God's will, keeps us dependent on the Holy Spirit, and is the means through which God releases His power in evangelism. Paul commanded that prayer be made "first of all" for all people (1 Timothy 2:1) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

### Card 2
**Q: What role does Jesus play in our prayers for the lost right now?**
**A:** Jesus "ever lives to make intercession" for us at the right hand of the Father (Hebrews 7:25) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. Our prayers are presented before God by Christ Himself, and we are invited to join in His ongoing intercessory work as members of His body.

### Card 3
**Q: What specific things should we ask for when praying for the lost by name?**
**A:** We should ask God to open their hearts (Acts 16:14) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV], open doors for the Word (Colossians 4:3) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV], give us boldness to speak clearly, and send the Holy Spirit to convict them of sin, righteousness, and judgment (John 16:8) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

### Card 4
**Q: Why is persistence in prayer for the lost important even when we do not see immediate results?**
**A:** Jesus taught the parable of the persistent widow to show that we should always pray and not lose heart (Luke 18:1) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. Some people may take years to come to Christ, but God is working in ways we cannot see. Persistence in prayer reflects faith that God is still moving.

### Card 5
**Q: What should you do immediately after someone responds to the gospel?**
**A:** Help them understand what just happened by reading Romans 10:9-10 [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV] again and assuring them of their salvation based on God's promise, not their feelings. Give them a simple first step: read a Gospel, begin daily prayer, and commit to meeting again soon.

### Card 6
**Q: What are the three most important things to cover in your first follow-up meeting with a new believer?**
**A:** Establish assurance of salvation using 1 John 5:11-13 [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV], teach them to pray by praying with them, and open the Bible with them starting with the Gospel of John.

### Card 7
**Q: Why is the first 30 days after conversion so critical for a new believer?**
**A:** Habits formed in the first month often persist for years. Neglect in this period can lead to doubt, vulnerability to false teaching, or even total apostasy. The apostles returned to cities where people had been converted to strengthen and encourage them (Acts 14:21-22) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV].

### Card 8
**Q: Why must a new believer be connected to a local church?**
**A:** The local church is God's design for growth and protection. New believers need the Word preached, fellowship with other believers, the ordinances (baptism and communion), accountability, and opportunities to serve. A believer disconnected from the church is like a coal removed from the fire — the flame will not survive.

### Card 9
**Q: What role does the person who led someone to Christ play in connecting them to the church?**
**A:** They should take personal responsibility to help the new believer find a healthy gospel-preaching church, take them along to their own church, introduce them to the pastor and other believers, and help them understand that church is a family they belong to, not a building they attend.

### Card 10
**Q: What is the ultimate goal of evangelism according to the Great Commission?**
**A:** The goal is not a decision but a disciple. Jesus commanded us to make disciples, baptizing them and teaching them to observe everything He commanded (Matthew 28:19-20) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]. A decision without follow-up is like a birth without care — the infant will not survive.
