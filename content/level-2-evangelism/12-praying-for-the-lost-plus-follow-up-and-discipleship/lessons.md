# Lessons — Praying for the Lost + Follow-Up & Discipleship

---

## Lesson 1 — The Foundation of All Evangelism Is Prayer

### HOOK

Imagine a farmer who carefully plants seeds in fertile soil but never waters them. Day after day he walks past the field, watching the ground grow hard and the seeds wither, wondering why nothing grows. That is exactly what we do when we share the gospel with someone but never pray for them. We plant the seed of the Word, but we withhold the water that the Holy Spirit uses to make it sprout.

### CORE TEACHING

Many believers treat prayer as a nice extra, something they do before they go out to evangelize — a kind of spiritual warm-up before the real work begins. But Scripture presents prayer as the primary work of evangelism, not a preparation for it. The apostle Paul wrote to Timothy with urgency: "Therefore I exhort first of all that supplications, prayers, intercessions, and giving of thanks be made for all men" (1 Timothy 2:1). Notice that word "first." Before the preaching, before the arguments, before the invitation — prayer comes first. Why? Because no human argument, no matter how clever, can open a heart that God has not opened. Lydia's heart was opened by the Lord before Paul ever spoke a word to her (Acts 16:14). That opening happens in response to prayer. Jesus Himself modeled this priority. Before He chose His twelve apostles, He spent the entire night in prayer (Luke 6:12). Before He went to the cross, He prayed in Gethsemane. On the cross, He prayed for His enemies. If the Son of God needed prayer to sustain His ministry, how much more do we? Prayer is not asking God to bless what we have already planned. Prayer is asking God to show us what He is already doing and then joining Him in it. When we pray for the lost, we are not trying to convince a reluctant God to save them. We are aligning ourselves with His will — because God desires all men to be saved (1 Timothy 2:4). Prayer positions us as partners in what God is already doing. It also keeps us dependent. The moment we stop praying is the moment we start relying on our own strength, and that strength will never produce spiritual fruit. The cross and resurrection are the content of the message we bring, but prayer is the atmosphere in which that message lands with power. A believer who prays for the lost will find their own heart changed first — they will grow in compassion, in boldness, and in desperation for souls to be saved. The evangelist who does not pray may still speak, but he speaks alone. The evangelist who prays speaks with heaven backing every word.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

1 Timothy 2:1-4 — Therefore I exhort first of all that supplications, prayers, intercessions, and giving of thanks be made for all men, for kings and all who are in authority, that we may lead a quiet and peaceable life in all godliness and reverence. For this is good and acceptable in the sight of God our Savior, who desires all men to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Luke 6:12 — Now it came to pass in those days that He went out to the mountain to pray, and continued all night in prayer to God (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Luke 10:2 — Then He said to them, "The harvest truly is great, but the laborers are few; therefore pray the Lord of the harvest to send out laborers into His harvest" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Acts 16:14 — Now a certain woman named Lydia heard us. She was a seller of purple from the city of Thyatira, who worshiped God. The Lord opened her heart to heed the things spoken by Paul (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, write down the names of five unsaved people you know. Place that list where you will see it every day, and commit to praying through each name by name for at least five minutes each morning. Ask God specifically to open their hearts and give you a natural opportunity to speak with them about Christ.

### SUMMARY

- Prayer is the foundation of evangelism, not a prelude to it — Scripture puts prayer first before proclamation.
- Jesus modeled a life of prayer before and during His ministry, showing that even He depended on the Father.
- Praying for the lost aligns us with God's will and keeps us dependent on the Holy Spirit rather than our own strength.

## Lesson 2 — Christ Our Intercessor: Praying in His Name

### HOOK

Imagine you owe a massive debt, far beyond anything you could ever repay. You go to the creditor to beg for mercy, but when you arrive, someone else is already there — someone who loves you, who has already paid the debt in full, and who is speaking to the creditor on your behalf. That is what prayer in the name of Jesus means.

### CORE TEACHING

One of the most comforting truths in Scripture is that we do not pray alone. Jesus Christ, the risen Son of God, is seated at the right hand of the Father, and His ongoing work is intercession. The writer of Hebrews declares that Jesus "ever lives to make intercession" for those who come to God through Him (Hebrews 7:25). This means that every prayer we offer for the lost is carried into the presence of God by Christ Himself. Our prayers are not weak, uncertain cries into the dark — they are presented before the Father by the One whose blood has already secured our access. This changes how we pray. When we understand that we are joining Christ in His intercessory work, our prayers become bolder and more confident. We are not trying to get God's attention; we have it through Christ. We are not trying to change God's mind; we are aligning with His heart. The apostle John wrote, "If we ask anything according to His will, He hears us" (1 John 5:14), and we know it is God's will that the lost be saved. So when we pray for an unsaved coworker or family member, we are praying in perfect alignment with God's revealed will. The night before His crucifixion, Jesus prayed not only for His disciples but for all who would believe through their message (John 17:20). He is still praying that prayer. Every time we intercede for someone who does not yet know Christ, we are joining our voice to His. This is not a small thing. Think of what it cost the Father to provide the way of salvation — the cross. The resurrection was the Father's declaration that the payment was accepted. If God gave His Son to save sinners, will He not answer prayers for their salvation? Romans 8:32 makes this argument: "He who did not spare His own Son, but delivered Him up for us all, how shall He not with Him also freely give us all things?" When you pray for the lost, you are standing on the finished work of Christ and joining the ongoing intercession of the risen Christ. There is no stronger position from which to pray.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Hebrews 7:25 — Therefore He is also able to save to the uttermost those who come to God through Him, since He ever lives to make intercession for them (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

John 17:20 — I do not pray for these alone, but also for those who will believe in Me through their word (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Romans 8:34 — Who is he who condemns? It is Christ who died, and furthermore is also risen, who is even at the right hand of God, who also makes intercession for us (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

1 John 5:14-15 — Now this is the confidence that we have in Him, that if we ask anything according to His will, He hears us. And if we know that He hears us, whatever we ask, we know that we have the petitions that we have asked of Him (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, before you pray for the five names on your list, pause and read Hebrews 7:25 aloud. Remind yourself that Jesus is praying with you and for you. Then pray with confidence, knowing that your prayers are joined with the intercession of Christ Himself.

### SUMMARY

- Jesus lives to intercede for us, and our prayers for the lost are carried into God's presence by Him.
- Praying in Jesus' name means praying in alignment with His will and joining His ongoing intercessory work.
- Because God desires all to be saved and gave His Son for that purpose, we can pray with bold confidence for the salvation of the lost.

## Lesson 3 — Praying for the Lost by Name

### HOOK

Think of a specific person you know who does not yet know Christ. You can picture their face, hear their voice, remember conversations you have had with them. Now ask yourself honestly: when was the last time you prayed for them by name, specifically, persistently, with real expectation that God would move?

### CORE TEACHING

Intercession for the lost is not meant to be a vague, general prayer — "Lord, save all the lost people of the world" — but a specific, intentional, persistent naming of individual souls before the throne of God. Paul modeled this when he wrote, "Brethren, my heart's desire and prayer to God for Israel is that they may be saved" (Romans 10:1). He was not praying generally for faceless masses; he was praying for his own people, his own family, his own nation. He prayed with personal anguish and specific longing. There are several things we should ask for when we pray for the lost by name. First, we ask God to open their hearts, just as He opened Lydia's heart (Acts 16:14). The natural human heart is blind to spiritual truth — the god of this age has blinded the minds of unbelievers (2 Corinthians 4:4). We are praying against that blindness and asking the Spirit to shine light into their understanding. Second, we ask for opportunities to speak. Paul asked the Colossians to pray that God would open a door for the Word (Colossians 4:3). We do not need to force conversations; we need to be ready when God opens the door. Third, we ask for boldness for ourselves. In the same passage, Paul asked for prayer that he would speak the mystery of Christ clearly. Fourth, we pray for the Holy Spirit to convict them of sin, righteousness, and judgment (John 16:8). Conviction is the Spirit's work, not ours. We cannot manufacture it. But we can pray for it. Finally, we pray with persistence. Jesus told the parable of the persistent widow (Luke 18:1-8) to teach that we should always pray and not lose heart. Some people we pray for may take years to come to Christ. That does not mean our prayers are failing. It means God is working in ways we cannot see. The cross paid for their salvation; the resurrection proved it. Our prayers do not add anything to that finished work, but they are the means God has chosen to apply it. Keep praying. Keep naming names. Keep asking.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Romans 10:1 — Brethren, my heart's desire and prayer to God for Israel is that they may be saved (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

2 Corinthians 4:3-4 — But even if our gospel is veiled, it is veiled to those who are perishing, whose minds the god of this age has blinded, who do not believe, lest the light of the gospel of the glory of Christ, who is the image of God, should shine on them (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Colossians 4:2-4 — Continue earnestly in prayer, being vigilant in it with thanksgiving; meanwhile praying also for us, that God would open to us a door for the word, to speak the mystery of Christ, for which I am also in chains, that I may reveal it as I ought to speak (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

John 16:8 — And when He has come, He will convict the world of sin, and of righteousness, and of judgment (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, take your list of five unsaved names and next to each name write one specific request: for one person, ask God to open their heart; for another, ask for an opportunity to speak; for another, ask for boldness for yourself. Pray through this specific list every day, and note any changes you see in your own concern for them.

### SUMMARY

- Intercession for the lost must be specific — praying for people by name with targeted requests.
- We pray for open hearts, open doors, personal boldness, and the Spirit's convicting work.
- Persistence in prayer is essential; God is working even when we cannot see immediate results.

## Lesson 4 — When Someone Says Yes: What Happens Next

### HOOK

You have just watched someone pray to receive Christ. There is joy in heaven, and the angels are rejoicing. But on earth, a question hangs in the air: now what? Everything they need to grow is waiting for them, but someone must show them where to find it.

### CORE TEACHING

When someone responds to the gospel, they are born again — but they are spiritual infants. A newborn baby cannot feed itself, cannot protect itself, and cannot survive without constant care. The same is true of a new believer. Their spirit has been made alive, but they have no idea how to pray, where to find truth in the Bible, what to do about their old habits, or how to relate to other Christians. This is where follow-up becomes the most urgent work in evangelism. The moment someone responds to Christ, the clock starts ticking. Studies and experience show that the vast majority of converts who are not followed up within the first 48 hours drift away. Not because their decision was insincere, but because no one helped them take their first steps. The apostle Peter compared new believers to newborn babies craving pure spiritual milk (1 Peter 2:2). That milk is the Word of God, and they need it immediately. The first thing you should do with a new believer is help them understand what just happened. Read Romans 10:9-10 with them again and assure them that if they have confessed Christ and believed in His resurrection, they are saved. Salvation is not based on feelings; it is based on the promise of God. Then give them a simple starting point: a Gospel to read, a plan for daily prayer, and a commitment to meet again soon. The cross and resurrection that brought them to salvation are also the foundation of their new life. They do not need to clean themselves up before coming to God — that is the old way of thinking. They come to God as they are, and God begins the work of transformation. Paul wrote that He who began a good work in them will complete it (Philippians 1:6). Follow-up is not about making them perfect overnight. It is about pointing them to the One who is perfect and letting Him do the work.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

1 Peter 2:2 — As newborn babes, desire the pure milk of the word, that you may grow thereby (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Philippians 1:6 — Being confident of this very thing, that He who has begun a good work in you will complete it until the day of Jesus Christ (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

2 Corinthians 5:17 — Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Acts 2:41-42 — Then those who gladly received his word were baptized; and that day about three thousand souls were added to them. And they continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine and fellowship, in the breaking of bread, and in prayers (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, prepare a simple "first steps" card or note for a new believer. Write down three things: read one chapter of the Gospel of John each day, pray each morning thanking God for saving you, and meet with me (or another believer) in three days. Keep this ready so that when someone responds to Christ, you can hand it to them immediately.

### SUMMARY

- A new believer is a spiritual infant who needs immediate care, teaching, and assurance.
- The first 48 hours after conversion are critical — follow-up must begin at once, not weeks later.
- The new believer's growth is grounded in the Word, prayer, and the confidence that God will complete what He started.

## Lesson 5 — Walking with a New Believer: The First 30 Days

### HOOK

A fire needs fuel, oxygen, and attention to keep burning. Left alone, the brightest flame will sputter and die. A new believer is the same. The fire of conversion is real, but without fuel — the Word, prayer, and fellowship — that fire will fade. The first thirty days determine the direction of a lifetime.

### CORE TEACHING

The period immediately following conversion is the most decisive in a believer's entire life. Habits formed in the first month often persist for years. Neglect in the first month can lead to a lifetime of struggle or even total apostasy. This is why follow-up is not optional — it is a spiritual emergency. The apostle Paul understood this urgency. After he and Barnabas preached in Derbe, Lystra, Iconium, and Antioch, they returned to each city to strengthen the disciples and encourage them to continue in the faith (Acts 14:21-22). They did not count decisions and move on. They went back. In your first meeting with a new believer, focus on three things. First, establish assurance of salvation. Read 1 John 5:11-13 together so they know they can be certain they have eternal life. Second, teach them to pray. Pray with them, not just for them. Model a simple, natural conversation with God. Third, open the Bible with them. Start with the Gospel of John — it was written specifically so that readers would believe that Jesus is the Christ (John 20:31). Read a passage together each time you meet and discuss what it means. In subsequent meetings, introduce the basics: the importance of baptism, the need for church fellowship, the practice of daily Bible reading, how to resist temptation, and how to share their new faith with others. Do not overwhelm them — one truth at a time, gently and patiently. The apostle Paul described his approach as gentle as a nursing mother caring for her own children (1 Thessalonians 2:7). That is the tone of effective follow-up: warm, patient, personal, and consistent. The cross and resurrection are the foundation you keep returning to. The new believer did not earn their salvation, and they cannot maintain it by their own effort. They grow by abiding in Christ. Your job is not to make them grow but to point them to the One who does.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Acts 14:21-22 — And when they had preached the gospel to that city and made many disciples, they returned to Lystra, Iconium, and Antioch, strengthening the souls of the disciples, exhorting them to continue in the faith, and saying, "We must through many tribulations enter the kingdom of God" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

1 Thessalonians 2:7-8 — But we were gentle among you, just as a nursing mother cherishes her own children. So, affectionately longing for you, we were well pleased to impart to you not only the gospel of God but also our own lives, because you had become dear to us (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

1 John 5:11-13 — And this is the testimony: that God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. He who has the Son has life; he who does not have the Son of God does not have life. These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life, and that you may continue to believe in the name of the Son of God (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, create a simple 30-day follow-up plan. Divide it into four weekly themes: Week 1 — Assurance and Prayer; Week 2 — Bible Reading and Baptism; Week 3 — Church Fellowship; Week 4 — Sharing Your Faith. If you do not have a new believer to disciple right now, prepare the plan anyway so you are ready when God brings someone across your path.

### SUMMARY

- The first thirty days after conversion are critical; habits formed now often last a lifetime.
- Effective follow-up focuses on assurance of salvation, learning to pray, reading Scripture, and connection to the church.
- The tone of follow-up must be gentle and patient, like a nursing mother caring for her child.

## Lesson 6 — Connecting New Believers to the Church

### HOOK

A single coal removed from the fire will cool and darken in minutes, no matter how brightly it was burning. A believer disconnected from the church is the same. They may have been ignited by the gospel, but without the heat of other believers around them, the flame will not survive.

### CORE TEACHING

One of the most urgent tasks in follow-up is connecting a new believer to a healthy local church. This is not a secondary concern or an optional extra. From the very first day of the church's existence, new believers were added to the fellowship of the church. Acts 2:41-42 records that three thousand people were baptized and added to the church on the day of Pentecost, and they devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching, fellowship, the breaking of bread, and prayer. The church was not an afterthought; it was the context in which their new life would grow. A new believer needs the church for several reasons. They need to hear the Word preached faithfully week after week, because the preaching of Scripture builds faith and corrects error. They need the fellowship of other believers, because isolation leads to vulnerability to doubt, temptation, and false teaching. They need the ordinances — baptism and communion — which are means of grace that strengthen faith. They need accountability from mature believers who can guide them when they stray. And they need opportunities to use their gifts in service to others. The writer of Hebrews commanded believers not to forsake assembling together, especially as the day of judgment approaches (Hebrews 10:24-25). This command applies to new believers as much as to anyone. As the person who led them to Christ or who is discipling them, you have a responsibility to help them find a church that preaches the gospel, believes the Bible, and loves the lost. Take them with you to your own church if possible. Introduce them to the pastor and to other believers your age. Help them understand that church is not a building they attend but a family they belong to. The cross and resurrection are the foundation of the church's message. A church that does not center on the crucified and risen Christ is not the kind of church a new believer needs. Be intentional about this connection. A new believer who is planted in a good church within the first weeks of their conversion has a much higher chance of growing to maturity.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Acts 2:42 — And they continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine and fellowship, in the breaking of bread, and in prayers (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Hebrews 10:24-25 — And let us consider one another in order to stir up love and good works, not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together, as is the manner of some, but exhorting one another, and so much the more as you see the Day approaching (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Ephesians 4:11-12 — And He Himself gave some to be apostles, some prophets, some evangelists, and some pastors and teachers, for the equipping of the saints for the work of ministry, for the edifying of the body of Christ (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, identify the three healthiest gospel-preaching churches in your area. Visit one of them if you have not been before, or confirm that your own church is sound. If you are currently discipling a new believer, invite them to join you at your church this Sunday and introduce them to at least three other believers after the service.

### SUMMARY

- The local church is God's design for the growth, protection, and equipping of every believer.
- New believers need the Word preached, fellowship with other believers, the ordinances, and accountability.
- Connecting a new believer to a healthy church within the first weeks of conversion is a critical responsibility for every disciple-maker.

## Lesson 7 — Commissioned to Go: The Harvest Is Waiting

### HOOK

If you have learned everything in this module but never acted on it, you are like a farmer who knows how to plant, how to water, and how to harvest, but never leaves the house. Knowledge without obedience is not growth — it is self-deception. The question is not whether you know what to do. The question is whether you will do it.

### CORE TEACHING

You have now completed the entire Level 2 evangelism curriculum. You have learned what the gospel is and why souls matter. You have learned how the Holy Spirit works in evangelism, how to start conversations, and how to share your testimony. You have learned to understand who you are talking to, how to lead someone to Christ, and what you are actually asking them to commit to. You have learned to overcome fear, to make evangelism a lifestyle, to use Scripture in conversation, and now to pray for the lost and follow up with new believers. You have the tools. The question that remains is not academic but personal: will you go? The Great Commission is not a suggestion from a gentle teacher. It is a command from the risen King who holds all authority in heaven and on earth. Jesus said, "Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all things that I have commanded you" (Matthew 28:19-20). The authority behind that command is the authority of the One who died and rose again. The cross purchased the salvation of every soul who will believe. The resurrection proved that death has been defeated and that the gospel is true. The command to go is rooted in the finished work of Christ. You are not being sent because you are qualified; you are being sent because you belong to the King. He has promised to be with you always, even to the end of the age. You do not go alone. The Holy Spirit goes before you, the risen Christ intercedes for you, and the Father's love pursues the lost through you. The harvest is plentiful. The workers are few. Every day that passes is a day closer to the return of the Lord. Every soul you reach is someone who will spend eternity with Christ. Every soul you do not reach is a soul that will spend eternity without Him. This is not guilt meant to crush you; it is reality meant to move you. You cannot save everyone, but you can save someone. You can pray for them. You can speak to them. You can walk with them after they believe. That is what you have been trained for. Now go.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Matthew 28:18-20 — And Jesus came and spoke to them, saying, "All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth. Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all things that I have commanded you; and lo, I am with you always, even to the end of the age" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

John 4:35 — Do you not say, "There are still four months and then comes the harvest"? Behold, I say to you, lift up your eyes and look at the fields, for they are already white for harvest (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

2 Corinthians 5:20 — Now then, we are ambassadors for Christ, as though God were pleading through us: we implore you on Christ's behalf, be reconciled to God (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, choose one person from your prayer list and take a concrete step toward sharing the gospel with them — invite them for a meal, send them a message, or ask them a question that opens a spiritual conversation. Do not wait for a perfect moment. The perfect moment is now.

### SUMMARY

- You have been equipped with everything you need to evangelize; obedience is now the only missing piece.
- The Great Commission is a command from the risen Christ, backed by His authority and His promise to be with you.
- The harvest is ready, and every day matters — go, pray, speak, and make disciples.

### MODULE CONCLUSION — A Commissioning Prayer

Go now in the name of the Father who sent His Son, the Son who died and rose for the lost, and the Holy Spirit who empowers every word and every step. You are not alone. The harvest is waiting. Pray without ceasing, speak without fear, and disciple without hesitation — until every nation hears that Jesus Christ is Lord.
