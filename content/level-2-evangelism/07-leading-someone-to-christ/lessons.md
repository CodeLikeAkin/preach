# Module 07 — Leading Someone to Christ

---

## Lesson 1 — The Goal Is Repentance and Faith, Not a Prayer

### HOOK
Imagine handing someone a set of words to repeat, telling them those words will save them, and then watching them walk away unchanged — convinced they are safe when they have never actually met the Savior. That is what happens every day when the goal of evangelism becomes getting a prayer rather than securing a conversion.

### CORE TEACHING
The most loving thing you can do when someone is ready to respond to the gospel is to give them something far more valuable than a script: you must give them clarity. The goal of leading someone to Christ is not to get them to recite a prayer after you. It is to see them genuinely repent of their sins and place their trust in Jesus Christ alone. These two realities — repentance and faith — are the door through which every person must enter. They are not two separate steps on a checklist but two sides of the same movement of the heart. Imagine a person standing at a crossroads. Repentance is turning away from the road they were on. Faith is stepping onto the new road. You cannot do one without the other. If a person says they believe but refuse to turn from their sin, their faith is hollow. If they try to clean up their life without trusting Christ, they are merely rearranging sin into self-righteousness. This is why the shortcut of a recited prayer is so dangerous. A person can repeat words with sincerity and still walk away with nothing more than a false assurance — inoculated against the real gospel because they believe they have already done what is required. The goal, then, is not to get a decision but to see a conversion. A decision can happen in a moment with no change of heart. Conversion happens when the Holy Spirit regenerates a soul, and that person, now alive to God, turns from sin and embraces Christ. Your role is not to manufacture that moment but to clear the path, explain what repentance and faith actually mean, and then let the Spirit do what only He can.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Mark 1:15 — "and saying, 'The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand. Repent, and believe in the gospel.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Acts 20:21 — "testifying to Jews, and also to Greeks, repentance toward God and faith toward our Lord Jesus Christ." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- 2 Corinthians 7:10 — "For godly sorrow produces repentance leading to salvation, not to be regretted; but the sorrow of the world produces death." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write down in one sentence each what you would say to someone who asks, "What does repentance mean?" and "What does it mean to believe?" Practice saying them aloud until you can explain both clearly in under thirty seconds.

### SUMMARY
- The goal of leading someone to Christ is genuine repentance and faith, not a recited prayer.
- Repentance and faith are two inseparable movements of one conversion.
- A person can pray a prayer sincerely and still be unconverted if there is no actual turning from sin and trusting in Christ.

## Lesson 2 — Understanding Repentance

### HOOK
A man once told a friend that he had decided to stop drinking, stop lying, and start going to church, and he called it repentance. He had changed his habits but not his heart. Six months later, he was drinking again, lying again, and done with church — because he had cleaned the outside without ever changing the inside.

### CORE TEACHING
Repentance is one of the most misunderstood words in evangelism. Many people hear it and think of a desperate person trying to earn God's favor by giving up sins. But that is not repentance; that is moralism, and it is exhausting. The best definition of repentance is a change of mind that leads to a changed life. It is not merely being sorry for sin. The Bible speaks of a "godly sorrow" that leads to repentance, but sorrow alone is not enough. A person can be sorry they got caught and still love their sin. True repentance begins when the Holy Spirit opens a person's eyes to see God for who He really is — holy, righteous, and just — and to see themselves for who they really are — guilty, helpless, and deserving of judgment. This is not a comfortable experience. It is like the moment a person realizes they have been walking in the wrong direction their entire life. Everything they thought about God, about themselves, about what matters — it all has to be reexamined. That is what happened to the Apostle Paul on the road to Damascus. He thought he was serving God by persecuting Christians. In one moment, he realized he was wrong about everything that mattered. That is repentance — not just giving up a few bad habits but having your entire worldview shattered and rebuilt around the truth of who God is and what He has done in Christ. Repentance is not a work you perform to earn salvation. It is the necessary turning that happens when you see the truth. You cannot truly come to Christ without it, because you cannot come to the light while clinging to the darkness. This is why helping someone understand repentance is so important. If they think they can simply add Jesus to their life without turning from sin, they have not understood the gospel. They are asking Jesus to join their kingdom instead of surrendering to His.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Acts 3:19 — "Repent therefore and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out, so that times of refreshing may come from the presence of the Lord." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- 2 Corinthians 7:10 — "For godly sorrow produces repentance leading to salvation, not to be regretted; but the sorrow of the world produces death." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Acts 26:20 — "but declared first to those in Damascus and in Jerusalem, and throughout all the region of Judea, and then to the Gentiles, that they should repent, turn to God, and do works befitting repentance." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, think of one person you know who is not a believer. Write down three specific ways their life would need to change if they genuinely repented. Use these to help you pray for them and to clarify your own understanding of what you are asking them to do.

### SUMMARY
- Repentance is a change of mind about God, sin, and self that results in a changed life.
- It is not merely feeling sorry for sin or cleaning up behavior without a changed heart.
- Repentance is not a work that earns salvation but the necessary turning that happens when a person sees the truth about God and themselves.

## Lesson 3 — Faith That Saves

### HOOK
A man once said he believed a bridge was strong enough to hold him. He had read the reports, studied the engineering, and told everyone he was confident. But when the moment came to step onto the bridge, he refused. His belief was real as far as it went, but it was not enough to move his feet.

### CORE TEACHING
Saving faith is more than agreeing that certain facts about Jesus are true. Even the demons believe that Jesus is the Son of God, and they tremble. Their belief is intellectually correct, but it does not save them. Saving faith includes three elements that must all be present. The first is knowledge. A person must understand who Jesus is and what He has done. They must know that Christ died for their sins according to the Scriptures, that He was buried, and that He rose again on the third day. Without this basic knowledge, there is nothing to believe. But knowledge alone is not enough. The second element is assent. A person must agree that these things are true. They must be convinced that the gospel is not a story or a metaphor but actual history with actual consequences. Many people in church have this much faith. They believe the facts of the gospel the way they believe historical facts about an ancient king. It is true, but it does not change their lives. The third element is trust. This is where saving faith becomes real. Trust means personally relying on Jesus Christ alone for salvation. It is one thing to believe that a parachute can save you; it is another thing to put it on and jump out of the plane. Saving faith is the moment when a person stops trusting in their own goodness, their own religious efforts, or their own decision, and instead rests entirely on the finished work of Christ. This is why you cannot pressure someone into saving faith. True trust is not manufactured by emotional manipulation or by walking someone through a script. It is awakened by the Holy Spirit through the Word of God. Your job is to present Christ clearly — who He is, what He did, why it matters — and then let the Holy Spirit use that truth to produce genuine faith. When a person truly sees Jesus for who He is, they will not need to be pushed. They will come.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- James 2:19 — "You believe that there is one God. You do well. Even the demons believe — and tremble!" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Romans 10:9-10 — "that if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved. For with the heart one believes unto righteousness, and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- John 3:16 — "For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, take a piece of paper and write "knowledge," "assent," and "trust" as three headings. Under each one, write one sentence explaining that element to a new believer. Keep this paper where you can refer to it when you talk to someone about the gospel.

### SUMMARY
- Saving faith includes knowledge of the facts, agreement that they are true, and personal trust in Christ alone.
- Intellectual belief alone is not enough; even the demons believe and tremble.
- True faith is awakened by the Holy Spirit through the Word and rests entirely on Christ's finished work.

## Lesson 4 — The Moment of Decision: What to Say and What Not to Say

### HOOK
A friend once sat across from someone who had just understood the gospel for the first time. The person's eyes were wet, their voice was shaking, and they said, "What do I do now?" In that moment, the wrong words could give them false peace. The right words could lead them into genuine life.

### CORE TEACHING
When a person is ready to respond to the gospel, the pressure to say the right thing can feel overwhelming. But the most important principle is this: do not replace the work of the Holy Spirit with your own words. Your goal is not to get them to repeat a prayer so you can pronounce them saved. Your goal is to help them, in their own words, turn from their sin and call upon the Lord. Start by asking clarifying questions. Ask them, "What do you understand about what we have discussed?" Let them explain the gospel back to you in their own words. If they cannot articulate it clearly, they are not ready to respond. Ask them, "Why do you want to follow Christ?" Make sure they are not responding out of fear of hell alone or pressure from you, but because they see Jesus for who He is. Then call them to respond directly to God. You can say something like, "You do not need to repeat words after me. Talk to God. Tell Him what you understand about your sin. Thank Him for what Christ did on the cross. Tell Him you are turning from your sin, and tell Him you are trusting Jesus alone to save you." If they are unsure what to say, you can offer a simple frame: "You can tell God that you know you have sinned against Him, that you believe Jesus died for your sins and rose again, and that you are putting your trust in Him alone." But let them speak from their heart, not from your script. When they finish, do not immediately declare them saved. Instead, point them to the promises of Scripture. Say something like, "If you have truly turned from your sin and put your trust in Christ, then what God says is true — you are forgiven." Then open the Bible and read promises like John 5:24 or Romans 10:9-10 with them. Let the Word, not your word, be the foundation of their assurance. Avoid telling them, "You are saved because you prayed that prayer." The prayer did not save them. Christ saves them. The prayer was simply their expression of faith.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Romans 10:13 — "For 'whoever calls on the name of the LORD shall be saved.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- John 5:24 — "Most assuredly, I say to you, he who hears My word and believes in Him who sent Me has everlasting life, and shall not come into judgment, but has passed from death into life." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Acts 16:30-31 — "And he brought them out and said, 'Sirs, what must I do to be saved?' So they said, 'Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and you will be saved, you and your household.'" (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write out three questions you would ask someone who says they want to respond to the gospel. Write them down, memorize them, and practice asking them in a conversation with a fellow believer.

### SUMMARY
- Let the person speak to God in their own words rather than repeating a scripted prayer.
- Ask clarifying questions to confirm they understand the gospel before they respond.
- Point them to the promises of Scripture for assurance, not to the prayer they recited.

## Lesson 5 — Leading With Scripture, Not Formulas

### HOOK
A builder once watched a man try to drive a nail using the handle of a hammer instead of the head. The nail bent, the wood split, and nothing was secured. The fault was not with the hammer but with how it was used. In the same way, the Word of God is powerful, but we must use it as it was intended.

### CORE TEACHING
The most important tool you have when leading someone to Christ is not a tract, a method, or a scripted prayer. It is the Word of God. The apostle Paul wrote that faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God. The Holy Spirit has promised to work through Scripture, not through human cleverness. This means that when you are with someone who is ready to respond, your instinct should be to open the Bible. Instead of asking them to repeat a prayer after you, walk them through key passages that explain the gospel, explain repentance, and explain faith. Let them read the words themselves. There is something powerful about a person reading John 3:16 with their own eyes and realizing, "This promise is for me." Do not rush past this moment. When a person is on the edge of conversion, they do not need you to say more. They need the Word to do its work. Let them sit under the Scripture. Let the Holy Spirit use the verses to produce faith. There is a reason the Bible describes the Word of God as a sword, a hammer, and a fire. It does not return void. But it must be wielded, not buried under human commentary. Many evangelistic methods skip this step. They move directly from presenting the gospel to having the person repeat a prayer, with almost no Scripture in between. This is like handing someone a map and then immediately telling them to start walking before they have looked at it. When you lead someone to Christ, put the Bible in their hands. Read promises to them. Ask them what they think. Let the Word be the voice that calls them to repentance and faith.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Romans 10:17 — "So then faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Isaiah 55:11 — "So shall My word be that goes forth from My mouth; it shall not return to Me void, but it shall accomplish what I please, and it shall prosper in the thing for which I sent it." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Hebrews 4:12 — "For the word of God is living and powerful, and sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing even to the division of soul and spirit, and of joints and marrow, and is a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- 1 Peter 1:23 — "having been born again, not of corruptible seed but incorruptible, through the word of God which lives and abides forever." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, memorize John 3:16, Romans 10:9-10, and Acts 3:19 so that you can quote them from memory in a conversation without needing to look them up. Practice saying each verse aloud five times every day.

### SUMMARY
- Faith is produced by the Word of God, not by human methods or scripts.
- When leading someone to Christ, put Scripture in their hands and let them read the promises themselves.
- The Holy Spirit works through the Word to bring genuine conversion.

## Lesson 6 — Assurance for the New Believer

### HOOK
A young woman who had just trusted Christ woke up the next morning terrified. She felt the same, saw the same world, and worried that nothing had actually changed. Without someone to help her anchor her confidence in God's promise, she might have drifted back into doubt and despair.

### CORE TEACHING
Assurance of salvation is one of the most precious gifts a new believer can receive, but it must be built on the right foundation. Many people look back at a prayer they prayed or a decision they made and try to draw confidence from that moment. But a moment cannot sustain a lifetime of faith. Only the promises of God can do that. When you are with a new believer, your first task is to anchor them in Scripture. Open to passages that contain clear promises of salvation for those who trust in Christ. John 5:24 says that whoever hears Jesus' word and believes in the Father who sent Him has everlasting life and shall not come into judgment. This is not a promise for those who prayed perfectly. It is for those who believe. Help them see that their assurance rests on what Christ has done, not on how they feel. Feelings fluctuate. A new believer may feel joyful one moment and terrified the next. That is normal. But the Word of God does not change. When they doubt, take them back to the verses. Ask them, "Do you believe that Jesus died for your sins and rose again? Are you trusting in Him alone?" If they say yes, then remind them that God says they are forgiven. The evidence of salvation is not the strength of their feelings but the object of their faith. At the same time, Scripture does point to evidence of genuine conversion. A new believer should see changes beginning in their life — a growing love for God, sorrow over sin, a desire for other believers, an appetite for God's Word. These are not the basis of their assurance but the confirmation of it. You can help them see these signs gently, without making them feel they must be perfect. The goal is to give them confidence that God, who began a good work in them, will carry it on to completion. This is not a guarantee that they will never struggle. It is a guarantee that God is faithful.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- John 5:24 — "Most assuredly, I say to you, he who hears My word and believes in Him who sent Me has everlasting life, and shall not come into judgment, but has passed from death into life." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- 1 John 5:11-13 — "And this is the testimony: that God has given us eternal life, and this life is in His Son. He who has the Son has life; he who does not have the Son of God does not have life. These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life, and that you may continue to believe in the name of the Son of God." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Philippians 1:6 — "being confident of this very thing, that He who has begun a good work in you will complete it until the day of Jesus Christ." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, write down John 5:24, 1 John 5:11-13, and Philippians 1:6 on a small card. Carry it with you. The next time you talk to a new believer, give them the card and read through the verses together.

### SUMMARY
- Assurance rests on the promises of God in Scripture, not on a past prayer or decision.
- Feelings fluctuate, but the Word of God does not change — anchor new believers in what God has said.
- Genuine conversion produces fruit over time, which confirms but does not replace faith in Christ as the basis of assurance.

## Lesson 7 — From Salvation to Baptism and Next Steps

### HOOK
A man who had just learned to swim was left alone in the deep end of a pool. He had taken the first step, but he had no idea what to do next. He could either be rescued by someone who showed him how to keep moving, or he could sink. New believers face the same reality.

### CORE TEACHING
The moment a person trusts Christ is the beginning, not the end. Yet many evangelists treat conversion as a finish line. They move on to the next person, leaving the new believer alone and unsure what to do next. This is not how the New Testament works. In the book of Acts, people who believed were immediately baptized, connected to other believers, and taught the apostles' doctrine. Conversion was always followed by community and growth. If you lead someone to Christ, you bear some responsibility for helping them take the next steps. Baptism is the first and most urgent next step. It is not optional. Jesus commanded it, and the early church practiced it immediately. A new believer should understand that baptism is not what saves them but is their public declaration of faith and their identification with Christ's death and resurrection. Help them find a local church where the gospel is preached and where they can grow. This is not always easy. Some new believers may have no idea how to find a healthy church. They need guidance. Offer to visit a church with them for the first few weeks. Introduce them to other believers. Help them connect with people who will walk alongside them. The first days of the Christian life are fragile. A new believer needs three things: the Word, prayer, and community. Encourage them to start reading the Bible daily, even if it is just a few verses. Teach them to pray simply — talking to God like a child talking to a father. And help them find at least one mature believer who can answer their questions and encourage them when they struggle. You do not need to be their permanent discipler. But you can be the bridge that gets them started.

### KEY SCRIPTURES
- Acts 2:41-42 — "Then those who gladly received his word were baptized; and that day about three thousand souls were added to them. And they continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine and fellowship, in the breaking of bread, and in prayers." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Matthew 28:19-20 — "Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all things that I have commanded you." (NKJV) [VERIFY]
- Romans 6:3-4 — "Or do you not know that as many of us as were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into His death? Therefore we were buried with Him through baptism into death, that just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life." (NKJV) [VERIFY]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
This week, identify three healthy churches in your area that preach the gospel faithfully. Write down their names, addresses, and service times. If you lead someone to Christ, you will be ready to help them find a church home immediately.

### SUMMARY
- Conversion is the beginning, not the end — every new believer needs to take the next steps of baptism, community, and growth.
- Baptism is the first act of obedience and a public declaration of faith in Christ.
- A new believer needs the Word, prayer, and community to grow; help them find a healthy church and a mature believer to walk with them.
