# Study Guide — Leading Someone to Christ

## 1. Core Definition

Leading someone to Christ means walking a person who has understood the gospel through the moment of genuine repentance and faith toward the Lord Jesus Christ, and then helping them establish their assurance on the promises of Scripture rather than on a ritual, a prayer, or a past decision. It is not a human technique that produces salvation but a Spirit-dependent process of calling sinners to turn from sin and trust in Christ alone.

## 2. Key Biblical Truths

- **Repentance and faith are the two sides of the same coin.** Jesus preached, "Repent, and believe in the gospel" (Mark 1:15). Repentance is a change of mind about sin, self, and God that leads to a changed life. Faith is the abandonment of all self-trust and the resting of the whole person on Christ's finished work. Neither is a single event; both mark the Christian's ongoing posture. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Regeneration precedes and enables repentance and faith.** A person who is spiritually dead cannot respond to God until the Holy Spirit makes them alive (Ephesians 2:1-5). The new birth is not the result of a decision; the decision is the result of the new birth. As John writes, "Whoever believes that Jesus is the Christ is born of God" (1 John 5:1) — the believing flows from having been born. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Genuine conversion produces a changed life.** The evidence that a person has truly come to Christ is not that they prayed a prayer but that they continue repenting and believing. The one who is born again walks in newness of life (Romans 6:4). A life marked by ongoing sin without repentance calls the reality of conversion into question. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **Assurance rests on the promises of God, not on human ceremony.** When a person trusts Christ, the Holy Spirit bears witness with their spirit that they are a child of God (Romans 8:16). Assurance is built through the Word, not through a leader pronouncing them saved after a recited prayer. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

- **The sinner's prayer (as a formula) is not found in Scripture.** Nowhere does the Bible instruct an evangelist to lead someone in a recited prayer to secure salvation. Salvation comes through calling on the Lord (Romans 10:13), but such a call flows from genuine faith, not from repeating words after someone. [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

## 3. Common Misconceptions

- **"The sinner's prayer saves you."** The belief that reciting a prayer (sincerely or not) guarantees salvation is one of the most dangerous errors in modern evangelism. No biblical passage prescribes this method. Salvation is by grace through faith, not by praying a specific formula.

- **"A person can be saved and then live like the devil."** Known as "easy-believism," this view divorces salvation from transformation. The New Testament consistently teaches that regeneration results in a changed life. While believers still struggle with sin, they cannot live in habitual, unrepentant sin without evidence that their profession was false.

- **"If someone prayed the prayer once, they are always saved regardless of how they live."** This confuses the perseverance of the saints with a license to sin. True believers persevere. Those who fall away entirely demonstrate they were never of us (1 John 2:19).

- **"I can tell someone they are saved immediately after they pray."** Only God knows the heart. The evangelist's role is to present the gospel clearly, call for repentance and faith, and then point to the promises of Scripture. The Holy Spirit gives assurance, not the evangelist.

## 4. Practical Application Angles

- **Learn to distinguish between walking someone through a formula and walking them through Scripture and conviction.** Focus on using the Word to bring understanding, not on getting a quick decision.

- **Develop a clear, biblical explanation of repentance.** Help people understand that repentance is not just sorrow for sin but a fundamental change of mind about who God is, who they are, and what they need.

- **Practice pausing after presenting the gospel.** Allow the Holy Spirit room to work. Silence and space often produce more genuine responses than hurried pressure to "pray this prayer."

- **Equip new believers with specific Scripture promises for assurance.** John 3:16, John 5:24, Romans 10:9-10, and 1 John 5:11-13 are key passages to walk through with a new convert.

- **Plan the follow-up conversation before you have it.** Know what you will say about baptism, church involvement, and the first steps of discipleship before you lead someone to Christ.

- **Learn to let the Word do the work.** When someone struggles with doubt or assurance, take them to the promises of God rather than trying to convince them with your own authority.

## 5. Key Terms & Definitions

- **Repentance (metanoia):** A change of mind, heart, and direction. It is turning from sin and self and turning toward God. It is not a work that earns salvation but the necessary flip side of faith.

- **Faith (pistis):** Trusting in the person and work of Jesus Christ alone for salvation. It includes intellectual assent (belief that the gospel is true), personal reliance (trusting Christ as Savior), and allegiance (submitting to Him as Lord).

- **Regeneration:** The supernatural work of the Holy Spirit by which a spiritually dead person is made alive. It is monergistic — a work of God alone — and precedes repentance and faith logically.

- **Conversion:** The human response to the gospel enabled by regeneration. It consists of repentance (turning from sin) and faith (turning to Christ). These occur together, not sequentially.

- **Assurance:** The confidence that one belongs to Christ, based on the promises of Scripture and the internal witness of the Holy Spirit, not on a past decision or prayer.

- **The sinner's prayer:** A common modern practice in which an evangelist leads a person in a recited prayer to "receive Christ." While calling on God is biblical, the formulaic use of this prayer as a guarantee of salvation is not supported by Scripture.

- **False conversion:** A profession of faith that does not result in a transformed life. Jesus warned about those who say "Lord, Lord" but do not enter the kingdom (Matthew 7:21-23).
