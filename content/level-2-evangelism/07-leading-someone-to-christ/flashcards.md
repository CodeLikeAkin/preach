# Flashcards — Leading Someone to Christ

## Q&A Pairs

**1. Q: What is the goal of leading someone to Christ?**
A: The goal is genuine repentance and faith in Jesus Christ, not merely getting someone to repeat a prayer. Salvation comes through turning from sin and trusting Christ, not through reciting a formula.

**2. Q: What is repentance?**
A: Repentance is a change of mind about God, sin, and self that leads to a changed life. It is not merely feeling sorry for sin but a fundamental turning from sin toward God.

**3. Q: Is the sinner's prayer found in the Bible?**
A: No. Nowhere does the Bible instruct an evangelist to lead someone through a recited prayer as a method of salvation. Calling on the Lord is biblical, but it flows from genuine faith, not from repeating a script.

**4. Q: What are the three elements of saving faith?**
A: Knowledge (understanding the gospel), assent (agreeing it is true), and trust (personally relying on Christ alone for salvation). All three must be present.

**5. Q: Can a person be saved by praying a prayer but never changing?**
A: No. Genuine conversion produces a changed life. While believers still struggle with sin, a life of habitual, unrepentant sin calls the reality of their profession into question.

**6. Q: How should you respond when someone wants to trust Christ?**
A: Let them speak to God in their own words rather than repeating a script. Ask clarifying questions to confirm their understanding. Then point them to the promises of Scripture for assurance.

**7. Q: What is the role of Scripture in leading someone to Christ?**
A: Faith comes by hearing the Word of God. The Holy Spirit works through Scripture to produce faith. When leading someone to Christ, put the Bible in their hands and let the promises do their work.

**8. Q: How should a new believer find assurance?**
A: Their assurance rests on the promises of God in Scripture, not on a past prayer or decision. They should trust in what God has said about those who believe in His Son.

**9. Q: What is the first step for a new believer after conversion?**
A: Baptism. It is the first act of obedience Jesus commanded and a public declaration of faith. It does not save but identifies the believer with Christ's death and resurrection.

**10. Q: What three things does a new believer need to grow?**
A: The Word of God (daily reading), prayer (talking to God), and community (a healthy local church and mature believers to walk alongside them).
