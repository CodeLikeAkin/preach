# Flashcards — Using Scripture in Conversation

## 10 Question & Answer Pairs

### Card 1
**Q: Why should Scripture lead in an evangelistic conversation rather than your own words?**
**A:** Because faith comes by hearing the Word of God (Romans 10:17). Scripture carries God's own authority and power — your words can inform a mind, but only God's Word can pierce a heart and produce saving faith.

### Card 2
**Q: How do you use the law to reach someone who thinks they are a good person?**
**A:** Ask gentle diagnostic questions based on the Ten Commandments: Have you lied? Stolen? Used God's name in vain? Looked with lust? The law functions as a mirror to show them their sin and their need for a Savior.

### Card 3
**Q: What verses make up the Roman Road and what is their order?**
**A:** Romans 3:23 (all have sinned), Romans 6:23 (wages of sin is death, gift of God is eternal life), Romans 5:8 (Christ died while we were sinners), Romans 10:9-10 (confess and believe), Romans 10:13 (whoever calls on the Lord shall be saved).

### Card 4
**Q: How do you answer someone who says, "I don't believe the Bible is true"?**
**A:** Briefly share that the Bible is a reliable collection of historical documents written by eyewitnesses during the lifetime of other eyewitnesses (2 Peter 1:16). Then gently redirect to the conscience — often the real issue is not intellectual doubt but moral resistance.

### Card 5
**Q: What is the difference between approaching a proud person and a guilty person with Scripture?**
**A:** The proud person needs the law to expose their sin. The guilty person already knows they are sinful and needs the promise of grace — verses like Romans 5:8 and Romans 8:1 that offer full pardon through Christ.

### Card 6
**Q: What does it mean to let Scripture do the heavy lifting in evangelism?**
**A:** It means answering questions with verses rather than long explanations, trusting that the Word of God will not return void (Isaiah 55:11), and knowing that the gospel itself — not your presentation — is the power of God for salvation.

### Card 7
**Q: How do you answer the objection "There is no evidence for God"?**
**A:** Romans 1:20 says God's invisible attributes are clearly seen through creation. A building implies a builder. The real issue is not lack of evidence but love for sin, which is why the law should follow.

### Card 8
**Q: How do you answer "Christianity is just one of many religions"?**
**A:** Jesus said, "I am the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through Me" (John 14:6). Christianity is exclusive because Jesus made an exclusive claim — only He died for sins and rose again.

### Card 9
**Q: How do you answer "A loving God wouldn't send people to hell"?**
**A:** A judge does not send a criminal to prison — the crime does. Sin sends people to hell. God is both loving and just; the cross is where His love and justice meet. He provided a way of escape through Christ.

### Card 10
**Q: How do you answer "The church is full of hypocrites"?**
**A:** A hypocrite is a pretender, not a true Christian. Jesus said the wheat and tares (true and false believers) grow together until judgment. The presence of hypocrites does not invalidate the truth of the gospel.
