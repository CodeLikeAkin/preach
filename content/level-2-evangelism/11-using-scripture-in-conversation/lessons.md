# Lessons — Using Scripture in Conversation

---

## Lesson 1 — Why Scripture Must Lead

### HOOK

Imagine trying to start a fire with your bare hands. You can rub sticks together until your palms bleed, but without a spark, nothing catches. That is what evangelism feels like when we rely on our own words, our own cleverness, our own persuasion. We exhaust ourselves and wonder why no one catches fire. The missing element is not more effort — it is the Word of God itself.

### CORE TEACHING

Many believers approach evangelism as if they need to be the spark. They prepare arguments, memorize talking points, and rehearse their delivery, and then they wonder why their words seem to bounce off the people they are talking to. The problem is not their sincerity or their effort; it is that they are trying to generate power that only God's Word can supply. Scripture is not just information about God; it is the living instrument through which God works. When you speak the Bible into a conversation, you are not merely quoting an ancient text — you are bringing the voice of the living God into the room. The apostle Paul wrote that faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word of God (Romans 10:17). That means faith is not produced by eloquent speeches or logical proofs. It is produced when the Word of God lands on a heart that the Spirit has prepared. This changes everything about how you prepare for evangelism. Instead of spending all your energy crafting the perfect argument, you spend your energy knowing the Scriptures themselves. You memorize key passages not so you can win debates but so you can speak God's words rather than your own. The difference is profound. When you say "I think..." a person can argue with your opinion. But when you say "The Bible says..." they are wrestling with something far bigger than you. The cross and resurrection are the center of this message. Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures and rose again according to the Scriptures. The power is in the event, and the event is preserved in the Word. Your job is not to make the Word powerful — it already is. Your job is to bring it into the conversation and trust the Holy Spirit to do what only He can do.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Romans 10:17 — So then faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

1 Peter 3:15 — But sanctify the Lord God in your hearts, and always be ready to give a defense to everyone who asks you a reason for the hope that is in you, with meekness and fear (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Hebrews 4:12 — For the word of God is living and powerful, and sharper than any two-edged sword, piercing even to the division of soul and spirit, and of joints and marrow, and is a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, pick one verse from the Key Scriptures above, write it on a card, and practice saying it aloud five times each day until you can say it from memory without looking. Then look for one natural moment in conversation to say it — even if the conversation is not about spiritual things yet.

### SUMMARY

- Faith is produced by the Word of God, not by human eloquence or argumentation.
- Speaking Scripture into a conversation carries God's own authority in a way that human opinions cannot.
- Your primary preparation for evangelism is knowing the Scriptures themselves, not crafting perfect arguments.

## Lesson 2 — Using the Law to Reach the Proud

### HOOK

Think of the last time you met someone who genuinely believed they were a good person. They may have said it outright: "I live a decent life. I help people. I have never murdered anyone. If there is a God, He should let me in." That confidence is not arrogance in the usual sense — it is blindness. And the cure for blindness is not an argument. It is a mirror.

### CORE TEACHING

The proud person does not need to hear how much God loves them. They already think they deserve His love. What they need is to see themselves honestly, and the only mirror accurate enough for that is the law of God. The apostle Paul wrote that by the law is the knowledge of sin (Romans 3:20). The law does not save anyone, but it silences every excuse. When you gently ask someone, "Have you kept the Ten Commandments?" you are not accusing them. You are handing them a mirror. You ask: "Have you ever told a lie? What do you call a person who lies?" They know the answer. "Have you ever taken something that did not belong to you, no matter how small? What does that make you?" The answers are already in their conscience. "Have you ever used God's name as a curse word?" Most people have, and they know it is wrong. "Have you ever looked at someone with lust?" Jesus said that makes you an adulterer at heart. By the time you finish these four questions, the person who started the conversation thinking they were a good person is suddenly silent. They are not silent because you won an argument. They are silent because the law has done its work. It has exposed them. At this point, the human instinct is to rush in with grace to make them feel better. But resist that instinct for a moment. Let the weight of their guilt settle. A person who does not feel the disease will not value the cure. Only when they admit, "I am a liar, a thief, a blasphemer, and an adulterer at heart — I deserve judgment," is grace truly good news. The cross becomes meaningful not when a person thinks they need a little help but when they realize they need a resurrection. This is not about making people feel bad. It is about telling them the truth so they can be saved. God's kindness leads to repentance, and part of that kindness is the honesty of the law.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Romans 3:20 — Therefore by the deeds of the law no flesh will be justified in His sight, for by the law is the knowledge of sin (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Exodus 20:1-17 — The Ten Commandments (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Matthew 5:27-28 — You have heard that it was said to those of old, "You shall not commit adultery." But I say to you that whoever looks at a woman to lust for her has already committed adultery with her in his heart (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, memorize the four diagnostic questions from this lesson (lying, stealing, blasphemy, lust) and ask them of one person you trust — not an unbeliever — just to practice the flow. Then pray for an opportunity to use them with someone who needs to hear them.

### SUMMARY

- The law exposes sin and silences self-righteousness, preparing the heart to receive grace.
- Gentle, diagnostic questions based on the Ten Commandments are more effective than direct accusations.
- The good news of grace only lands as good news after a person has felt the weight of their guilt.

## Lesson 3 — Grace for the Guilty

### HOOK

Some people you meet will not tell you they are good people. They will tell you they are broken, that they have made a mess of their lives, that God could never want someone like them. They already know they are guilty. They do not need a mirror. They need a physician.

### CORE TEACHING

The person who is already crushed by their own sin requires a completely different approach than the proud person. If you start going through the Ten Commandments with someone who already believes they are a failure, you will only drive them deeper into despair. They already know they have failed. What they need is not more diagnosis but a cure. This is where the gospel shines brightest. To the guilty, you do not bring the law — you bring the promise. You turn to passages like Romans 5:8: "But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us." Notice the timing — not after we cleaned ourselves up, but while we were still sinners. That is the hope the guilty desperately need. You explain that God does not require them to fix themselves before coming to Him. In fact, He knows they cannot. That is why Jesus came. He lived the perfect life they could not live, died the death they deserved to die, and rose again to prove that the payment was accepted. Everything they need has already been accomplished. Their part is not to earn it but to receive it. You walk them through the simplicity of the call: repent — turn from their sin — and believe — put their trust in Christ alone. You show them that salvation is not about trying harder but about surrendering to the One who has already done everything. The guilty person often hesitates because they feel unworthy. The truth is that none of us are worthy. That is the whole point. Grace is not God overlooking sin; it is God punishing sin in Christ so that He can forgive the sinner justly. The cross is not a tragedy — it is a triumph. The resurrection is not a story — it is the proof. When you show a guilty person that their sin has already been paid for and that Jesus is offering them full pardon, the weight lifts. That is the power of grace properly understood.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Romans 5:8 — But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Romans 8:1 — There is therefore now no condemnation to those who are in Christ Jesus, who do not walk according to the flesh, but according to the Spirit (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Isaiah 1:18 — "Come now, and let us reason together," says the LORD, "Though your sins are like scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they are red like crimson, they shall be as wool" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, identify one person you know who seems burdened by guilt or shame. Write Romans 5:8 on a note and leave it where they might find it, or send it to them in a message with no other explanation. Then pray that God would use that verse to lift their eyes to Christ.

### SUMMARY

- The guilty person does not need more law; they need the promise of grace.
- Salvation is full pardon for sin, accomplished by Christ and received through repentance and faith.
- No one is too sinful to be saved because Christ died for sinners, not for the righteous.

## Lesson 4 — The Roman Road

### HOOK

Every traveler needs a map. If you are driving to a city you have never visited, you do not just guess the way — you follow a route that someone else has already proven works. Sharing the gospel is no different. There is a pathway through the book of Romans that has guided countless travelers from lost to found. Once you know it, you will never be lost for words again.

### CORE TEACHING

The Roman Road is a sequence of verses from the book of Romans that presents the gospel in a clear, logical order. It is not the only way to share the gospel, but it is one of the most effective because it answers the essential questions every person must face: What is my problem? What is the penalty? What is the solution? How do I respond? It begins with Romans 3:23: "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." This establishes the universal problem — every person, without exception, is a sinner. No one is exempt. The second stop is Romans 6:23a: "For the wages of sin is death." This explains the penalty. Sin is not a minor infraction; it carries a death sentence. But that same verse contains the turn: "but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." The third stop is Romans 5:8, which we looked at in the previous lesson — while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. This establishes that God did not wait for us to get better. He acted while we were at our worst. The fourth stop is Romans 10:9-10: "If you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved." This answers the question of response. It is not about good works or religious rituals; it is about confession and belief. The final stop is Romans 10:13: "For whoever calls on the name of the LORD shall be saved." This is the broad invitation — whoever. Not whoever is good enough, whoever has cleaned up their life, whoever belongs to the right church. Whoever calls on the Lord. When you lay these verses end to end, you have the entire gospel in a handful of Scripture passages. You do not need a outline or notes. You need these verses in your heart and the courage to speak them. The cross is the center of every stop on this road. The resurrection is the power behind every promise. This road does not end at a religion. It ends at a person — Jesus Christ.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Romans 3:23 — For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Romans 6:23 — For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Romans 10:9-10 — That if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved. For with the heart one believes unto righteousness, and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Romans 10:13 — For whoever calls on the name of the LORD shall be saved (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, write the five Roman Road verses on separate cards. Practice walking through them aloud in order three times each day — not just reading them but explaining each one in your own words after you say it. By the end of the week, find one person to walk through the entire sequence with.

### SUMMARY

- The Roman Road presents the gospel in five clear steps: sin, penalty, substitute, response, and salvation.
- Each verse answers a specific question a person needs resolved before they can trust Christ.
- Memorizing this sequence gives you a complete gospel presentation you can use anywhere, anytime.

## Lesson 5 — Answering "I Don't Believe the Bible"

### HOOK

Sooner or later, someone will say it to you: "The Bible is just a book written by men. It has been changed so many times. You cannot trust it." If you have no answer, the conversation stops right there. But if you know what the Bible says about itself, you can turn that objection into the very doorway through which the gospel enters.

### CORE TEACHING

When someone says they do not believe the Bible, it is tempting to jump into a debate about manuscript evidence, translation accuracy, or archaeological findings. Those things have their place, but the most powerful response is not a lecture — it is a verse. The apostle Peter gave us the answer nearly two thousand years before anyone raised these objections. In 2 Peter 1:16, he wrote: "For we did not follow cunningly devised fables when we made known to you the power and coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, but were eyewitnesses of His majesty." The Bible is not a collection of myths passed down through generations like a game of telephone. It is a reliable collection of historical documents written by eyewitnesses during the lifetime of other eyewitnesses. When Paul wrote that Christ appeared to over five hundred people at once, most of whom were still alive when he wrote it (1 Corinthians 15:6), he was making a claim that could be fact-checked. Anyone who doubted could go ask those witnesses. You can share this answer gently and briefly, then redirect to the conscience. Often, the real reason a person rejects the Bible is not intellectual — it is moral. They do not want to submit to what it says. Jesus Himself said in John 3:19: "And this is the condemnation, that the light has come into the world, and men loved darkness rather than light, because their deeds were evil." The solution is not to argue them into believing the Bible is true. The solution is to let the law expose their sin, so that they see why they need the Bible's message. Once a person realizes they are guilty before God, the question of whether the Bible is true becomes suddenly urgent. They are no longer debating a book. They are running from a Judge. And the gospel tells them there is a way to be saved.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

2 Peter 1:16 — For we did not follow cunningly devised fables when we made known to you the power and coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, but were eyewitnesses of His majesty (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

1 Corinthians 15:3-6 — For I delivered to you first of all that which I also received: that Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, and that He was buried, and that He rose again the third day according to the Scriptures, and that He was seen by Cephas, then by the twelve. After that He was seen by over five hundred brethren at once, of whom the greater part remain to the present, but some have fallen asleep (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, memorize 2 Peter 1:16 word-perfect. Then practice explaining in one minute why the Bible is trustworthy — using the categories "eyewitnesses," "historical documents," and "fulfilled prophecy." Say it out loud to a mirror until it feels natural.

### SUMMARY

- The Bible presents itself as eyewitness testimony, not mythology, and its claims can be examined.
- Many who reject the Bible do so for moral reasons, not intellectual ones — they love darkness more than light.
- A brief, honest answer about the Bible's reliability, followed by a turn to the law, is more effective than a long debate.

## Lesson 6 — Letting Scripture Do the Heavy Lifting

### HOOK

There is a story of a man carrying a heavy sack on his back who was offered a horse to ride. He accepted the horse but kept the sack on his shoulders. When asked why, he said, "The horse is carrying me; I did not want to burden the horse with the sack too." That is how many believers share the gospel — carrying the weight of persuasion on their own shoulders while the Word of God stands ready to do the work.

### CORE TEACHING

One of the most important lessons in evangelism is knowing when to stop talking. Many believers feel that if they just explain the gospel a little more clearly, add one more argument, or find the perfect illustration, the person will finally believe. But the power to save does not reside in your explanation; it resides in the Word. The apostle Paul said it plainly: "I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ, for it is the power of God to salvation for everyone who believes" (Romans 1:16). The gospel itself is the power — not your presentation of it. This realization is freeing. It means you do not have to be eloquent, persuasive, or quick-witted. You simply have to be faithful. You bring the Word into the conversation and trust the Spirit to apply it. The prophet Isaiah wrote that God's Word will not return void; it will accomplish what God purposes (Isaiah 55:11). The success of the Word does not depend on the skill of the one who speaks it. Letting Scripture do the heavy lifting means learning to answer a question with a verse rather than a lecture. When someone asks, "Why should I follow God?" you do not need a five-point argument. You can say, "The Bible says He created you, and you belong to Him." When they ask, "Why is there suffering?" you can say, "The Bible says we live in a fallen world that is in rebellion against God, and suffering is the result." Short, Scripture-saturated answers carry more weight than long, clever explanations. They also leave room for the Holy Spirit to work. When you have said everything you can say, stop. Do not fill the silence. Let the Word settle. The seed has been planted. Your job is not to dig it up and check if it has grown. Your job is to water it with prayer and trust the Gardener.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Romans 1:16 — For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ, for it is the power of God to salvation for everyone who believes, for the Jew first and also for the Greek (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Isaiah 55:11 — So shall My word be that goes forth from My mouth; it shall not return to Me void, but it shall accomplish what I please, and it shall prosper in the thing for which I sent it (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

1 Corinthians 2:4-5 — And my speech and my preaching were not with persuasive words of human wisdom, but in demonstration of the Spirit and of power, that your faith should not be in the wisdom of men but in the power of God (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, identify the three shortest, most powerful verses you know (such as Romans 6:23, John 3:16, or Romans 10:13). Practice answering any spiritual question with one of those three verses instead of a long explanation. Notice how often one verse does more than a dozen of your own sentences.

### SUMMARY

- The gospel itself is the power of God for salvation, not your presentation of it.
- Short, Scripture-saturated answers leave room for the Holy Spirit to work on the conscience.
- After you have spoken the Word, trust God with the outcome and do not try to manufacture results.

## Lesson 7 — Handling Common Objections with Scripture

### HOOK

You are in a conversation that is going well. The person is listening. They seem interested. Then suddenly they throw out a wall: "But what about all the suffering in the world?" or "Christianity is just one religion among many." That wall can stop you cold if you have no answer. But with a few verses ready, you can turn that wall into a doorway.

### CORE TEACHING

Every believer who shares their faith regularly will encounter the same objections again and again. The good news is that the Bible has short, satisfying answers for the most common ones. You do not need a full apologetics course. You need to know what Scripture says and trust the Spirit to use it. When someone says, "There is no evidence for God," you can point to Romans 1:20: "For since the creation of the world His invisible attributes are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made, even His eternal power and Godhead, so that they are without excuse." A building needs a builder, a creation needs a Creator. The evidence is all around them — their real problem is not lack of evidence but love for sin. When someone says, "A loving God would not send people to hell," you can gently explain that God does not send people to hell any more than a judge sends a criminal to prison. Their sins send them there. The judge merely carries out justice. God is both loving and just, and the cross is where both attributes meet — His justice against sin and His love for the sinner. When someone says, "Christianity is just one of many religions," you can point to John 14:6 where Jesus said, "I am the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through Me." Christianity is exclusive not because Christians are narrow-minded but because Jesus made an exclusive claim. Only He died for sins. Only He rose from the dead. No other religion offers a Savior who actually saves. When someone says, "The church is full of hypocrites," you can agree that hypocrites exist in the church but remind them that a hypocrite is not a true Christian — he is a pretender. Jesus Himself said the wheat and tares would grow together until the harvest. The presence of hypocrites does not invalidate the truth of the gospel. In every case, after giving a brief answer, turn the conversation back to the person's own standing before God. The goal is not to win an intellectual debate but to bring them to the cross. The resurrection is the proof that Jesus is who He claimed to be. Keep the conversation moving toward that center.

### KEY SCRIPTURES

Romans 1:20 — For since the creation of the world His invisible attributes are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made, even His eternal power and Godhead, so that they are without excuse (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

John 14:6 — Jesus said to him, "I am the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through Me" (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

Matthew 13:24-30 — The parable of the wheat and the tares (NKJV) [VERIFY AGAINST NKJV]

### PRACTICAL APPLICATION

This week, write down the three objections you are most likely to hear from the people you regularly speak with. Next to each objection, write one verse that addresses it and a single-sentence response using that verse. Practice saying each response aloud until you can deliver it naturally in under thirty seconds.

### SUMMARY

- Most common objections have concise biblical answers that do not require extensive theological training.
- After answering an objection briefly, always turn the conversation back to the person's standing before God.
- The cross and resurrection remain the center of every conversation; objections are side roads, not the destination.
