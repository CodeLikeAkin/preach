# God With Us | Voddie Baucham

**Source:** Founders Ministries — https://founders.org/sermons/god-with-us-voddie-baucham/ (page behind Cloudflare, partial transcript from messageofgod.org)
**Date:** January 22, 2021 — Founders "The Only God" national conference, Southwest Florida
**Scripture:** Galatians 4:1-7

---

## Summary

Voddie Baucham preaches on "God With Us: The Incarnate God, Jesus Christ, the God-Man." He emphasizes that this is one of the most critical yet often overlooked aspects of Christian theology. People are comfortable with Jesus as long as we aren't too clear about who He truly is. Muslims, Mormons, Hindus, and Buddhists can all accept Jesus as a good man, teacher, or prophet. But once we declare that Jesus is more than that — that He is God incarnate, God with us — then things change. Those who were willing to gather before might start to scatter.

## Key Scripture: Galatians 4:1-7

> "But when the fullness of time had come, God sent forth His Son, born of a woman, born under the law, to redeem those who were under the law, so that we might receive adoption as sons. And because you are sons, God has sent the Spirit of His Son into our hearts, crying, 'Abba, Father.' So you are no longer a slave, but a son; and if a son, then an heir through God."

## Key Points

- **The incarnation is central**: Jesus is not merely a prophet or teacher — He is God incarnate, the God-Man
- **The world tolerates a diminished Jesus**: Many are happy to celebrate Christmas as long as the true meaning of Christ's divinity is overlooked
- **The fullness of time**: Jesus came at exactly the right moment in God's redemptive plan to redeem those under the law and secure adoption as sons
- **The Trinity in redemption**: The Father sends the Son, born of a woman, born under the law; the Spirit of the Son is sent into our hearts crying "Abba, Father"
- **Our status changes**: No longer slaves but sons and heirs through God

## Illustration

Baucham references the Guinness World Record for the most expensive Christmas tree — displayed in Abu Dhabi, UAE (a Muslim country where converting to Christianity is illegal), valued at $11,026,900, decorated with Rolex watches, diamond rings, and 181 pieces of jewelry. This illustrates how the world will celebrate Christmas while rejecting the Christ it represents.
