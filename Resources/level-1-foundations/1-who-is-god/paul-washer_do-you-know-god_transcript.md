# Paul Washer — Do You Know God? (Full Sermon Transcript)

Source: https://singjupost.com/paul-washer-sermon-do-you-know-god-transcript/

Based on Romans 12:1-2

*'Therefore I urge you, brethren, by the mercies of God, to present your bodies a living and holy sacrifice, acceptable to God, which is your spiritual service of worship. And do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind, so that you may prove what the will of God is, that which is good and acceptable and perfect.'*

---

## Summary

Paul Washer exhorts believers to present their bodies as living sacrifices to God, not through mere duty or discipline, but by growing in the knowledge of God's mercies revealed in Christ. The more we comprehend who God is and what He has done for us in the Gospel, the more our affections are drawn toward Him and the more empowered we are to live holy lives. True transformation comes from beholding God's glory, not from moralism, programs, or gimmicks.

## Key Themes

- **Motivation for sacrifice**: The mercies of God (Romans 1-11) are the foundation for presenting our bodies as living sacrifices
- **Knowing God drives devotion**: The more we see of God's beauty, glory, and excellencies, the more our affections are drawn out toward Him
- **Holiness is seeing God's value**: God is holy — in a category all to Himself, of infinite worth. To be holy is to recognize His value and live for Him as the highest reason
- **Transformation through the Word**: Not conformity to the world, but metamorphosis through renewing the mind by Scripture
- **The priority of prayer**: Corporate and personal prayer are essential for spiritual life
- **Discipline for godliness**: We must cut out worldly influences and cultivate the mind of Christ
- **The preacher's job is to exegete God**: To reveal the beauty, glory, power, and excellency of God so people are captivated by Him

## Transcript

### Opening: The Call to Living Sacrifice

Paul urges us by the mercies of God to present our bodies as a living and holy sacrifice. Satan is a liar, but there is one thing he says in Job that rings true: you can take away a lot from a man — his house, his car, his bank accounts — but it will not do him as much damage as touching his body. When you ask a man to give up his very life, you are asking for the most precious thing he possesses, something that cannot be replaced once spent.

Paul is calling us to give ourselves entirely to Christ. He is not making a suggestion or putting forth an idea. He is saying: this is our new reality. If you are Christian, it is nonsensical to hold back. The most precious thing you have, you give to Him.

### Where Do We Gain the Motivation?

Most of us agree we should give our lives as a living sacrifice. The problem is: where do we find the motivation, the impulse, the strength, the passion that endures?

Paul tells us: **"By the mercies of God."**

The more you understand and comprehend the mercies of God toward you as revealed in the cross and the Gospel work of Jesus Christ, the more empowered you will be to grow in devotion and laying down your life for Him.

The word "therefore" in Romans 12:1 points back to everything Paul has shared in the first eleven chapters of Romans. In Ephesians, the same pattern appears — three chapters of deep theology about all God has done for us in Christ, then "therefore, live this way."

The more I comprehend the mercy and grace of God in Jesus Christ — past mercies, present mercies, and future grace throughout all eternity — the more I am empowered to live my life.

### How to Love God More

Do you need to love God more? Yes. But how?

Paul Washer illustrates with his marriage of 23 years: he loves his wife more now not because he tries harder, but because he sees more of her virtue. Her virtue draws out his affections. You do not make yourself love someone more; the virtue in that person draws love out of you.

The same is true in Christianity. The more you see of the virtues of God — if you are regenerate, if your heart has been renewed — the more that will draw out your affections and motivate you to follow Him.

### The Neglect of Studying God's Attributes

Washer asks a pointed question: "How many years have you dedicated to studying the attributes of God?" Many have expertise in Greek, Hebrew, and systematic theology, but their study of who God is amounts to nearly zero.

This is one reason why our love for God is so little in the church. What does it matter if you learn all the rules and there is no power to obey them? Where is the power, the strength, the zeal? They come from knowing God.

The preacher's job is not to give moralisms or quaint stories. His job is to **exegete God** — to reveal God, to explain God, to let people know the beauty and glory and power and excellency of God, so they walk out excited not just about what they're supposed to do, but about who He is.

### The Beauty of God

The beauty of God is so great that if you were to catch one glimpse without being supernaturally strengthened, it would melt your mind to nothing. The delight would overwhelm your heart and burst it. These are the types of things that drive men and women into the mission field and make martyrs sing — because they know their God.

### Present Your Bodies

Paul says "present your bodies." Why this language? Because if Jesus has your heart, He will have your ears, your eyes, your tongue, your hands, your feet. Nobody cares about your heart — the issue is what you do, because that is what truly manifests the contents of your heart.

Offer your bodies as a **living** sacrifice — zealous, passionate, on fire for God. True life comes from God, from the Spirit of God, from the Word of God, not from programs or gimmicks.

### A Holy Sacrifice

The word "holy" means cut, separate, distinct. God is in a category all to Himself. There is no one like God. He is unique, of infinite value, infinitely above all other value combined.

When you start seeing God that way, everything changes. God does everything for His own glory — because He is the most worthy. If God chooses the highest reason to do what He does, it is Himself. Who is more worthy of love than God?

To be holy means to recognize God's value as God recognizes His value, and to live your life for Him as your highest reason.

### Turning to God from Idols

1 Thessalonians 1:9 — "You turned to God from idols." The order is significant. They did not grow tired of their idols; rather, Paul proclaimed a God who so excelled their idols that they were captivated and torn away from what they had seen. This is to continue throughout our entire Christian life — growing in the grace and knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.

### Do Not Be Conformed to This World

Do not be pressed into the world's mold. You are what you eat spiritually. You are bombarded 16 hours a day with the filth of this world, yet you have no plan of reading Scripture, of prayer, of fellowship with the saints.

Cut things out of your life that influence you toward worldliness. Start adding things that influence you toward godliness: the Word of God, reading it, studying it, memorizing it, meditating upon it, obeying it, sharing it.

### Be Transformed

"Be transformed" — metamorphosized, the same word used of Christ in the Transfiguration. It is supernatural. The Word of God is powerful, and the Spirit of God works through the Word of God. If you want to be transformed, stay in the Word, looking for greater and greater visions of God in Christ.

**Cultivate the mind of Christ** by being in Christ's Word.
