SCRIPTURE: Jude 1 — To them that are sanctified by God the Father, and preserved in Jesus Christ, and called.

A SERMON DELIVERED ON LORD'S-DAY MORNING, FEBRUARY 9TH, 1862, BY C. H. SPURGEON, AT THE METROPOLITAN TABERNACLE, NEWINGTON.

— Threefold Sanctification —

"To them that are sanctified by God the Father, and preserved in Jesus Christ, and called."—Jude 1.

I. THE SOURCE OF SANCTIFICATION is the Father. "Sanctified by God the Father." The Father, as the fountain of all grace, is the origin of that sanctification which his people receive. He chose us unto holiness, and he gives us in Christ Jesus the power by which we become holy.

II. THE STREAMS OF SANCTIFICATION come through Jesus Christ. "Preserved in Jesus Christ." We are not only sanctified by the Father's decree and by the Spirit's work, but we are preserved, kept, maintained in a state of grace and holiness, through our union with the Lord Jesus Christ.

III. THE APPLICATION OF SANCTIFICATION is by the Holy Spirit, who is here meant by the word "called." The calling is the effectual calling of the Holy Ghost, by which we are brought out of darkness into marvelous light, and made partakers of the holiness of God.

Thus we have the three Persons of the blessed Trinity all co-operating in the great work of sanctification. The Father purposed it, the Son secured it, and the Spirit applies it. This is a beautiful illustration of the unity of the three divine Persons in the work of grace. Let us adore the triune God, who thus condescends to dwell with men, and to make us holy.

Let me now proceed to draw some practical inferences from this subject.

1. First, then, let us LEARN THE DIGNITY OF THE WORK OF SANCTIFICATION. It has the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost engaged in it. It is no mean thing to be made holy. It is a work worthy of God himself.

2. Secondly, let us LEARN THE CERTAINTY OF THE BELIEVER'S SANCTIFICATION. If the Father sanctifies, if the Son preserves, if the Spirit calls, then surely we shall be holy. The work is begun, and it shall be completed.

3. Thirdly, let us LEARN THE IMPORTANCE OF HOLINESS. If the triune God is engaged in producing it, it must be of the utmost importance.

4. Fourthly, let us LEARN THE TRINITY OF THE GODHEAD. We have here distinct mention of the Father, of Jesus Christ, and of the Holy Spirit, all engaged in the same work. They are three, and yet they are one. The Father sanctifies, the Son preserves, and the Spirit calls. Yet they are not three gods, but one God. This is a mystery, but it is a revealed truth, and we believe it.

Beloved, let us give diligence to make our calling and election sure. Let us seek to be sanctified wholly, in spirit, soul, and body. Let us look to the Father as the source of our holiness, to the Son as the preserver of our holiness, and to the Holy Spirit as the effectual caller who brings us into the actual possession of holiness. And so may we be kept unto the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, to whom be glory forever and ever. Amen.
