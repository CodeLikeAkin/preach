TITLE: The Great Supreme — A Sermon on the Trinity
FROM: Spurgeon's Sermons, Volume 7

— The Great Supreme —

"And this is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent."—John 17:3.

I shall not pretend this morning to dive into the depths of this verse, but simply use it as a text from which to speak of the knowledge of God as the great essential of eternal life.

I. First, THE KNOWLEDGE OF GOD IS ETERNAL LIFE.

1. It is the cause of life. Where there is no knowledge of God, the soul is dead. The first spark of life is when God is known in some measure.

2. It is the sustenance of life. As a man lives by food, so the soul lives by knowing God. The more we know him, the stronger our spiritual life becomes.

3. It is the perfection of life. Heaven itself consists in knowing God fully. "Now we see through a glass darkly; but then face to face: now I know in part; but then shall I know even as also I am known."

II. Secondly, THIS KNOWLEDGE IS OF THE ONLY TRUE GOD.

1. It is the knowledge of the Father, as the only true God. Not that the Son and the Spirit are not true God; but the Father is the fountain of Deity, and is eminently called "the only true God," to distinguish him from the false gods of the heathen.

2. It is the knowledge of the Son, as Jesus Christ whom he has sent. For we cannot know the Father truly without knowing the Son. "No man knoweth the Son but the Father; neither knoweth any man the Father, save the Son, and he to whomsoever the Son will reveal him."

3. It is the knowledge of the Holy Spirit, for he it is who reveals both the Father and the Son to us. "No man can say that Jesus is the Lord, but by the Holy Ghost."

Thus the knowledge of the Triune God is eternal life. To know the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, in their unity and in their distinct personalities, this is life indeed.

III. Thirdly, LET US CONSIDER THE BLESSEDNESS OF THIS KNOWLEDGE.

1. It brings peace. To know God is to be at peace with him through Jesus Christ.

2. It brings joy. "Rejoice in the Lord always: and again I say, Rejoice."

3. It brings hope. "We know that when he shall appear, we shall be like him; for we shall see him as he is."

4. It brings holiness. For the knowledge of God purifies the heart.

5. It brings immortality. For this is eternal life, to know the only true God.

IV. Fourthly, THIS KNOWLEDGE IS OBTAINED ONLY THROUGH JESUS CHRIST.

"No man cometh unto the Father but by me." Christ is the only teacher, the only mediator, the only way. Through him we know the Father, and through him we receive the Spirit.

1. He reveals the Father. "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father."

2. He sends the Spirit. "He shall glorify me: for he shall receive of mine, and shall show it unto you."

3. He gives eternal life. "My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me: and I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish."

Let us then prize this knowledge above all other knowledge. Let us seek to grow in grace and in the knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. For this is the great supreme purpose of our existence—that we might know God, and enjoy him forever.

And in this knowledge we find the Trinity fully displayed. The Father is the fountain of all good. The Son is the channel through which it flows. The Holy Spirit is the power by which it is applied to our souls. And these three are one. Let us worship them, and give them all the glory, world without end. Amen.
