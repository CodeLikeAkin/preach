A SERMON INTENDED FOR READING ON LORD'S DAY, JANUARY 21, 1855, DELIVERED BY C. H. SPURGEON, AT NEW PARK STREET CHAPEL, SOUTHWARK.

— The Personality of the Holy Ghost —

"And I will pray the Father, and he shall give you another Comforter, that he may abide with you for ever; even the Spirit of truth; whom the world cannot receive, because it seeth him not, neither knoweth him: but ye know him; for he dwelleth with you, and shall be in you."—John xiv. 16, 17.

I BELIEVE in the Holy Ghost"—this is an article of the creed of the Christian church. It is a very clear and prominent doctrine of the New Testament, that there is a Being, the Holy Ghost, who is the author of all spiritual life in the souls of men. But we must not rest satisfied with a mere doctrinal belief in the existence and agency of the Holy Spirit. We should seek to know him, to have him dwelling in us, to be acquainted with him, and to be led by his power. I do not think we shall have much of the blessing of the Holy Spirit, unless we practically understand and realize his personality. Let me just remark that too many of us think of the Holy Spirit as if he were merely an emanation, something like a vapor, or a gas, without any existence of his own. Many divines speak of the "sacred influence" and of the "blessed influence" of the Spirit; and we are very apt to think of the influence, but we forget the Being from whom it comes. The Unitarians speak of the Holy Spirit as if it were only an attribute, or the energy of God. But when we are Scriptural, we speak of the Holy Ghost as a person. We speak of him as existing, as living, as acting, as having a personal subsistence. I wish this morning to bear my testimony to this great truth; and I pray God that we may be enabled, during the service, to realize the presence and person of the Holy Spirit.

I. In endeavoring to set before you THE PERSONALITY OF THE HOLY SPIRIT, we shall begin by noticing his PERSONAL DESIGNATIONS.

The Holy Ghost is spoken with in all the expressions which imply personality. He is called by the name of "Spirit," and "Holy Ghost," many times in Scripture. But we find him endowed with the personal pronouns. Jesus Christ says, "He shall teach you all things." This implies a person. "When the Spirit of truth is come, he will guide you into all truth: for he shall not speak of himself; but whatsoever he shall hear, that shall he speak; and he will show you things to come" (John 16:13). So again we read, "I will pray the Father, and he shall give you another Comforter, that he may abide with you for ever" (John 14:16). "But when the Comforter is come, whom I will send unto you from the Father, even the Spirit of truth, which proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me" (John 15:26). The term "Comforter" signifies a person. Now we could not speak of a sacred influence, or an emanation, as a comforter. It is a person that cometh to us; and the Holy Spirit is represented as coming to us, acting upon us, and abiding in us, as a person.

II. We will next notice his PERSONAL ATTRIBUTES.

1. He has a mind. "He that searcheth the hearts knoweth what is the mind of the Spirit" (Rom. 8:27). He has a will. "But all these worketh that one and the selfsame Spirit, dividing to every man severally as he will" (1 Cor. 12:11). He has understanding. "The Spirit searcheth all things, yea, the deep things of God" (1 Cor. 2:10). He has the power of a self-conscious and self-acting agent. He "worketh" all things. He can be grieved: "And grieve not the holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption" (Eph. 4:30). He can be vexed: "They rebelled, and vexed his holy Spirit" (Isa. 63:10). He can be blasphemed against: "The blasphemy against the Holy Ghost shall not be forgiven unto men" (Matt. 12:31). All these are personal attributes. You cannot slander an attribute, vex an influence, or grieve an emanation. These things can only be done to a person.

2. He has personal offices. He acts as a teacher. "But the Comforter, which is the Holy Ghost, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things" (John 14:26). He acts as a guide: "Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth" (John 16:13). He commands: "The Holy Ghost said, Separate me Barnabas and Saul for the work whereunto I have called them" (Acts 13:2). He directs: "They were forbidden of the Holy Ghost to preach the word in Asia" (Acts 16:6). He creates: "By his Spirit he hath garnished the heavens" (Job 26:13). He quickens: "It is the Spirit that quickeneth" (John 6:63). He comforts: "Then had the churches rest throughout all Judea and Galilee and Samaria, and were edified; and walking in the fear of the Lord, and in the comfort of the Holy Ghost, were multiplied" (Acts 9:31). He sanctifies: "Being sanctified by the Holy Ghost" (Rom. 15:16). He bears witness: "The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God" (Rom. 8:16). He helps our infirmities: "The Spirit also helpeth our infirmities" (Rom. 8:26). He makes intercession: "The Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered" (Rom. 8:26).

III. Now let me speak of his PERSONAL RELATIONS.

1. He sustains a relation to the Father and the Son. He is the Spirit of God, and the Spirit of Christ. He proceedeth from the Father, and is sent by the Son. He is one with them, and equal to them. He is not a servant, but a sovereign.

2. He sustains a relation to the world. He strives with men: "My spirit shall not always strive with man" (Gen. 6:3). He convinces of sin, of righteousness, and of judgment (John 16:8-11).

3. He sustains a relation to the church. He dwells in it: "Know ye not that ye are the temple of God, and that the Spirit of God dwelleth in you?" (1 Cor. 3:16). He gives gifts unto men: "Now there are diversities of gifts, but the same Spirit" (1 Cor. 12:4). He ordains ministers: "Take heed therefore unto yourselves, and to all the flock, over the which the Holy Ghost hath made you overseers" (Acts 20:28).

4. He sustains a relation to individual believers. He dwells in them: "What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you" (1 Cor. 6:19). He leads them: "For as many as are led by the Spirit of God, they are the sons of God" (Rom. 8:14). He witnesses with them: "The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit" (Rom. 8:16). He seals them: "After that ye believed, ye were sealed with that holy Spirit of promise" (Eph. 1:13).

IV. Now, consider THE PRACTICAL DUTIES WHICH ARISE FROM THIS TRUTH.

If the Holy Spirit be a person, we should—

1. Reverence him. We must not trifle with him. We must not blaspheme him.

2. We should love him. He is the great Author of our spiritual life. He has quickened us, and comforted us, and taught us. He deserves our love.

3. We should seek his fellowship. We should pray for his presence, and cultivate his company.

4. We should obey him. He says, "Separate me Barnabas and Saul." He speaks, and we should obey.

5. We should be careful not to grieve him. "Grieve not the holy Spirit of God."

6. We should honor him in our baptism. We are baptized in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost.

7. We should invoke his aid in prayer. We should pray in the Holy Ghost.

8. We should expect his blessing in the means of grace.

V. Let me now speak of THE BLESSED EFFECTS THAT FOLLOW FROM A REALIZATION OF THIS DOCTRINE.

1. It gives us a sense of the nearness of God. The Holy Spirit is not a remote influence, but a present person. He is with us, and in us. 

2. It makes us careful of our bodies. They are temples of the Holy Ghost.

3. It gives us strength. He is the Spirit of power.

4. It gives us comfort. He is the Comforter.

5. It gives us guidance. He leads us into all truth.

6. It gives us assurance. He witnesses with our spirit.

Beloved, let us always remember that the Holy Spirit is not a mere influence, but a divine Person. Let us treat him as such. Let us adore him, love him, trust him, obey him, and honor him. Let us pray for a fresh baptism of his power. May the Lord pour out his Spirit upon us all, for Jesus' sake. Amen.
