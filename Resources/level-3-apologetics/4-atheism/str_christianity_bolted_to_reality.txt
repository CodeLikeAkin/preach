Christianity: Bolted to Reality
Source: https://www.str.org/w/christianity-bolted-to-reality
Author: Greg Koukl
Published: 02/21/2013

SUMMARY:
Greg Koukl argues that Christianity is not a "house of cards" that can be easily dismissed, but rather a worldview system "bolted down to reality."

KEY POINTS:
1. The "New Atheists" (Dawkins, Harris, Dennett) attack Christianity aggressively but their arguments are shallow - they attack what professing Christians do rather than Biblical Christianity itself.

2. Christianity is durable because it's a complex worldview that explains reality, not an emotional wish.

3. Cosmological argument: The universe had a beginning (2nd Law of Thermodynamics). Something non-physical must have caused it. Agent causation (we initiate actions ourselves) provides a reasonable model.

4. Design argument: The world appears designed. Design-by-design is as reasonable as design-by-blind-watchmaker (Darwinian evolution).

5. Christianity's foundational principles resonate with our deepest intuitions about justice, mercy, and the nature of reality.

6. Christianity provides adequate explanatory power for origins, morality, and meaning - unlike atheism or Islam.
