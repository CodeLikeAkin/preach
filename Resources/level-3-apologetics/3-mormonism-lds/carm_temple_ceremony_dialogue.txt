This article is a dialogue between Matt Slick (CARM founder) and a Mormon named Larry
in a chat room. Key discussion points:

- Larry initially didn't believe Matt was the real Matt Slick
- Slick argues that Mormonism teaches many gods (Mormon Doctrine, p. 163), a mother
  goddess (Articles of Faith, p. 443), God was once a man (Mormon Doctrine, p. 321),
  humans can become gods (Teachings of Joseph Smith, pp. 345-347, 354), God resides
  near the star Kolob (Pearl of Great Price, pp. 34-35)
- Slick challenges the LDS temple practice of wearing fig leaf aprons, arguing God
  rejected Adam and Eve's fig leaves (Gen. 3:7) and replaced them with animal skins
  requiring bloodshed (Gen. 3:21) — symbolizing that human works are rejected and only
  Christ's sacrifice covers sin
- Larry argues the aprons represent "knowledge of good and evil" and that God "gave
  them something even better"
- Slick contends that 2 Nephi 25:23 ("saved by grace after all you can do") adds works
  to salvation, whereas Christianity teaches justification by faith alone (Rom. 5:1)
