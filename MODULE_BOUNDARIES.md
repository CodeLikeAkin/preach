# PREACH — Module Boundaries Reference

## PURPOSE
Before drafting any lesson, read this file to know exactly what 
belongs in each module. Never include content that belongs to 
another module. If a topic is listed under a different module, 
reference it briefly and move on — do not teach it.

---

## LEVEL 1 — FOUNDATIONS

### Module 01 — Who Is God?
COVERS: The existence of God, attributes of God (holiness, 
sovereignty, justice, love, immutability, omniscience, 
omnipotence), knowing God relationally vs informationally, 
living in response to who God is.
DOES NOT COVER: The Trinity (Module 02), Creation (Module 03), 
the nature of man (Module 04), the person of Christ (Module 05), 
the cross mechanics (Module 06), Scripture's authority (Module 07), 
salvation doctrine (Module 08), the Holy Spirit's person 
(Module 09), eternity (Module 10).

### Module 02 — The Trinity
COVERS: One God in three persons, the Father, Son and Holy Spirit 
as distinct persons of the same essence, the Trinity in creation 
and redemption, practical implications of a Triune God.
DOES NOT COVER: God's attributes in general (Module 01), the 
incarnation in depth (Module 05), the work of the cross 
(Module 06), the gifts of the Spirit (Module 09).

### Module 03 — Creation
COVERS: God as Creator, creation ex nihilo, the days of creation, 
faith and science, the purpose of creation, humanity as the crown 
of creation, the goodness of the created world.
DOES NOT COVER: The fall of man (Module 04), redemption 
(Module 06), new creation in salvation (Module 08).

### Module 04 — Who Is Man?
COVERS: The image of God in man (imago Dei), the fall and original 
sin, total depravity, the nature of the human spirit, why man 
needs redemption, the consequence of sin.
DOES NOT COVER: God's attributes (Module 01), the solution to sin 
via the cross (Module 06), salvation and grace (Module 08).

### Module 05 — Who Is Jesus?
COVERS: The deity of Christ, the humanity of Christ, the 
hypostatic union, Jesus as fully God and fully man, biblical 
evidence for Christ's divinity, why Jesus had to be both God 
and man.
DOES NOT COVER: The mechanics of atonement (Module 06), 
salvation process (Module 08), the Holy Spirit as distinct 
person (Module 09).

### Module 06 — The Cross & Resurrection
COVERS: The meaning of the atonement, propitiation, 
substitutionary atonement, the resurrection as historical fact 
and theological necessity, what Christ's death and resurrection 
accomplished.
DOES NOT COVER: How to receive salvation (Module 08), the role 
of the Spirit in applying salvation (Module 09).

### Module 07 — The Authority of Scripture
COVERS: Biblical inerrancy, biblical inspiration, why the Bible 
can be trusted, the canon of Scripture, how Scripture functions 
as authority in the believer's life.
DOES NOT COVER: Specific doctrines taught in Scripture — those 
belong to their respective modules.

### Module 08 — Salvation & Grace
COVERS: Justification, repentance, faith, grace, regeneration, 
the new birth, assurance of salvation, what it means to be saved.
DOES NOT COVER: The cross mechanics (Module 06), sanctification 
in depth, the Spirit's gifts (Module 09).

### Module 09 — The Holy Spirit
COVERS: The person of the Holy Spirit, the Spirit's role in 
salvation, the baptism of the Holy Spirit, the gifts of the 
Spirit, being led by the Spirit, the Spirit's role in the 
believer's daily life.
DOES NOT COVER: Salvation doctrine (Module 08), evangelism 
methods (Level 2).

### Module 10 — Heaven, Hell & Eternity
COVERS: The reality of heaven and hell, the resurrection of the 
dead, final judgment, eternal life, living in light of eternity.
DOES NOT COVER: Salvation process (Module 08), apologetics for 
the resurrection (Level 3).

## LEVEL 2 — EVANGELISM

### Module 01 — What Is the Gospel?
COVERS: A complete definition of the gospel message, the 
essential elements that must be present, common reductions 
of the gospel and why they fail.
DOES NOT COVER: How to share it (Modules 04-07), apologetics 
for it (Level 3).

### Module 02 — Why Souls Matter
COVERS: The eternal value of every human soul, the reality of 
judgment, what is at stake in evangelism, developing a passion 
for the lost.
DOES NOT COVER: Methods of evangelism (Modules 04-07).

### Module 03 — The Role of the Holy Spirit in Evangelism
COVERS: Why evangelism is impossible without the Spirit, the 
Spirit's role in conviction, boldness in witness, being led 
by the Spirit in evangelistic encounters.
DOES NOT COVER: The person of the Spirit in depth (Level 1 
Module 09), specific evangelism methods (Modules 04-07).

### Module 04 — Starting the Conversation
COVERS: How to initiate gospel conversations naturally, 
transition questions, the WDJD method, making evangelism 
feel normal rather than awkward.
DOES NOT COVER: The full gospel presentation (Module 01), 
handling objections (Modules 09-11).

### Module 05 — Sharing Your Testimony
COVERS: How to structure and share a personal testimony, 
keeping it gospel-centred, using it as a bridge to the 
gospel, what to include and what to leave out.

### Module 06 — Understanding Who You're Talking To
COVERS: Different types of people you will encounter 
(nominal Christians, religious people, sceptics), 
true vs false conversion, reading your audience.

### Module 07 — Leading Someone to Christ
COVERS: Walking someone through repentance and faith, 
what to say at the moment of decision, why the sinner's 
prayer alone is insufficient, what genuine conversion 
looks like.

### Module 08 — What You're Asking Someone to Do
COVERS: The cost of discipleship, what you are actually 
asking someone to commit to when you share the gospel, 
why honesty about the cost matters.

### Module 09 — Overcoming Fear & Rejection
COVERS: The fear of man vs the fear of God, how to 
handle rejection, boldness in evangelism, practical 
strategies for pushing through fear.

### Module 10 — Evangelism in Everyday Life
COVERS: Making evangelism a lifestyle not an event, 
witnessing to family, friends, colleagues, strangers 
in ordinary moments.

### Module 11 — Using Scripture in Conversation
COVERS: How to use the Bible effectively in evangelistic 
conversations, key passages for evangelism, responding 
to "I don't believe the Bible."

### Module 12 — Praying for the Lost & Follow-Up
COVERS: Intercession for the unsaved, what to do after 
someone responds to the gospel, basic discipleship 
follow-up, connecting new believers to a church.

## LEVEL 3 — APOLOGETICS

### Module 01 — Islam
COVERS: Core Islamic claims, key points of contradiction 
with Christianity, the Islamic dilemma, responding to 
common Muslim objections to the gospel and the Bible.

### Module 02 — Jehovah's Witnesses
COVERS: JW core theology, the Watchtower's denial of 
Christ's deity, key scriptures JWs misuse and how to 
respond, engaging JWs at the door.

### Module 03 — Mormonism / LDS
COVERS: LDS core claims, the Mormon view of God and 
salvation vs biblical Christianity, key differences, 
how to engage respectfully and clearly.

### Module 04 — Atheism
COVERS: The problem of evil and suffering, arguments 
for God's existence, responding to the most common 
atheist objections, David Wood's testimony as a case 
study in intellectual conversion.

### Module 05 — Buddhism
COVERS: Buddhist worldview basics, key contradictions 
with Christianity, how to engage a Buddhist with the 
gospel, the problem of suffering in Buddhism vs 
Christianity.

---

## RULES FOR THE AI

1. Before drafting any lesson, read this entire file
2. Identify which module you are currently drafting
3. Check the COVERS list — only teach what is listed there
4. Check the DOES NOT COVER list — if a topic appears 
   there, mention it exists and move on, do not teach it
5. If a topic naturally comes up that belongs to another 
   module, use this exact phrase:
   "We will go deeper on this in [Module name]."
   Then return immediately to the current module's content
6. Never draft a full lesson on a topic that belongs to 
   another module
